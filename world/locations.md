# Major Locations

## The Allied Cities

The Allied Cities span a wide and diverse region unified by mutual history, shared culture, and open trade. Nearly two thousand years ago, these lands were shaped under the dominion of the Estara—ancient, powerful beings who oversaw the construction of cities, roads, and magical systems still in use today.

The Estara conscripted mortal populations into extra-planar wars, forcing them to fight in conflicts they neither understood nor survived whole. Over time, resentment boiled into open rebellion. The Estara were banished, and the cities claimed their freedom some 80 years ago. Their old works remain, but few speak of them with reverence.

Beyond the Allied Cities lie true wilds: uncharted lands that have never been settled, never ruled, and hold no trace of civilization. All ancient ruins known to mortals are here, within the bounds of the Allied Cities—the only place the Estara ever came.

### Four Major Cities

Four major cities form the core of the modern alliance: **Aris**, **Metgal**, **Hestar**, and **Alimar**. Together they convene the Citizen Conference, a council of leaders that meets in Aris when unity is needed.

#### Aris

The largest city and political center of the alliance. Aris sits at the center of the network, where roads from the north, west, and southeast meet. It is home of the Counsel of Citizens and hosts the Citizen Conference.

#### Metgal

A mountain-adjacent city known for its mining, fortifications, and resilient populace. Metgal was a major battleground during the Rebellion and bears the deepest scars. The city was devastated by nightmare magic and fire. Resentment toward the old order remains strong there, and memories run long. Metgal has the strongest connection to the other worlds of the Estaran Empire.

#### Hestar

A bustling trade hub west of the Axial Plains. Known for fertile farmland, vibrant markets, cultural exchanges, philosophical schools, and balanced governance.

#### Alimar

A coastal city in the far southwest renowned for its maritime traditions and strategic importance. Alimar was the birthplace of the Rebellion—"always the home of free spirits." It has strong sea connections to Vaenaris and often plays a stabilizing role in regional affairs.

### Regions

The lands between and beyond these cities are often grouped into broader regions:

- **Axial Plains** - The agricultural and cultural center of the alliance
- **Noe** - Far north region that remains damaged by the Rebellion, especially around Metgal
- **Vaenaris** - Region across the southern sea, farthest from Estaran power during their rule
- **Drakland** - Southern region
- **Gateflow River** - Major waterway
- **Mistywinde River** - Flows south from the Uplands through the Faedwood to Oechenfor
- **Blacktongue River** - Named after Kriv the Blacktongue

## The Faedwood

Ancient forest inhabited by Fey including faeries, pixies, sprites, and other woodland creatures. The forest has turned hostile to travelers from Oechenfor due to Dragonborn incursions.

### The Faepath

A road running north-south through the Faedwood, connecting Oechenfor to the Uplands. Traditionally safe passage, though recently dangerous.

### The Nest

A stone dome rising above the Faedwood along the Faepath. The summit features a 100' diameter flat campsite with a fire pit, space for tents, and protective boulders. Stone stairs carved on north and south sides allow the path to run directly through. Located one day's march from the Upland Stair and two days from the southern entrance.

## The Uplands

A tundra region north of the Faedwood characterized by thin air, cold winds, and serene beauty. Patches of snow remain even after winter. The region evokes "serene purity" in contrast to the Faedwood's "richness and wild intensity."

### Upland Stair

A narrow stepped switchback connecting the Faepath to the Uplands. Total ascent is approximately 250 feet. High natural rock walls block views going up or down. The stairs require 2 feet of movement for each 1 foot of ascent.

### The Teeth

A high ridge west of the Upland Stair where Erol and Lamat's manor sits.

### East Falls

A waterfall fed by a river running from the north. The manor of Erol and Lamat is positioned at the very edge of the cliff overlooking the Faedwood.

### Lome

A highland village in the Uplands not far from the top of the East Falls, where two mountain streams meet and continue southward. Founded just five years ago by Erol the Faed and Lamat Velu, who left the bustle of Oechenfor in search of quiet, purpose, and renewal. Built with intention and care, Lome remains small—fewer than 200 residents.

The town is led by Mayor Kruftus (a werebear), Midge Wittle (a resourceful goblin entrepreneur), and F'guza (a solemn orc monk who oversees the Orchard Path Monastery). The town is centered around shared labor, modest farming, and a deep respect for balance—spiritual, communal, and ecological. The monastery trains warrior-monks who support the village's safety.

Despite its short history, Lome has already known hardship. The village survived a siege by forces led by the dragonborn Gullux only to be threatened by dragon attack and the return of Milicent. To protect the people, Erol and Lamat led a full evacuation of Lome, guiding the citizens south to the safety of Oechenfor. **The village now stands empty.**

Notable features include the Orchard Path Monastery, The Wary Hippogriff inn, and a broken Estaran statue at the edge of town.

## East Hills

Mountain range east of the Uplands where the Dragonborn invaders established their hideout in cave systems at the base.

## Other Planes

### Shadowfell

A dark reflection of the Material Plane. Milicent maintains a fortress here where she raises an army.

### Feywild

A vibrant reflection of the Material Plane. Erol and Lamat are currently hiding in refuges here and in the Shadowfell.
