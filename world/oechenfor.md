# Oechenfor
**Frontier City on the Mistywinde River**

## Summary

Oechenfor is a rich and fast-growing frontier city on the Mistywinde River, just south of the Faedwood. Founded twenty years ago, it has become a magnet for traders, builders, spellwrights, and mercenaries looking to strike it big—or start over. It is far enough from the capitals to avoid their scrutiny, making it an ideal home for those with complicated pasts, unregistered talents, or ambitions better left unexamined.

The city's location at the edge of the wilds brings opportunity as well as danger, and few ask too many questions as long as the coin is good. Oechenfor is not lawless, but it is practical—order is maintained by necessity, not philosophy. Many come for gold, land, or reinvention, and stay for the stability that wealth and fear can buy.

Though nominally aligned with the Allied Cities, Oechenfor's priorities are local and its ties to the central powers are informal.

## Leadership

Oechenfor's most prominent resident is **Chief Citizen Aldrin Stoneward**, a legendary veteran of the Rebellion, who holds the respect of the people more for his accomplishments than for his ideals. His founding companions—four of whom still reside in the city—have left their mark as well, shaping Oechenfor into a place where talent, not title, earns your place.

## Trade and Agriculture

Oechenfor's prosperity rests not just on bold settlers and proximity to the wilds, but on its thriving agriculture and growing trade networks. The fertile land along the Mistywinde River has yielded strong harvests, and nearby villages feed a constant supply of grain, produce, and livestock into the city markets. Much of this success is thanks to the Van Morgaaren Trade Company, a privately funded enterprise that has built roads, warehouses, river docks, and an efficient guild-run market system.

The Company operates with a wide margin of freedom, cutting deals where others see risk, and maintaining the flow of goods between Oechenfor, Aris, and the Allied Cities beyond. While some whisper that the Van Morgaarens are more powerful than the Counselors themselves, few argue with results. If there's coin to be made on the edge of civilization, chances are the Company already has a stake in it.

## The Oechenfor Region

The lands north of Oechenfor are wild, ancient, and only partially tamed. The great forest known as the Faedwood stretches across much of the region, thick with magic, mist, and places where the world grows thin. Travelers follow the Faepath, a narrow, winding route through the trees, but even then, it's easy to lose your way—or lose time.

Midway through the forest stands the Nest, a stone dome that rises above the canopy like an eye. It's a common stop for messengers, merchants, and adventurers making the long journey north. Beyond it, the path climbs the Upland Stair, a steep natural ascent into the highlands above.

Towering over the northern forest is the wall of stone known as the Teeth, a massive cliff marking the boundary between the Faedwood and the Uplands. At its base in the west lies Naeliste, a fey city where Eladrin, Goblins, and Bullywugs have made a home beside the West Falls, which spill from the highlands above. It is at least a three day walk from Naeliste to the south or east edge of the Faedwood. There are no roads or paths, and travellers occasionally find themselves lost in the Feywild or Shadowfell.

Above the Teeth, in the Uplands, is the quiet village of Lome, reachable by foot in four days under good conditions. Along the southern edge of the forest stand two stone strongholds—**Grackle Rock** and **Tomot Rock**—built as much to watch the Faedwood as to guard against what might emerge from it.

## Resources and Landmarks

### Citizen's Hall

Located in Van Morgaaren Square, at the heart of Oechenfor, the Citizen's Hall serves as the city's seat of governance and public gathering. It houses council chambers, legal archives, and a wide stone forum where announcements, declarations, and public debates are held. Chief Citizen Aldrin Stoneward maintains an office here, and it remains the city's symbolic and administrative center.

### The Crossed Pike

A rough but well-kept tavern near the riverfront, frequented by caravan guards, mercenaries, and off-duty city watch. It's run by a retired soldier named Garra Pike, who keeps a spear above the bar and a blacklist behind it. The Crossed Pike is a good place to hear rumors, find muscle, or vanish into a crowd.

### Van Morgaaren Trade Company Headquarters

A stone-and-brick compound near the Mistywinde docks, this is the center of Oechenfor's trade empire. It coordinates merchant traffic, warehouse contracts, and long-range shipments across the Allied Cities. Access to rare goods, quick contracts, and discreet delivery services can often be arranged—for the right price.

### The Dustwright Armory

Specializing in practical weapons and hard-worn gear, the Dustwrights are a family of smiths with deep military roots. Their wares are well-regarded among caravan guards and border scouts. They occasionally take commissions for magical modifications if trusted or impressed.

### Thornfield's Chapel

Austere and orderly, this chapel is maintained by Brother Alistair Thornfield, a cleric of the Spirits of Order known for his stern manner and unwavering adherence to structure. He offers guidance to those seeking discipline, correction, or clarity—but not comfort. Thornfield never got along with Erol, and he distrusts those who favor intuition over law.

### Bastion Lock & Ward

A private security firm offering protection services, bodyguard contracts, vault wards, and magical containment. Founded by former adventurers and backed quietly by the Van Morgaarens, they operate with legal authority and minimal oversight. They're expensive, efficient, and discreet—and take non-interference very seriously.

### The Ironleaf Apothecary

A cramped but fragrant shop known for its remedies, reagents, and resistance potions. Proprietor Selkin Ironleaf, an aging halfling herbalist, buys exotic ingredients and sells field-tested concoctions tailored for travel beyond the city. The back room sometimes hosts quiet auctions for rare or illegal substances.

### Adrum's Vault

A subterranean archive and secure storage facility operated by Adrum Zannis and her descendants. It serves as a records hall, magical repository, and neutral ground for high-value exchanges. Entry requires recommendation or sponsorship by one of the Founders.

### The Rivergate Docks

The heart of Oechenfor's shipping and logistics network, built along the Mistywinde River. The docks are always active with barges, fishing boats, and armored trade vessels. Jobs are often posted here for escort duty, salvage, and ferry transport upriver toward the Teeth. The Bullybear Tavern where Peno usually sleeps is located here.

### The Scriptorum of Practical Enchantments

A workshop run by journeyman Spellscribes that produces reliable, mid-grade magical items and scrolls. Their work is standardized and sold through official city channels. Adventurers often come here for charms, light wards, or backup spell scrolls before venturing north.

### The Broken Ring

An underground ring of retired adventurers who provide services off the official books—guides, fences, informants, and the occasional muscle. Most are older, discreet, and no longer care for rules. If you need something done quietly, someone at the Broken Ring knows how.
