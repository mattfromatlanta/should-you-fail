# The Animated Plants of Qu

## Basic Information
**Race:** Animated Plants (Fey-created)
**Role:** Dominant society of Qu
**Status:** Active
**First Appearance:** Chapter 6a
**Location:** Qu, edge of the Feywild Wasteland

## Description

The dominant culture of Qu is a living society of animated plants—beings brought to life by Fey magic hundreds of years ago who have since mastered the druidic arts themselves. They no longer depend on outside magic to sustain or grow their community; they animate new plants through their own craft and pass those arts down through generations. They are the city's builders, traders, and keepers of memory.

## The Generational Divide

The deepest fault line in Qu's society is not politics or economics—it is climate, and what climate reveals about the world's wounds.

**The Elder Plants** were animated before the Estarans scarred the outer Feywild. They are creatures of gentle, temperate landscapes: birch trees with papery bark, spreading ferns, holly with its dark waxy leaves, berry bushes heavy with fruit. In the world that existed before the devastation, they thrived. In the damaged Feywild they now inhabit—rocky, arid, stripped of moisture—many of them struggle to stay healthy. Some are visibly suffering: leaves that won't fully unfurl, roots that find no purchase, bark that cracks in the dry air.

**The Younger Plants** were animated into a world already changed. They are desert-born: sage and its silver-grey resilience, aloe with its thick stored water, cacti in all their armored variety. They do not know the world the elders describe. The wasteland is simply home to them—harsh but navigable, their forms perfectly suited to it.

## Culture and Feeling

There is genuine sadness among the elder Plants of Qu. They remember—or carry the memory of—a Feywild that was green and wet and wild. They long for that world to be healed and know that their young struggle in a land that was made for something different than what they are.

But there is no bitterness toward the younger generation—only love. The elders take profound pride in the young cacti and desert plants: hardy, cheerful, deeply alive. The Fey arts that created the elders were passed down faithfully, and the younger generation carries them with skill and confidence. The community continues to grow.

Among the younger plants, there is also quiet optimism. They know the story of what was lost, and many share the hope for healing. But they are not broken by living in the world as it is. They have built something real in Qu, and they intend to keep building.

## Relationship to the Party

The party passed through Qu during their pursuit of Cetyri and Bronik. The Plants of Qu represent the human cost—if "human" is the right word—of the Estaran devastation: not a catastrophic loss, but a slow, generational reshaping of what life looks like and what can grow. They are a living reminder of why the Estarans must be stopped.

## Druidic Tradition

The Plants of Qu practice a specialized form of druid magic focused on animating and sustaining plant life. This tradition originated from the Fey who first gave them life, but has been developed and refined by the plants themselves over centuries. They animate new members of their community deliberately and carefully—it is considered both a sacred act and a civic one.
