# Chapter 7 NPCs

---

## Key Players

### Maelric Sturmwald
*Human. Chief Exarch of the Allied Cities. Veteran of the Rebellion.*

Fell into an unexplained catatonic trance two months ago while meditating on his subconscious. He is alive but unreachable. No character in the game understands what caused the trance or how to reverse it. His throne in the Hall of Allies sits empty. Gallifax has kept strict vigil ever since, trusting no one to reach him through psychic means. His collapse has thrown the Allied Cities into political crisis.

---

### Gallifax
*Ancient bronze dragon. Aegis and Steward of Free People.*

Protector of the Allied Cities for generations. His lair is in the Hall of Allies in Aris. During the Rebellion he witnessed the Estarans unleash nightmare magic that burned Metgal — appalled, he switched sides and helped mortals win. His hoard is modest for his age; he values knowledge and artifacts over gold.

Currently he keeps strict vigil over Maelric's catatonic body. He has been watching Nava Kurn and the Young Bastards for weeks. He holds what she lit responsible for everything that follows.

---

### Aldrin Stoneward
*Human. Chief Citizen and Founder of Oechenfor. Ageless veteran.*

Middle-aged in appearance, dark skin, bald, tall and regal. Carries the longsword Valor. Gifted with ageless life by the Estarans before the First Rebellion. Fought alongside Maelric and Gallifax in the Rebellion. Co-founded Oechenfor with Erol and other Founders about twenty years ago.

In Aris now, hoping to help the catatonic Maelric and stabilize the alliance. Caught instead in an escalating political crisis. His reputation as a fair-minded moderate makes him valuable — and trapped — in the middle. During the riot he is entirely consumed by Erol's safety and won't leave her side.

---

### Erol the Faed
*Eladrin warlock. Veteran. Co-Founder of Oechenfor and Lome.*

Stunning and wild — her presence is defined more by obvious supernatural power than beauty. Her aspect (like all Eladrin) reflects her mood, but Erol's range is unusual: she blends aspects or takes on ones other Eladrin don't. A morning thunderstorm. A false spring in January. During the riot, she appears as an electrical storm in winter.

Was once Milicent's warlock; conscripted by the Estarans; on return, broke the bond and eventually went to Xilo. Now Xilo's warlock. Co-founded Oechenfor with Aldrin — they were once partners. Deep mutual affection remains; Aldrin is the one who got away from her perspective, though she knows she can't live in his structured orbit.

During the riot: completely drained after calling up tremendous arcane fury for what she believed was an existential threat, then being crippled by Estaran psychic tampering. Won't be useful for some time.

---

### Lamat Velu
*Veteran. Co-Founder of Oechenfor and Lome. Partner to Erol.*

Race unknown. Founded the Orchard Path Monastery. Lives with Erol. A more internally ordered mind than Erol — the order is a personal discipline, not an external one. Disappeared in autumn after discovering Milicent was targeting him; returned with Erol at the end of Chapter 2.

During the riot: was sent ahead by Erol to warn Gallifax. Found at the Hall of Allies if the party goes there early. He failed to get the dragon to emerge and isn't sure what to do. Has pieced together that the waterfront event was part of a larger plan.

---

## The Conspirators

### Ardo Manessi
*Human. City councilor of Aris. Age 47.*

Publicly: a charismatic, pragmatic statesman backing the Veterans while projecting moderate sympathy for all sides. He moves rooms and builds alliances. The image of the responsible leader the city will want when the dust settles.

Privately: has been plotting Maelric's death for months. Ekert Shae is his secret instrument — employed for years, operating inside Nava's circle at Ardo's command. Ardo views Ekert as the knife he needs to kill his rivals and would cut him loose without hesitation if it served him. During the riot he is at Aldrin's home, receiving couriers, reinforcing his image.

*The assassins do not know Ardo exists. Only Ekert knows the full chain.*

---

### Ekert Shae
*Illusionist. Ardo Manessi's secret operative.*

Not charismatic, but compensates with remarkable cunning and real magical ability. Reads who is vulnerable, what pressure to apply, when to act. Joined the Young Bastards at Ardo's command and climbed into Nava's inner circle as a trusted advisor — a position he cultivated deliberately and holds through genuine usefulness.

The waterfront illusion was his plan from the start. He designed it, provided the magical execution, and amplified Nava's voice for full effect. Nava organized the crowd and the message; Ekert built the weapon she held.

Has no ideology. Veterans and Young Bastards alike are terrain. He expects to be rewarded handsomely when Ardo becomes Exarch. That expectation keeps him careful and committed.

During the riot: at Nava's side, consoling her disingenuously and subtly steering her toward dangerous situations — keeping her near the worst of the fighting.

*If cornered, he will throw Ardo under the bus without hesitation to save himself.*

---

### Corven
*Human. Young Bastard militant. Ringleader of the assassination trio.*

The steadiest of the three and the one Ekert worked on first. Longtime Young Bastard with a genuine grievance — lost work, lost status. Has convinced himself that removing Maelric is a political act, not a murder. Knows the Underworks well. Keeps the other two focused and is the most likely to follow through.

**Hardest to break under questioning. Most likely to have useful information about Ekert if he does talk.**

---

### Aldrek
*Human. Young Bastard militant. Muscle of the trio.*

Large, aggressive, impatient. Fewer political convictions than Corven — his anger is more personal. The biggest risk in the trio is Aldrek deciding to move early or alone. In a confrontation, most dangerous physically. Least useful information source.

---

### Breva
*Human. Young Bastard militant. Youngest of the trio.*

Most recently radicalized. Still has doubts she hasn't voiced to Corven or Aldrek. Ekert recruited her last, partly because her apparent loyalty to Nava made her useful cover. Doesn't fully understand how thoroughly she's been used.

**The weak point. The most likely to break under any real pressure. Fastest route to Ekert's name. If the party can reach her before the assassination attempt, she is the most reachable.**

---

## Political Figures

### Nava Kurn
*Dwarf. Age 45. Leader of the Young Bastards.*

Charismatic and sharp-tongued on the surface. A genuine idealist underneath. Truly believes that the generations born since the Rebellion have been cheated — that Veteran longevity has calcified power into a permanent aristocracy that was never supposed to exist. She does not hate Veterans personally. She thinks they are a broken class: people who outlived their moment and cannot see it.

Her theory of change is confrontation through exposure. She organized the waterfront illusion because she believed — sincerely — that showing Aris how unprepared the Veteran leadership is might shame them into yielding. She miscalculated badly.

What she does not know: the plan she thought was hers was designed and executed by Ekert, who shaped her instinct into an operation. And she does not know that Ekert and Ardo have been quietly stoking Veteran resentment of the Young Bastards for months — poisoning every path toward reconciliation. Every step she takes toward her goals, she takes on a floor Ekert has been quietly tilting against her.

During the riot: despondent. Trying to rally her demonstrators to be brave and represent themselves honorably. Ekert is at her side.

## Allies in Aris

### Yrk the Trickster (Ar'gix)
*Ancient goblin. Nearly 2,000 years old.*

Survivor of the First War. Co-created the Shadow Projector with Milicent ~400 years ago. Broke with her when she began enslaving mortals and fled to the Drekklands. Great-great-great-grandfather to Eigitch. Confers the Trickster's Blessing through ritual combat death.

Currently in Aris with Felicity. Arrived hours before the party found the waterfront. Has already seen Xilo, Twi'Xu, and Bronik. Has a world-crossing device and needs to talk to the party about Metgal.

---

### Felicity Roo
*Human warlock. Milicent's chief mortal operative.*

Led the quickling kidnapping operations in Oechenfor. Set fire to the safehouse that ultimately led the party to Milicent's fortress. Directly represents Milicent's interests in the mortal world.

Currently traveling with Yrk. Arrived in Aris hours before the riot, couldn't find the party. With the world-crossing device now ready, she and Yrk have urgent news about Chapter 8.

Can conceivably intervene with Gallifax if positioned near Nava when events move — one of the only realistic checks on him in that scenario.

---

### Xilo, Lord of Robins
*Archfey. One of the seven original Rebel Fey.*

An Animal Lord — he literally is a robin, moving between small bird, hybrid, and humanoid form. Quick, bright, and restless. His lieutenant Twi'Xu is stationed in Aris. Volunteered to travel with the party after they slew Egedzoo; the first Fey lord to visit the mortal capital.

Makes no binding pledges on behalf of the Rebel Fey — he speaks for himself, though he carries influence. In Aris now, building contacts and acting as an envoy of goodwill. Could intervene with Gallifax if already near Nava when events move.
