ENCOUNTER DESIGN — GULLUX/BRONIK STRUCTURE (Critical)
------------------------------------------------------

The encounter nodes (enc-ch8-05b, enc-ch8-05c) have the brothers' personalities and positions
swapped relative to ch8-ice-world-history.txt.

enc-ch8-05b (The Old Garrison) describes Bronik as volatile, reckless, and baitable — assigned
to hold the garrison by Gullux. But per the history doc, Bronik is the cunning one and lives in
the remote section of the garrison, observing. Gullux is the bully.

enc-ch8-05c (Gullux Comes Down) describes Gullux as patient and methodical — the one who stays
back while Bronik holds the garrison. Again, the opposite of the established personalities.

The structural question: Do you want the garrison encounter (E05b) to feature Gullux as the
main threat, with Bronik observing from the remote section and potentially offering help after
Gullux falls — making E05c obsolete or repurposed? Or is the sequence (garrison first, then
Gullux responds) correct, just with the brothers' names swapped throughout?

ANSWER:

The garrison is large and there are four sub-encounters. At one point it held a thousand Estarans, but they left after the Fey were defeated. Now about a dozen frost giants and two adult white dragons (Gullux and Bronik) have taken residence:

A) Entrance + Garrison basement: there is only one ground-level entrance to the garrison. Estarans have great feathered angel wings and can fly. Their main entrance was on the rafters level of the garrison's great hall. The ground entrance is in a separate structure from the main hall: A small sentry tower on the southwest corner of the compound. There is a large gate that can be opened. On the ground floor of this small tower, there is a large floor hatch (20' across) which slides open to allow a creature to drop down to a tunnel. This tunnel is wide, tall, and unlit, leading a few hundred feet east-north-east. It leads to the garrison basement.

The garrison basement has 30' ceilings and is as wide and long as the garrison above, which is to say many hundred feet long from north to south and about a third as wide east-west. There is debris and trash from the Estarans supply stash. What was left behind has decayed over centuries and is now being plundered by the Frost Giants. There is nothing of significant value.

There are a handful of frost giants looking around the basement when the party arrives. They are spread out enough and the room is big enough that they won't have to take them all on at once, but there could be fights in rapid succession.

Two hatches in the basement's ceiling lead to the garrison above. They are also large and slide open by magic to let creatures ascend. There is a tunnel from the northeast corner like the first tunnel. This leads to the very bottom of the control tower, which stands a few hundred feet east of the great hall's northeast corner. Natural light reaches the bottom from above, though dimly. It is possible to fly to the top of the control tower from the basement

B) Garrison Great Hall Ground Floor:

The ground floor is wide open. Great pillars lead to the roof, about 130' above. This room has many rows of bunks and a few tables. There aren't many other decorations. Two hatches connect to the basement and a flying creature can fly to the platforms in the garrison rafters unimpeded.

A larger group of 8 Frost Giants are here. TODO: create a frost giant leader and perhaps a shaman.

C) Garrison Rafters

A set of broad wooden platforms 100' above the great hall floor. There is a large gap in the west wall (100' above the ground) which was the main entrance for the Estaran soldiers. Gullux has made his nest on this level.

D) Control Tower

This is a distinct, much larger tower to the east of the northeast corner of the great hall. It is 200' tall and the room at its top was once the nerve center of Estaran operations on Ikellor. Now it is Bronik's nest. He has knocked a hole in the west wall which he uses to come and go.


Encounter FLOW:

The party can explore and may have to fight giants or dragons as they go. Frost Giants see the party as a sign of the Estarans returning and will fight as if their lives depend on keeping the party from telling the Estarans about them.

If the party meet Gullux, he will fight them ferociously, likely to his death. He will taunt them in Draconic, frequently promising that he is Gullux the Terror and he will freeze them and eat their icy guts.

If the party meet Bronik after they defeat Gullux, he will try to bargain for his life and promise to tell them where they can find a great treasure if they let him go. He'll tell them about the Dead Forest and Torma's lair. If they have not yet fought Gullux, Bronik will try to fight them but will flee quickly if things go bad.

ESTARAN SOLDIERS AT FARTHIM — NO NPC NODES
--------------------------------------------

The history doc names all 12 Estarans stationed at Farthim:

  Cersael (Commander)
  Tarliel (Chief Aid)
  Kemiel (Arcanist)
  Motiel, Casius, Taor, Verael, Vasor, Zasais, Cellius, Kaus, Rael (soldiers)

Their logs, drill records, and strategy documents would be in the garrison and Projector Hall.
Should these become individual NPC nodes, or is documenting them within the garrison/Farthim
location entries sufficient?

ANSWER:

There is no need to document them individually. The names are just in case they come up. There should be one node to describe the last remaining Estarans on this world and their relationship to the people of Farthim.

Important detail: The Estarans didn't leave much behind. The star charts leading to other worlds are embossed in the walls of the Projector Hall, which is why they weren't removd. They didn't leave documents behind. They are generally very careful not to leave technology or records because they don't want mortals to learn their magic or techniques. If an Estaran finds out about the party's Radiant Lance, they would consider it a grave transgression and a dire warning that this universe is slipping away from them.

Important detail 2:

We'll want to review the nodes we have on Estaran history and culture. Once updates are finished from this document, create a new summary of what the game book has to say about Estarans. We'll be looking for inconsistencies and gaps in that area next.

FARTHIM SETTLERS' ORIGIN
--------------------------

The history doc says the original settlers came from "the world of Aris and Oechenfor and from
other worlds." The Farthimers have no cultural memory of this.

Should location-farthim note that some ancestors came from Aerus — a detail a sharp party member
might eventually connect, especially if they learn the world's Estaran name is Ikellor and their
home world is Aerus?

ANSWER:

Yes, make this connection. In fact, the star chart in the Projection Hall--which should show routes to three other worlds--should include Aerus.

A note on the Estaran language: The common tongue derives from Estaran, which is why it is present on the worlds that Estarans have inhabited. Estaran is more formal than common and the alphabet is more complex. It takes a DC 10 study action (INT) to decipher a text in original Estaran for a common speaker, but it can be done.

On other languages: There is, in fact, a single common tongue across all worlds. However, all the fey languages -- Sylvan, Goblin, Eladrin, Bullywug -- are wildly different on different worlds. There has been no cross-polination of different fey languages across worlds. Draconic is universal across time and across the cosmos. Estarans and Fey are natural enemies. Dragons are the truest expression of existence. That is why they can destroy the Estarans and why the Estarans fear them. Their innate qualities do not change over time. That includes their language.
