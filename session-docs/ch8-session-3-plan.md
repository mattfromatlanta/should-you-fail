## 1. Frost Giants in the Field

**Trigger:** Any foray east of Farthim — escorting a hunting party, marching toward the garrison, or scouting the river valley. The giants control everything east of the ring.

### How it unfolds
- **Setup:** Open snowfield east of the ring. Deep snow, no real cover, poor footing for smaller creatures, room for giants to use reach. If a Farthim hunting party is along, Amalia or Marx briefs the party on the pattern: giants come fast, hit hard, don't negotiate.
- **The patrol:** 4–5 frost giants. They've been tracking the party for some time before contact (Gullux's scouts have been watching since arrival).
- **First moves:** Spread wide to cut off retreat west toward Farthim. Press aggressively. Game animals are incidental — the priority is the party.

### How the Giants React (key beat)
When they get close enough to see the party clearly, the giants react with **horror, not rage**. Weapons, magic, organized fighting — to a giant this is the signature of an Estaran return.

They assume the Estarans sent the party — a mirror of Farthim's belief, from the opposite side. The Estarans killed giants in great numbers; even Cersael's Section maintained the cull for centuries.

Their fear is total. **They fight to the death.** They will not flee, will not parley, will not surrender — anything else risks word reaching the Estarans about where the tribe is, and that is the one thing they cannot allow.

A pressed or dying giant may scream in Giant or Elvish (Ikellor dialects) — fragments of old grief, rage that doesn't fit the body, hatred of "the angels" that no living giant should remember but every giant feels.

### What the Party Should Walk Away With
- The giants are terrified, not territorial.
- Both sides — Farthim and the giants — believe the Estarans sent the party.
- Something in the giants remembers Fey grief. They cannot place it.
- The patrol came from the east, toward the mountains: the garrison.

Cetyri (pseudodragon) observes from a hood or pack. Still and watchful. He does not engage. Gullux and Bronik are not here.

## 2. The Garrison

### Introduction (≈100 words)

The Estaran garrison stands in the mountains north-east of Farthim, at the source of the river that runs through the valley. It was built three centuries ago to support the war against the Fey — scaled for a thousand soldiers, fortified, austere, monumental. When the Fey were defeated the wartime force departed, and the garrison sat empty for a century. Centuries ago Farthim supplied it; no Farthimer alive has been there. In the last month, roughly a dozen frost giants and the dragons **Gullux** and **Bronik** have taken residence at the dragons' insistence. The structure is intact, cold, and mostly hollow. Estarans fly — every entrance reflects that.

### Sub-Area 6a — Sentry Tower, Tunnel, Basement

**Approach:** The only ground entrance is a small sentry tower at the southwest corner of the compound. The main hall has no ground door — the Estarans flew in from a 100-foot opening in the rafters.

**The tower:** Large gate. On the ground floor, a 20-foot magical floor hatch slides open to a wide unlit tunnel running a few hundred feet east-north-east into the basement.

**The basement:** 30-foot ceilings; runs the full footprint of the great hall above — many hundreds of feet north-south, about a third as wide. Centuries-rotted Estaran supply stash; nothing of value.

**Threats:** A handful of frost giants scavenging through the debris, spread out enough that the party can engage them in rapid succession rather than all at once.

**Connections:**
- Two large ceiling hatches → up into the great hall.
- A second tunnel in the northeast corner → bottom of the control tower. Dim natural light from far above; flying creatures can ascend the full height.

### Sub-Area 6b — Great Hall, Ground Floor

**Layout:** Wide open. Pillars rise 130 feet to the roof. Rows of bunks, a few tables, sparse decoration. Two hatches connect to the basement. A flying creature can reach the rafters unimpeded.

**Threats:** ~8 frost giants gathered here.

This is the biggest concentration of giants in the garrison and the most likely set-piece engagement.

### Sub-Area 6c — Rafters (Gullux's Nest)

**Layout:** Broad wooden platforms 100 feet above the great-hall floor. A 100-foot-tall gap in the west wall — the Estarans' main flight entrance. Gullux has made his nest here.

**Threat: Gullux** — Adult white dragon. Older brother. Bully and sadist; sees smaller creatures as playthings.

**Behavior:** Fights ferociously, **likely to his death.** Taunts in Draconic, announces himself:
> *"I am Gullux the Terror — I will freeze you and eat your icy guts."*

No parley. No retreat.

### Sub-Area 6d — Control Tower (Bronik's Nest)

**Layout:** A distinct, much larger tower east of the great hall's northeast corner. 200 feet tall. The top room was once the nerve center of Estaran operations on Ikellor. Bronik has knocked a hole in the west wall to come and go.

**Threat: Bronik** — Adult white dragon. Younger brother, slightly smaller, the cunning one. Despises Gullux. Resents his mother; yearns for her death but knows he cannot challenge her.

**Behavior depends on order of encounter:**
- **If party reaches Bronik AFTER Gullux has fallen:** He **bargains for his life**. Offers information in exchange for being let go — specifically, the location of the Dead Forest and Torma's lair, framed as "a great treasure."
- **If party reaches Bronik BEFORE Gullux:** He will try to fight, but **flees quickly** if things turn against him. Not a strategist; not committed to dying for the position.

### Encounter Flow Notes
- The garrison is large; areas can be engaged in sequence. The party can scout, retreat, return.
- Frost giants react the same here as in the field: horror, then fight to the death rather than risk word reaching the Estarans.
- Estarans left nothing portable behind. No documents, no caches, no tech. Only what is embossed or built-in survives — and nothing in the garrison is embossed the way the Projector Hall's star chart is.
- After the garrison falls, the path opens north-east into the Dead Forest, and from there to the rifts that lead to the dead Feywild and Torma. **Saved for a later session.**
