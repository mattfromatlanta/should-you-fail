# Chapter 7 Session Guide: Aris, The Fracturing Capital

## Status Going In

- Party is at the waterfront amphitheater, observing in secret
- A hundred radiant beams have just descended at sunset, each carrying an apparent Estaran warrior with a flaming sword
- Alarm bells are ringing across Aris
- **Picks up at E06**

---

## E06: The Riot and the Dragon

### Setup

The Estaran warriors are descending. The party is at the waterfront. Veterans are flooding in. Everything is about to go very wrong — and then very strange.

---

### Read Aloud — Veterans Respond

> Bells ring out and deep horns echo across the city. Shouting from the avenues leading to the waterfront — at first a few voices, then dozens, then a hundred or more Veterans race into the plaza. They are disorganized and the scene quickly becomes chaotic. The Estarans continue descending and are now very close.
>
> Most but not all of the Veterans are armed. Some are struggling to put on ill-fitting armor. A few are shouting orders but no one is listening. Then — Aldrin charges in on the back of an armored rhinoceros. He halts at the leading edge of the ragged crowd. Eyes flock to him. He draws his famous long blade and calls the Veterans to rally. Some call out with pride, but the response is less enthusiastic than expected. Fear has gripped them.
>
> Erol arrives by misty step. She has the aspect of an electrical storm in winter — none of the party have ever seen an Eladrin in a state like this. The depth of her power, only hinted at before, is now undeniable. An Estaran touches down and blazes with radiant energy. He turns to Erol and squares with her. Then she seems to freeze. She grabs the sides of her head and cries out in agony. She falls to her knees.
>
> Aldrin sees this and forgets everything else. He charges his mount to put himself between the Estaran and Erol, giving a ferocious, primal scream at his enemy. But Erol is not alone — many Veterans are similarly crippled, and some are already fleeing in terror. A wave of despair hits the party. The battle is lost before it starts...

*Pause. Collect player responses. Ask for their affective reaction as well as their actions. Play out the first moments, then continue.*

---

### Read Aloud — The Illusion Collapses

> Every Estaran warrior freezes instantly — even suspended in space. The telltale translucency of an illusion. The stunned Veterans stare in silence. The plaza is profoundly quiet. Then dry, staccato coughing from the thick of it — Erol is recovering. Other affected Veterans regain themselves. Murmuring rises, then incredulous shouts.
>
> Behind the crowd, away from the river, a single magically amplified voice calls out: *"VETERANS. LOOK AT YOURSELVES. YOU'VE ALL GONE TO PIECES."* A symphony of horns and drums picks up. Chanting echoes down city streets: *"MAKE WAY. MAKE WAY. MAKE WAY TODAY. FOR EVERY SOUL IS KING."*
>
> The mood among the Veterans turns to fury. Curses and oaths. *"It's Nava. Has to be. Damn her, and all her damned ingrates."*
>
> Bands of Veterans form and charge off into the city looking for a fight. Crowds of Young Bastard demonstrators pour into the streets, chanting with fists in the air. Where Veterans encounter Bastard crowds, bloodshed begins.

---

### NPC Positions During the Riot

**Aldrin Stoneward** — At the waterfront, then entirely preoccupied with Erol's safety. Will not leave her. Gives the party advice but will not join them.

**Erol the Faed** — Completely drained. She called up tremendous arcane fury for what she believed was an existential threat, then was crippled by what is actually a method of Estaran psychic tampering designed to suppress Veteran resistance. She won't be useful for some time. Won't leave Aldrin's side.

**Lamat Velu** — Was sent ahead by Erol to warn Gallifax. Found at the Hall of Allies if the party goes there before Gallifax emerges. He couldn't get the dragon to come out and isn't sure what to do. Has pieced together that the waterfront event was part of a larger plan. Wants to hear what the party saw. Considering gathering clear-headed allies to calm things down.

**Ardo Manessi** — At Aldrin's home. Receiving and sending couriers to allies around the city. Playing the role of deeply concerned, thoughtful statesman — exactly the moderate voice the city will want when this is over. Only one person in Aris knows what he truly plans.

**Nava Kurn** — Can be found with investigation. Rallying her demonstrators: be brave, don't fight unless attacked, represent themselves honorably. Privately despondent — she did not anticipate this. Ekert is at her side, consoling her disingenuously and subtly steering her toward more dangerous situations. On the off chance a furious Veteran kills her for him.

**Ekert Shae** — At Nava's side. Playing the supportive advisor. His real goal in this moment: keep Nava in dangerous ground.

**Felicity and Yrk** — In the city. Arrived hours earlier, couldn't find the party. Have already seen Xilo, Twi'Xu, and Bronik. They have a world-crossing device and they need to talk. If the party goes out into the city to intervene in the riot, they will find each other.

---

### Read Aloud — Gallifax Leaves

*Five hours in. Fires spreading. No sign the violence will stop.*

> Whether prompted by others or acting on his own judgment, Gallifax emerges from the Hall of Allies. He goes where the violence is worst, alighting between warring bands and demonstrating his crackling fury. He commands Veterans to stand down. Those who do not yield, he hurts. He is trying hard to restrain himself.

**This is the moment.** The window for the assassins has opened. Move to E07.

---

### Mechanics

- Each street brawl: 2–4 Veterans vs. 2–4 Young Bastards (commoner or guard stats, nonlethal by default)
- De-escalate a confrontation: DC 15 Persuasion (advantage with Aldrin, Erol, or Nava present)
- Streets are difficult terrain; visibility poor; fires may start in the Living Districts
- DC 12 Investigation (advantage if party has seen real Estarans) notices the illusion was too static, lacks full dimensional depth
- Detect magic or equivalent immediately reveals illusion

## E07: The Attempt on Maelric

### Setup

The moment Gallifax leaves the Hall of Allies, a signal is sent. Three Young Bastard militants — Corven, Aldrek, and Breva, manipulated into action over days by Ekert Shae — enter the Hall through sealed Estaran tunnels in the Underworks.

**There are no hints.** The party must decide on their own to go to Maelric when they see Gallifax take to the air. Any other priority — the riot, their contacts, Nava, Aldrin — means the assassins complete their work. The moment is brief. The window is real.

---

### The Assassins

| Name | Role | Disposition |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Corven** | Ringleader | Steadiest; knows the Underworks; hardest to break; most useful info if he talks |
| **Aldrek** | Muscle | Aggressive, impatient; most likely to act rashly; least useful info source |
| **Breva** | Youngest | Most recently radicalized; still has doubts; most likely to surrender; fastest route to Ekert's name |

None of them know Ardo Manessi exists in this scheme. They know Ekert as "Nava's chum."

---

### If the Party Arrives in Time

They find the three inside the Hall before the deed is done. The party is substantially more powerful — this is not a balanced fight. Corven and Aldrek will attempt to withdraw through the Underworks entrance while buying time. Breva is the most likely to surrender immediately.

**Capture and questioning:**
- All three will defend what they intended — they claim they did what everybody wanted anyway
- Under pressure, they surface "Nava's chum" Ekert as someone deeply sympathetic to their ideas who seemed to think bold men needed to act
- Breva gives up Ekert's name fastest
- Corven is hardest to break but holds the most information
- The dead-drop location for their payment and the Underworks route both trace back to Ardo if investigated further

**Maelric survives.** He remains in his trance, undisturbed. Gallifax, returning to find him alive, is grim and silent. The weight of having left is plain on his face.

---

### If the Party Does Not Go

Corven and Aldrek are efficient. Maelric does not suffer. By the time anyone raises the alarm, the assassins are back in the Underworks and gone. No body left behind. No sign of entry. The Hall's guards — those not drawn out by the riot — find Maelric cold in his trance-throne. The wound is small and precise.

Ardo Manessi will be among the first to arrive on the scene, looking shaken and grief-stricken.

Move to E08.

---

## E08: The Aftermath

### If Maelric Survived

> Gallifax returns to the Hall of Allies and stands over Maelric for a long moment without speaking. His relief and shame are both plain.

The assassins are likely in custody or fled. If captured:
- They will not deny their actions — they'll be loud about it
- They will surface Ekert Shae as "Nava's chum" who kept reminding them bold men were needed
- Ekert will try to avoid being interviewed. Under real scrutiny he cannot conceal his role. **If cornered, he will throw Ardo under the bus without hesitation.**

Ardo remains in Aris, still dangerous, still positioned as the moderate voice of stability. He is not beaten yet.

**Evidence thread if the party pulls it:**
1. Capture/pressure the trio → they name Ekert
2. Corner Ekert → he names Ardo
3. Dead-drop location + Underworks route both trace back to Ardo
4. Give Aldrin or Erol sufficient reason to dig → they can act on it

---

### If Maelric Died

> Gallifax returns to find Maelric cold. No roar — something lower. A sound that shakes the crystal in the walls and the bones in the chest. He does not move for several minutes. Then he asks, very quietly, who did this. No one can give him a name.

Gallifax does not investigate. He has watched Nava Kurn for weeks. He knows what she lit. He finds her in the square.

> Nava sees him coming and does not run. She faces him. Gallifax does not speak. He pins her to the ground under a massive claw and electrocutes her at point blank range with his breath. It is not a warning. It is not a performance. It takes less than a breath to decide. Hundreds of people are watching. Nava Kurn has no chance of surviving.

Gallifax will fight anyone who stands in his way. He is fast enough and strong enough that stopping him is unlikely. He is not interested in killing the party, but he will not hold back. Erol, Lamat, and Aldrin are too far or too spent. **Xilo** could intervene if already near Nava. **Felicity and Yrk** are the only other realistic check — if they are with or close to Nava when events move.

When it is over, Gallifax looks at the crowd, then lifts from the square and does not come back. He leaves Aris. He leaves Maelric's body. He leaves the city he has guarded for a century.

**Ardo's path:** With Maelric dead and Nava killed, Ardo moves quickly into the vacuum — the grieving, reasonable voice of stability. He will be chosen as the next Citizen Exarch, and it will seem like other people's ideas.

The evidence to expose him still exists. Whether Aris, without Gallifax and without Maelric, can act on it is uncertain. But it is not nothing.

---

### The Party's Choice

Felicity and Yrk deliver the news they've been trying to tell the party since they arrived: the world-crossing device is ready. They believe they know where to go. **Chapter 8 takes the party to Metgal.**

Whatever remains unfinished in Aris — Ardo, the city's political wreckage, the people they've come to care about — the clock has run out. Stay and pursue it, or go.
