# Chapter 6a: Into the Wild

## Overview

After securing Milicent's agreement to cease her mortal conscription, the party returns to Naeliste to convince Cetyri to join their cause. However, they discover that Cetyri has fled to the Feywild after Lomani's questioning revealed her knowledge of dragon multiplicity. With Bronik accompanying him out of friendship, Cetyri has run to protect the secret of his multiverse-spanning nature.

The party pursues them into the Feywild, where they find themselves in a scorched wasteland—the outer regions devastated by ancient Estaran influence. After encounters with hobgoblin bandits and a tense meeting with Twi'Xu (Animal Lord and lieutenant to Xilo, one of the Rebel Fey), they track Cetyri and Bronik to the settlement of Qu.

Through honest conversation, the party convinces Cetyri to stop running and accept his potential role in Milicent's plan. Not wanting to miss the opportunity to gain Xilo as an ally, the party commissions an airship journey deeper into the Feywild's eternal twilight forest. However, they are attacked mid-flight by hobgoblin sky-riders and Egedzoo, the adult blue dragon they encountered at Rinick's Diner. Though they repel the attack, their airship is damaged and crashes near a mysterious tower structure.

Now grounded in the heart of the Feywild, the party discovers the tower is the memorial tomb of Ozevriel—a hero slain in the first rebellion against the Estarans. Once a pilgrimage site, it has fallen into disrepair and been largely forgotten. The party explores the memorial, defeats trapped demons, and discovers Milicent's old observatory at the tower's peak. There they meet Hootini and thoroughly review Milicent's research on the Estarans. Finally, Xilo arrives at the crashed airship to meet with them.

## Key Events

### 1. Discovery of Cetyri's Flight
Upon returning to Naeliste after their alliance with Milicent, the party discovers that Cetyri has fled. Through conversation with Lomani Nae, they learn that her careful questioning about dragon multiplicity spooked the young gold dragon. Cetyri realized that Lomani had discovered the truth about his soul reaching across realities, and he ran to keep that knowledge from spreading. Bronik, Erol's half-dragon son, accompanied him out of simple friendship and loyalty.

### 2. Pursuit Into the Feywild
The party is only a day or so behind Cetyri and Bronik. They immediately set out in pursuit, crossing into the Feywild via one of the hidden paths through the Faedwood.

### 3. The Scorched Wasteland
The party emerges into a barren, desolate landscape cloaked in perpetual twilight. The outer regions of the Feywild—those closest to the Material Plane—were scorched and ruined during the Estaran occupation. There is a fundamental incompatibility between the wild, chaotic nature of the Fey world and the intensely ordered, geometric nature of Estaran reality. The clash left these borderlands dead.

The landscape is rocky desert dominated by enormous thorn vines the size of mountain ranges, visible on the horizon to the north and east—monuments to the violence done to this realm.

### 4. Hobgoblin Bandit Attack
Shortly after arriving, the party is attacked by hobgoblin bandits who operate in the wasteland. The party successfully repels them and scouts the area from the air, discovering what appears to be a structure a mile away along a ride leading northeast.

### 5. Rinick's Diner
The structure proves to be a roadside watering hole called Rinick's Diner—a surprisingly civilized establishment in the middle of the wasteland. Rinick, a human originally from the Allied Cities, has created a thriving neutral-ground business. All bandit factions and travelers respect the diner as a safe zone where violence is forbidden.

### 6. Twi'Xu and the Blue Dragon
Upon entering the diner, the party stumbles into a tense standoff. Two solitary patrons are watching each other with dangerous intensity. Aelira uses her Robe of Eyes to perceive their true forms:

- **Twi'Xu**, Animal Lord and Lieutenant of Bluebirds, sworn vassal to Xilo, Lord of Robins and one of the seven Rebel Fey.

- **Egedzoo**, an adult blue dragon seeking to establish territory in the wasteland and unite the hobgoblin bandits under his rule

Twi'Xu is observing the dragon on Xilo's orders, but neither wants to fight in Rinick's neutral territory. Egedzoo departs quietly to avoid direct confrontation with an Animal Lord.

### 7. Arranging Audience with Xilo
The party engages Twi'Xu in conversation and explains their search for Cetyri and Bronik. Twi'Xu confirms the two passed through heading to Qu and are likely already there. When the party asks for an audience with Xilo, Twi'Xu reveals he is close enough to Xilo's inner council to know that the Lord of Robins will want to meet anyone working with Milicent. All the Rebel Fey understand that without Milicent's drastic measures during the rebellion, the Estarans would never have been driven away.

Twi'Xu agrees to help arrange a meeting but cannot leave his post—doing so would give Egedzoo too much freedom to operate without surveillance.

### 8. Journey to Qu
The party continues southwest to the settlement of Qu, a rougher town on the edge of the wasteland. They encounter local robbers after arrival but handle them without serious trouble.

### 9. Confronting Cetyri
Using magic to locate them, the party finds Cetyri and Bronik just as they are preparing to leave Qu and travel deeper into the Feywild. The party arranges a heart-to-heart conversation and lays everything bare: Milicent's plan, the depths of the Estaran threat and their 54 previous apocalypses, the Dragon Singularity, and the critical role Cetyri can play in saving all existence.

Cetyri is overwhelmed but understands the stakes. He agrees not to run anymore. While he needs time to become comfortable with his role and the risks involved, he recognizes that joining the cause is the only path forward.

### 10. Commissioning the Airship
Not wanting to miss the opportunity to gain Xilo as a powerful ally—and hoping to corroborate what they've learned about the Estaran threat—the party commissions Captain Marvo and his airship to take them deeper into the Feywild, toward the eternal twilight forest at the continent's heart.

### 11. Aerial Attack
As the airship pushes north into the primal Feywild, it is attacked mid-flight by hobgoblin bandits riding wyverns, led by Egedzoo himself. The party successfully repels the attack and drives off the dragon without destroying him entirely. However, the airship's engine sustains critical damage during the battle.

### 12. Emergency Landing
Captain Marvo is nearly killed but manages to survive as the damaged airship makes an emergency landing. The party finds themselves grounded near a mysterious tower structure rising from the eternal twilight forest.

### 13. Discovering the Memorial
With their airship requiring repairs and unable to continue, the party sets out to explore the nearby tower. They soon realize this is no ordinary structure—it is the memorial tomb of Ozevriel, a hero slain during the first rebellion against the Estarans.

Once a pilgrimage site revered throughout Fey history, the memorial has fallen into disrepair over the centuries. Now only a few remember its existence or significance.

### 14. The Locked Curse
The party encounters a powerful arcane trap in the antechamber that has held demons and worgs for fifty years. Through careful investigation and skillful manipulation, they successfully release the trap.

### 15. Battle with the Trapped
When the trap releases, the demons and worgs attack. The party defeats 2 Arcanaloths and 5 Dire Worgs, freeing the memorial from its unwanted occupants.

### 16. Exploring Ozevriel's Tomb
The party enters the tomb proper and finds Ozevriel himself preserved in crystal, standing eternal watch. His verse from the Ode to Seven Lords is inscribed on the platform beneath him.

### 17. Milicent's Observatory
At the top of the spiral staircase, the party discovers Milicent's ancient observatory—a place she built to study the stars and moon before she became the Shadowdame. The chamber is remarkably preserved, filled with astronomical instruments, star charts, and decades of research.

### 18. Meeting Hootini
Hootini, Aelira's fey companion, awaits them in the observatory. He reveals that he is one of the Moon Fey who once served Milicent. He explains the significance of the location and encourages the party to investigate Milicent's research.

### 19. Investigating the Research
Over the course of several hours, the party thoroughly examines Milicent's documents and instruments. They discover critical information about the Estarans:

- The Estarans are not native to this world and come from somewhere else entirely
- Necrotic energy uniquely damages Estaran materials, representing a fundamental philosophical opposition
- The Estarans likely use devices called Radiant Projectors to travel between worlds
- Estaran incursions follow an escalating pattern that will eventually lead to permanent presence
- Milicent was methodically researching how to stop this escalation

The party takes four key documents with them for future reference.

### 20. Xilo's Arrival
After completing their investigation, Hootini senses Xilo's arrival. The Lord of Robins has come to the crashed airship. The party descends from the observatory to meet with him.

### 21. Meeting Xilo
At the grounded airship, the party meets Xilo, Lord of Robins—one of the original seven Rebel Fey who fought alongside Milicent against the Estarans. He is quick-witted, pragmatic, and immediately helpful, recognizing the party as Milicent's allies.

Xilo shares information about Milicent's past, Ozevriel's significance, and the weakened state of the Feywild since both heroes departed. He explains his current concern: Egedzoo is building power in the wasteland and must be stopped before he becomes too entrenched.

### 22. The Proposal
Xilo proposes that the party help him slay Egedzoo. He will arrange for the dragon's hobgoblin army to be engaged, allowing the party to infiltrate the lair with minimal obstruction. In exchange, they can claim the dragon's treasure and leave the Feywild with Xilo's alliance secured.

The party agrees to help slay Egedzoo.

## Major NPCs

### Lomani Nae
The scholar in Naeliste whose careful questions about dragon souls revealed she had discovered Cetyri's multiverse-spanning nature. Her investigation, while academically motivated, inadvertently triggered Cetyri's flight.

### Cetyri
The blind young gold dragon at the center of Milicent's plan. After learning that Lomani had uncovered the truth about his soul, he fled to the Feywild to prevent the knowledge from spreading. Through the party's honest explanation of the stakes, he agrees to stop running and accept his potential role as the Dragon Singularity. He needs time to process but understands the necessity.

### Bronik
Torma's half-dragon son and Cetyri's friend. He accompanied Cetyri into the Feywild out of simple loyalty and friendship, not fully understanding why they were running.

### Rinick
Human proprietor of Rinick's Diner, originally from the Allied Cities. Has built a successful neutral-territory establishment in the Feywild wasteland that is respected by all factions as a violence-free zone.

### Twi'Xu, Animal Lord, Lieutenant of Bluebirds
Powerful fey creature in service to Xilo, Lord of Robins. One of Xilo's closest advisors, stationed at Rinick's Diner to observe Egedzoo's activities. Agrees to help arrange a meeting with Xilo after learning the party works with Milicent. Understands the debt the Rebel Fey owe to the Shadowdame for her role in driving away the Estarans.

### Egedzoo
Adult blue dragon seeking to carve out territory in the Feywild wasteland and unite the hobgoblin bandit factions under his rule. Avoids direct confrontation with Twi'Xu at the diner but later attacks the party's airship with hobgoblin sky-riders. Driven off but not slain. Now the party must face him in his lair.

### Captain Marvo
Airship pilot commissioned by the party to transport them deeper into the Feywild. Nearly killed when his ship is damaged by Egedzoo's attack but manages to survive and land the vessel.

### Hootini
Aelira's fey companion, one of the Moon Fey who once served Milicent. He finds the party at Ozevriel's memorial and guides them through Milicent's research. Serves as a link between the party and the Fey world.

### Xilo, Lord of Robins
One of the seven Rebel Fey who fought alongside Milicent against the Estarans. Maintains surveillance on threats like Egedzoo through agents like Twi'Xu. Quick-witted, pragmatic, and loyal to Milicent's memory. Proposes an alliance: help him slay Egedzoo, and he will support the party against the Estarans when they return.

Dragon settlement where the party returns after allying with Milicent, only to discover Cetyri has fled.

The ancient forest path the party uses to cross from the Material Plane into the Feywild.

The outer regions of the Feywild, devastated by Estaran influence during the ancient war. A perpetual twilight landscape of rocky desert and dead land, marked by impossibly large thorn vines on the horizon. Home to hobgoblin bandit factions.

A roadside establishment in the wasteland, recognized as neutral territory where violence is forbidden. Serves travelers and bandits alike. Site of the tense encounter between Twi'Xu and Egedzoo.

The heart of the Feywild continent, where the realm exists in its primal, untouched state. A vast forest under perpetual twilight—the true nature of the Fey world.

### The Memorial of Ozevriel
An ancient tower and tomb complex dedicated to Ozevriel, a hero slain in the first rebellion against the Estarans. Once a pilgrimage site, now fallen into disrepair and largely forgotten. Contains:
- A stone patio entrance
- Underground tunnel and antechamber (where the trap and demons were)
- Ozevriel's tomb chamber with his crystal-preserved body
- A spiral staircase leading upward
- Milicent's observatory at the tower's peak

## Key Revelations

### Lomani's Discovery
Lomani Nae has successfully uncovered the truth about Cetyri's multiverse-spanning soul through careful scholarly investigation. Her questions revealed she understood the implications of dragon multiplicity and Cetyri's unique nature.

### Cetyri's Understanding
After the party's honest explanation, Cetyri now understands:
- The 54 previous apocalypses enacted by the Estarans
- The concept of the Dragon Singularity and what it means
- His potential to become a Unary Great Wyrm
- The existential stakes of refusing to participate
- That running is futile when all of existence hangs in the balance

While frightened and needing time to process, he has committed to the cause.

### The Wasteland's Origin
The scorched outer regions of the Feywild are a scar from the Estaran occupation. The fundamental incompatibility between Estaran order and Fey chaos left these borderlands dead when the two forces clashed. This demonstrates the reality-level damage the Estarans can inflict simply through their presence.

### Xilo's Position
Xilo and the other Rebel Fey understand that Milicent's extreme measures during the rebellion were necessary to drive away the Estarans. Despite any disagreements over her current methods, they recognize the debt they owe her and will be open to meeting her allies.

### Ozevriel's Significance
The memorial tower is dedicated to Ozevriel, who died in the first rebellion against the Estarans. He was one of the two pillars that gave the Feywild stability—his death weakened the realm fundamentally. Milicent was the other pillar, and her departure to the Shadowfell worsened the Feywild's condition. Without both of them, the Feywild lost its equilibrium and became unable to heal from Estaran corruption.

### Milicent's Research
Through examining her observatory, the party learns that before becoming the Shadowdame, Milicent was a brilliant scholar who studied order and predictability—unusual for a Fey. Her methodical research on the Estarans revealed:
- Their outworld origin
- Their vulnerability to necrotic energy
- Their use of Radiant Projectors for cross-world transit
- The escalating pattern of their incursions
- That current defensive strategies only achieve temporary withdrawal

This research laid the foundation for her transformation into the antithesis of Estaran order.

## Key Documents Recovered

The party takes four primary source documents from the observatory:

1. **"Systematic Testing of Energetic Applications to Estaran Artifacts"** - Proves necrotic energy uniquely disrupts Estaran materials
2. **Letter from Yrk to Milicent** - Theorizes about Radiant Projectors and suggests searching near Metgal
3. **"Evidence for Outworld Origin"** - Proves the Estarans did not evolve in this world and may be visiting many others
4. **"Analysis of Estaran Temporal Patterns"** - Shows the escalating cycle of incursions and dormancy periods

## Chapter Outcomes

The party successfully prevents Cetyri from disappearing deeper into the Feywild and convinces him to join their cause willingly. While he needs time to accept his role, he understands the necessity and has committed to working with them.

The party has secured an audience with Xilo, one of the seven Rebel Fey and a powerful potential ally against the Estarans. They've learned about Milicent's past as a scholar and the depth of her research on the Estaran threat.

The party has recovered critical intelligence from Milicent's observatory, including documents that reveal Estaran vulnerabilities and patterns.

The party has agreed to help Xilo slay Egedzoo in exchange for his alliance and safe passage back to the Material Plane. The assault on the dragon's lair will be the next challenge they face.

## What Comes Next

The party must prepare for the assault on Egedzoo's lair. Xilo will draw the hobgoblin army away, and the party will face the adult blue dragon in his canyon stronghold. Success will secure Xilo's alliance and allow them to return to the Material Plane with Cetyri, ready to continue building the coalition against the Estarans.

See **Chapter 6b: Death to Egedzoo** for the details of the assault.
