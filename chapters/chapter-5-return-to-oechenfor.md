# Chapter 5: Return to Oechenfor

## Overview

With the Trickster's Blessing protecting them from Milicent's gaze, the party returns from Naeliste to investigate the crisis in Oechenfor. On their journey through the Faedwood, centaurs warn them of bad omens over the city. They arrive to discover that promising young citizens—some very young—have been disappearing since the party left.

Through investigation, the party traces the disappearances to Gord Lankenmangled, the imprisoned dwarf merchant who has been crafting specialized tools from within his cell. These tools, made from star metal fragments matching those found at the Estaran tower in the Drekklands, are designed for quicklings to use in their abduction operations.

The party pursues the quicklings to their safehouse and captures the kidnapping tools. The next evening, instead of executing their plan, the quicklings and their leader Felicity burn their safehouse to lure the party back. When the party returns, they are pulled into a shadow projection once more.

After defeating Felicity's minions in the shadow space, the party is invited to go deeper rather than being returned. They accept and find themselves fully in the Shadowfell, standing face to face with Yrk and Milicent's honor guard. They are brought as guests to Milicent's fortress.

In her audience hall, Milicent treats the party with honor and respect, attempting to recruit them willingly. She brings them to a sealed chamber containing an imprisoned Estaran. Here, the party learns the horrifying truth: the Estarans are an existential threat to all existence who have destroyed the universe to save their empire 54 times before.

Milicent reveals the concept of the Dragon Singularity—a cosmic Great Wyrm that synthesizes from a dragon's many souls across realities into a single instance. These Unary Great Wyrms are what the Estarans fear most, and Milicent's plan is for Cetyri to become the Singularity and defeat the Estarans permanently.

Finally understanding Milicent's plan and their role, the party agrees to work with her on one condition: She must leave the mortals alone and put her hopes in their hands. She accepts and returns them safely to Oechenfor.

## Key Events

### 1. Return Through the Faedwood
The party travels back from Naeliste to Oechenfor, protected by the Trickster's Blessing. Along the way, they encounter centaurs who warn them of dark omens hanging over the city—a sign that something is deeply wrong.

### 2. The Missing Children
Upon arrival, the party seeks out Brother Alistair Thornfield at the town hall. He informs them that people with promise—particularly young people with exceptional talent or potential—have been disappearing since the party departed. The city is gripped by fear and confusion.

### 3. Investigation
The party uses a variety of skills, spells, and resources to search the town for clues about the disappearances. Their investigation eventually leads them to an unlikely source.

### 4. Gord Lankenmangled's Betrayal
Following the trail, the party discovers that Gord Lankenmangled—the dwarf merchant imprisoned for keeping artisans in indentured servitude—has been working from within his cell. He has been crafting specialized tools from star metal fragments, the same material they discovered at the Estaran tower in the Drekklands. These tools are designed for quicklings to wield in their kidnapping operations.

### 5. The Quickling Safehouse
The party tracks the quicklings to their safehouse and raids it, managing to capture the star metal tools before they can be used to abduct more victims. This appears to disrupt Milicent's operation in Oechenfor.

### 6. The Fire Trap
The following evening, instead of continuing their kidnapping plan, the quicklings and their leader Felicity set fire to their own safehouse. The blaze is designed to draw the party back to the location, and it succeeds.

### 7. Into Shadow Again
When the party returns to investigate the fire, they are once more pulled into a shadow projection. This time they face Felicity's minions in combat within the twisted shadow space.

### 8. The Invitation
After the party defeats Felicity's forces, something unexpected happens. Instead of being expelled back to the Material Plane, they sense an invitation—an offer to go deeper into the shadow. Understanding this may be their chance to finally confront Milicent, the party accepts.

### 9. The Shadowfell
The party fully transitions into the Shadowfell and finds themselves standing before Yrk the Trickster and members of Milicent's honor guard. Rather than enemies, they are treated as expected guests. Yrk has arranged the meeting as promised.

### 10. Milicent's Fortress
The party is escorted to Milicent's mountain fortress, where the Shadow Projector is housed. They are treated with honor and respect—not as captives, but as potential allies. Milicent is attempting to recruit them willingly, believing they have the strength and potential to help her cause.

### 11. The Imprisoned Estaran
Milicent brings the party to a sealed chamber deep in her fortress. Within is an imprisoned Estaran—one of the ancient enemy. This is the first time the party has seen one of these beings in person. Through this captive, Milicent demonstrates the true threat the Estarans represent.

### 12. The Truth of 54 Worlds
The Estaran reveals or Milicent explains the horrifying truth: the Estarans have destroyed and recreated the Material Plane 54 times before. Each time a rebellion threatened their empire or a Dragon Singularity emerged to challenge them, they enacted their ultimate contingency—annihilation of the entire universe, followed by reconstruction under their control. The current world is the 55th iteration, and the Estarans are preparing to do it again.

### 13. The Dragon Singularity
Milicent explains the concept that the party has been learning about throughout their journey. A Dragon Singularity—or Unary Great Wyrm—occurs when a dragon successfully unifies all versions of their soul across multiple realities into a single, impossibly powerful being. This is what the Testament of Embers described when Varkhasia the red dragon nearly destroyed the Estaran Empire. This is what Lomani Nae discovered in Cetyri's soul—the capacity to reach across realities.

Milicent reveals that this has been her plan all along: to guide Cetyri to become the Dragon Singularity and use that cosmic power to permanently defeat the Estarans before they can complete their 55th apocalypse.

### 14. The Alliance
Understanding the stakes and Milicent's true purpose, the party faces a choice. Milicent's methods—enslaving mortals through the Shadow Projector to build the Dread Legion—are abhorrent, but her cause is the survival of all existence. The party agrees to work with her, but on one critical condition: she must leave the mortals alone. No more kidnappings, no more conscription. She must put her hopes entirely in the party's hands.

Milicent considers this carefully. She has spent centuries building her army, and now these mortals ask her to abandon that work and trust them instead. After a tense moment, she agrees.

### 15. Return to Oechenfor
Milicent keeps her word and safely returns the party to Oechenfor. The disappearances will stop. The party now carries the weight of their alliance—they have promised to help stop an existential threat, and they must find Cetyri and help him become what Milicent believes he must be.

## Major NPCs

### Brother Alistair Thornfield
Cleric of Oechenfor who informs the party of the disappearances. He has been trying to maintain order and calm in the city while people with promise vanish without explanation.

### Gord Lankenmangled
Imprisoned dwarf merchant and owner of Lankmangled Arms. Despite his incarceration, he has been crafting specialized star metal tools from within his cell. These tools are designed for quicklings to use in kidnapping operations. His motivation for helping Milicent remains unclear—whether coerced, promised freedom, or motivated by revenge against those who imprisoned him (particularly Cormin).

### Felicity
Leader of the quickling operation in Oechenfor. Commands the minions who have been abducting promising citizens. Sets the fire trap that draws the party into the final shadow projection.

### Quicklings
Fey servants of Milicent who have been conducting the kidnapping operations. Fast, clever, and loyal to the Shadowdame. They use the star metal tools crafted by Gord to capture their targets.

### Yrk the Trickster
Ancient goblin who arranged the meeting with Milicent as he promised. Appears in the Shadowfell to greet the party and escort them to Milicent's fortress, demonstrating that despite his past with her, he maintains some form of relationship or agreement.

### Milicent's Honor Guard
Elite warriors who serve the Shadowdame directly. They escort the party through the fortress and demonstrate the military might Milicent has assembled.

### Milicent (The Shadowdame)
Finally encountered directly for the first time. One of the seven original Rebel Fey, former patron to Erol, creator of the Shadow Projector. She treats the party with respect and attempts to recruit them to her cause willingly. She reveals the truth about the Estarans' repeated apocalypses and her plan to create a Dragon Singularity from Cetyri. She agrees to stop enslaving mortals in exchange for the party's alliance.

### The Imprisoned Estaran
A captive member of the ancient enemy, held in a sealed chamber in Milicent's fortress. Through this being, the party learns the true nature of the Estaran threat and witnesses firsthand what Milicent has been fighting against.

## Locations

### The Faedwood
Ancient forest the party must travel through to reach Oechenfor from Naeliste. Home to centaurs who serve as harbingers of the trouble in the city.

### Oechenfor
The party's home base, now gripped by fear due to the mysterious disappearances. The city's atmosphere has darkened considerably since they left.

### Town Hall
Where Brother Thornfield briefs the party on the crisis affecting the city.

### Oechenfor Prison
Where Gord Lankenmangled is incarcerated, yet somehow continues to craft tools for Milicent's agents. The security or complicity of the prison becomes a question.

### The Quickling Safehouse
Hidden location in or near Oechenfor where Felicity and her quicklings operate. Eventually burned by the quicklings themselves to lure the party into a trap.

### Milicent's Mountain Fortress (Shadowfell)
The party's first direct visit to the Shadowdame's seat of power. A massive fortress in the Shadowfell where the Shadow Projector is housed. Contains audience halls, sealed chambers, and quarters for Milicent's growing army. Demonstrates the scale of her operation.

### The Sealed Chamber
Deep within the fortress, where Milicent keeps an imprisoned Estaran. A place of revelation where the party learns the truth about the enemy they face.

## Key Revelations

### The Estaran Apocalypse Cycle
The Estarans have destroyed and recreated the Material Plane 54 times before. Each time their empire was threatened—by rebellion or by the emergence of a Dragon Singularity—they enacted their ultimate contingency: complete annihilation of the universe, followed by reconstruction under their absolute control. The current Material Plane is the 55th iteration. This explains:
- The Aboleth's question about "how many moments" the world has existed
- The fragmented memories of veterans across the Allied Cities
- Why the Testament of Embers speaks of Varkhasia destroying the Estara, yet they still exist
- The true stakes of the conflict—failure means literal universal extinction

### Milicent's True Purpose
Milicent's actions—the Shadow Projector, the Dread Legion, the kidnappings—are all part of a desperate plan to prevent the 55th apocalypse. She is racing against time to build a force capable of permanently defeating the Estarans before they complete their next doomsday weapon. Her methods are monstrous, but her cause is the survival of all existence.

### The Dragon Singularity Plan
Milicent's ultimate strategy involves Cetyri. She has orchestrated events to guide him toward becoming a Unary Great Wyrm—a Dragon Singularity who unifies all versions of his soul across all realities into one impossibly powerful being. This is what the Estarans fear most, as Varkhasia nearly destroyed them before they annihilated his universe. This explains:
- Why Milicent kept Cetyri prisoner for a century
- Why she tortured him (possibly to damage his soul in the specific way needed)
- Why Lomani Nae found his soul reaching across realities
- Why Erol spoke of "Cetyri's choice"
- The true purpose behind everything that has happened to the blind gold dragon

### Star Metal Connection
The fragments found at the Estaran tower in the Drekklands are the same material Gord Lankenmangled has been using to craft tools. This suggests:
- Milicent has access to or has scavenged Estaran materials
- The star metal has specific properties useful for planar manipulation
- There may be more Estaran ruins scattered across the world

### Yrk's Continued Role
Despite fleeing from Milicent centuries ago when she began enslaving mortals, Yrk maintains enough of a relationship with her to arrange meetings and apparently move freely in her realm. This suggests their break was not absolute, and Yrk may have conflicted feelings about her methods versus her cause.

### Gord's Complicity
The imprisoned merchant has been working for Milicent from within his cell, either coerced, bribed with promises of freedom, or motivated by revenge. This raises questions about security in Oechenfor and who else might be compromised.

## Chapter Outcomes

The party successfully stops the kidnappings in Oechenfor by capturing the star metal tools and confronting the quickling operation. However, this was always going to lead to a meeting with Milicent—Yrk had arranged it, and the party's investigation served to prove their capability.

The party meets Milicent face to face for the first time and learns the full truth about the Estaran threat. They understand the stakes: the survival of all existence hangs in the balance. The Estarans have destroyed the universe 54 times before and are preparing to do it again.

The party forms an alliance with Milicent, but on their terms. They convince her to abandon her practice of enslaving mortals through the Shadow Projector. In exchange, she must trust them to help execute her plan. This represents a massive shift—Milicent has spent centuries building the Dread Legion, and now she must put her hopes in a small group of mortals instead.

The party returns to Oechenfor safely with new purpose and terrible knowledge. They must now find Cetyri and Bronik (who fled into the Feywild in Chapter 3) and help the blind gold dragon understand and accept his potential to become the Dragon Singularity. The fate of the 55th universe—and all future universes—may depend on their success.

The disappearances in Oechenfor will cease. Gord Lankenmangled's role will need to be addressed. And the party must decide how much of this truth to share with the people of Oechenfor, the Allied Cities, and their allies. The weight of cosmic knowledge now rests on their shoulders.
