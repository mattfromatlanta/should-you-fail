# Chapter 7: Story Recap

The party secured a major alliance in the Feywild. After convincing Cetyri to stop running and accept his role in the coming war, they assaulted the canyon stronghold of Egedzoo — an adult blue dragon building power in the wasteland. With Xilo's forces drawing away the hobgoblin army, the party infiltrated and killed Egedzoo decisively. Xilo, Lord of Beasts, pledged himself as envoy of the Rebel Fey and now travels with the party.

The group has returned to the Material Plane with a formidable entourage: Cetyri, Bronik, Xilo, and Hootini. Their destination is Aris, capital of the Allied Cities — and the city is in crisis. Chief Exarch Maelric fell into a catatonic trance two months ago after triggering an Estaran psychic trap. Gallifax keeps vigil over him in the Hall of Allies, trusting no one.

Two factions have filled the power vacuum: the Veterans, who want a Council of Regency, and the Young Bastards, who demand new leadership. Aldrin and Erol are in Aris trying to hold things together. Yrk and Felicity are en route with a breakthrough device capable of crossing worlds — and they're pushing the party to leave.

## Updates and Corrections

### Corrections

_Review these against the knowledge base and either revise or update as needed..._

Xilo is the Lord of Robins. He has the personality of a Robin and the energy of a song bird. He should be referred to as Lord of Robins, Xilo, or the Rebel Fey. He is not the leader of the Rebel Fey. They have no leader.

Xilo is Lord of Robins and Twi'Xu is Lieutenant of Bluebirds. They are so titled because they *are* those things, though they each have a humanoid and hybrid form. Note that this is what Animal Lords are like. Not all of the seven Rebel Fey are Animal Lords, though most are.

Better to say he volunteered himself to travel with the party than to say he pledged anything. Fey are immortal and tend not to make unbreakable promises.

There is no such thing as a Council of Regency. The Veterans don't want a king or royalty any more than anyone else. The problem is they live long due to Estaran magic and have been the dominant political class for 150 years since the 2nd Rebellion. Whole generations have lived and died without ever inheriting political power. The Young Bastards are the loosely affiliated activisists and leaders who argue that this has to change. The name Young Bastards, by the way, is a little bit ironic. Many of them are quite old by mortal standards. They just don't have unnatural long life and by comparison to Veterans, they are young.

### Updates

*This is chapter 7 so far in the last session.*

The party arrived in Aris after a month on the road. They stopped in Naeliste and they stopped in Oechenfor on the way. Xilo, Bronik, and Cytiri are with them. Twi'Xu was sent forward to make arrangements, which he did

They met Twi'Xu on arrival, who led them into the city in the High Ward. Many guest houses for dignitaries are available here.

On their way to town, they witnessed a near fight between a group of protesting Young Bastards and a trio of veterans. If the fight had happened, the veterans would likely have killed protestors. However, the party intervened and a fight was avoided.

The first morning they met Aldrin and were introduced to Ardo. They got a sense of how things have been going and what's happening with Gallifax. They heard about Nava for the first time, though they haven't met her.

They went to visit Erol as well, and got her perspective on things. She explained how hard loss is for the longer lived creatures like Gallifax. She gave her view that the Young Bastards have a valid complaint and that the Veterans are too hidebound.

Following this, the party--especially Cormin--went out to find the protestors they saved yesterday and collect information about the mood of the town. They picked up the rumor that something big is planned for the waterfront that evening and not to believe their worst fears.

Armed with this knowledge, they went to the waterfront's enormous amphitheatre. They did everything they could to hide themselves so they could observe without interfering. To their surprise, no crowd of activists gathered. Instead, as the sun set, a hundred radiant beams descended from the heavens. Each carried an Estaran warrior, sword aflame, descending on Aris. Alarm bells wrang out around the city.
