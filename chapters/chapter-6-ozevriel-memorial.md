# Chapter 6: The Memorial of Ozevriel

## Overview

After securing Milicent's agreement to cease her mortal conscription, the party returns to Naeliste to convince Cetyri to join their cause. However, they discover that Cetyri has fled to the Feywild after Lomani's questioning revealed her knowledge of dragon multiplicity. With Bronik accompanying him out of friendship, Cetyri has run to protect the secret of his multiverse-spanning nature.

The party pursues them into the Feywild, where they find themselves in a scorched wasteland—the outer regions devastated by ancient Estaran influence. After encounters with hobgoblin bandits and a tense meeting with Twi'Xu (Animal Lord and lieutenant to Xilo, one of the Rebel Fey), they track Cetyri and Bronik to the settlement of Qu.

Through honest conversation, the party convinces Cetyri to stop running and accept his potential role in Milicent's plan. Not wanting to miss the opportunity to gain Xilo as an ally, the party commissions an airship journey deeper into the Feywild's eternal twilight forest. However, they are attacked mid-flight by hobgoblin sky-riders and Egedzoo, the adult blue dragon they encountered at Rinick's Diner. Though they repel the attack, their airship is damaged and crashes near a mysterious tower structure.

Now grounded in the heart of the Feywild, the party discovers the tower is the memorial tomb of Ozevriel—a hero slain in the first rebellion against the Estarans. Once a pilgrimage site, it has fallen into disrepair and been largely forgotten. The party must explore the memorial as they wait for repairs and seek passage to meet with Xilo.

## Key Events

### 1. Discovery of Cetyri's Flight
Upon returning to Naeliste after their alliance with Milicent, the party discovers that Cetyri has fled. Through conversation with Lomani Nae, they learn that her careful questioning about dragon multiplicity spooked the young gold dragon. Cetyri realized that Lomani had discovered the truth about his soul reaching across realities, and he ran to keep that knowledge from spreading. Bronik, Erol's half-dragon son, accompanied him out of simple friendship and loyalty.

### 2. Pursuit Into the Feywild
The party is only a day or so behind Cetyri and Bronik. They immediately set out in pursuit, crossing into the Feywild via one of the hidden paths through the Faedwood.

### 3. The Scorched Wasteland
The party emerges into a barren, desolate landscape cloaked in perpetual twilight. The outer regions of the Feywild—those closest to the Material Plane—were scorched and ruined during the Estaran occupation. There is a fundamental incompatibility between the wild, chaotic nature of the Fey world and the intensely ordered, geometric nature of Estaran reality. The clash left these borderlands dead.

The landscape is rocky desert dominated by enormous thorn vines the size of mountain ranges, visible on the horizon to the north and east—monuments to the violence done to this realm.

### 4. Hobgoblin Bandit Attack
Shortly after arriving, the party is attacked by hobgoblin bandits who operate in the wasteland. The party successfully repels them and scouts the area from the air, discovering what appears to be a structure a mile away along a ride leading northeast.

### 5. Rinick's Diner
The structure proves to be a roadside watering hole called Rinick's Diner—a surprisingly civilized establishment in the middle of the wasteland. Rinick, a human originally from the Allied Cities, has created a thriving neutral-ground business. All bandit factions and travelers respect the diner as a safe zone where violence is forbidden.

### 6. Twi'Xu and the Blue Dragon
Upon entering the diner, the party stumbles into a tense standoff. Two solitary patrons are watching each other with dangerous intensity. Aelira uses her Robe of Eyes to perceive their true forms:

- **Twi'Xu**, Animal Lord and Lieutenant of Bluebirds, sworn vassal to Xilo, Lord of Robins and one of the seven Rebel Fey.

- **Egedzoo**, an adult blue dragon seeking to establish territory in the wasteland and unite the hobgoblin bandits under his rule

Twi'Xu is observing the dragon on Xilo's orders, but neither wants to fight in Rinick's neutral territory. Egedzoo departs quietly to avoid direct confrontation with an Animal Lord.

### 7. Arranging Audience with Xilo
The party engages Twi'Xu in conversation and explains their search for Cetyri and Bronik. Twi'Xu confirms the two passed through heading to Qu and are likely already there. When the party asks for an audience with Xilo, Twi'Xu reveals he is close enough to Xilo's inner council to know that the Lord of Robins will want to meet anyone working with Milicent. All the Rebel Fey understand that without Milicent's drastic measures during the rebellion, the Estarans would never have been driven away.

Twi'Xu agrees to help arrange a meeting but cannot leave his post—doing so would give Egedzoo too much freedom to operate without surveillance.

### 8. Journey to Qu
The party continues southwest to the settlement of Qu, a rougher town on the edge of the wasteland. They encounter local robbers after arrival but handle them without serious trouble.

### 9. Confronting Cetyri
Using magic to locate them, the party finds Cetyri and Bronik just as they are preparing to leave Qu and travel deeper into the Feywild. The party arranges a heart-to-heart conversation and lays everything bare: Milicent's plan, the depths of the Estaran threat and their 54 previous apocalypses, the Dragon Singularity, and the critical role Cetyri can play in saving all existence.

Cetyri is overwhelmed but understands the stakes. He agrees not to run anymore. While he needs time to become comfortable with his role and the risks involved, he recognizes that joining the cause is the only path forward.

### 10. Commissioning the Airship
Not wanting to miss the opportunity to gain Xilo as a powerful ally—and hoping to corroborate what they've learned about the Estaran threat—the party commissions Captain Marvo and his airship to take them deeper into the Feywild, toward the eternal twilight forest at the continent's heart.

### 11. Aerial Attack
As the airship pushes north into the primal Feywild, it is attacked mid-flight by hobgoblin bandits riding wyverns, led by Egedzoo himself. The party successfully repels the attack and drives off the dragon without destroying him entirely. However, the airship's engine sustains critical damage during the battle.

### 12. Emergency Landing
Captain Marvo is nearly killed but manages to survive as the damaged airship makes an emergency landing. The party finds themselves grounded near a mysterious tower structure rising from the eternal twilight forest.

### 13. Discovering the Memorial
With their airship requiring repairs and unable to continue, the party sets out to explore the nearby tower. They soon realize this is no ordinary structure—it is the memorial tomb of Ozevriel, a hero slain during the first rebellion against the Estarans.

Once a pilgrimage site revered throughout Fey history, the memorial has fallen into disrepair over the centuries. Now only a few remember its existence or significance. The party prepares to explore the forgotten tomb.

## Key Revelations

### Lomani's Discovery
Lomani Nae has successfully uncovered the truth about Cetyri's multiverse-spanning soul through careful scholarly investigation. Her questions revealed she understood the implications of dragon multiplicity and Cetyri's unique nature.

### Cetyri's Understanding
After the party's honest explanation, Cetyri now understands:
- The 54 previous apocalypses enacted by the Estarans
- The concept of the Dragon Singularity and what it means
- His potential to become a Unary Great Wyrm
- The existential stakes of refusing to participate
- That running is futile when all of existence hangs in the balance

While frightened and needing time to process, he has committed to the cause.

### The Wasteland's Origin
The scorched outer regions of the Feywild are a scar from the Estaran occupation. The fundamental incompatibility between Estaran order and Fey chaos left these borderlands dead when the two forces clashed. This demonstrates the reality-level damage the Estarans can inflict simply through their presence.

### Xilo's Position
Xilo and the other Rebel Fey understand that Milicent's extreme measures during the rebellion were necessary to drive away the Estarans. Despite any disagreements over her current methods, they recognize the debt they owe her and will be open to meeting her allies.

### Ozevriel's Significance
The memorial tower is dedicated to Ozevriel, who died in the first rebellion against the Estarans. This suggests there were heroes and martyrs in that ancient conflict whose stories have been largely forgotten over the millennia.

## Chapter Outcomes

The party successfully prevents Cetyri from disappearing deeper into the Feywild and convinces him to join their cause willingly. While he needs time to accept his role, he understands the necessity and has committed to working with them.

The party has made contact with Twi'Xu and begun the process of securing an audience with Xilo, one of the seven Rebel Fey and a powerful potential ally against the Estarans.

The party's airship has been damaged in combat with Egedzoo and is grounded near the Memorial of Ozevriel. While waiting for repairs, they must explore the ancient tomb and navigate whatever challenges or inhabitants they find within.

The stage is set for the party to meet directly with Xilo and potentially gain the support of another ancient power in their fight against the Estarans.

## UPCOMING ENCOUNTERS: The Memorial of Ozevriel

The following encounters are planned but need detailed development. Each section includes design questions to help flesh out the encounter.

### Encounter 1: A Locked Curse

**Location**: The antechamber of Ozevriel's tomb

#### Background
The antechamber was once used as a place to hold periodic memorial ceremonies for Ozevriel. In its undisturbed state, this was a large, clean space with minimal adornment that could hold about 100 medium creatures comfortably. The site was quietly protected by its constructors with a powerful arcane trap to stop raiders and thieves. Anyone who entered the space with (unmasked) ill intentions would be subject to psychic assault and could be trapped in eternal confusion and madness.

About 50 years ago, a cluster of demons and slaves attempted to raid the tomb and sprung the trap. The trap has a personality and an emotional state but it does not communicate directly with creatures. Its purpose is to protect the tomb and only let in those who approach with appropriate reverence.

#### Goal
The characters must unspring the trap to make their way deeper into the tomb.

#### What the Party Sees
The space is fragmented in an illogical way. Light has refracted and it is difficult to make out the underlying physical structure of the space through rainbow rays and fey mist. It is equally difficult to make out that there are creatures trapped inside.

#### Learning About the Trap
The party can attempt the following actions to learn about the trap until they roll **3 failures total** or manage to learn everything they can.

**Search (DC 20)** to:
- Assess the original structure and understand the space
- Reveal the presence of creatures in the mist
- Intuit that the psychic disposition of creatures entering the antechamber can set or unset the trap

**Study (DC 20)** to:
- Understand physical mechanisms that can be manipulated to try to reset the machine
- Recognize the arcane fey nature of the trap, including the notion that it's intended to induce madness and trap trespassers
- Understand the kind of attitudes and ritual circumspection that could induce the trap to react positively
- Discover that the trap is sentient

**Influence (DC 20)** to:
- Improve the trap's disposition to the party
- Cause the trap to behave a certain way

#### Attempting to Reset the Trap
Once the party understands enough about the trap, they can attempt to reset it. They need **five successes or two failures** to resolve the challenge.

**Utilize (DC 15)** to:
- **(STR/DEX)** Leverage the mechanisms back into place
- **(INT/WIS)** Observe the trap's reactions and guide the party's effort
- **(CHA)** Entice and calm the trap and perform the appropriate rituals

#### Consequences
- **Five successes**: The trap releases properly. The trapped creatures are released but will be in a state of stupor and frightened for the coming fight.
- **Two failures**: The trap springs chaotically and expels a wave of terrifying psychic energy. All party members must make a **DC 15 Charisma save** or take **3d6 psychic damage**. The party will be frightened for one round to start the fight.

### Encounter 2: Set Free...

When the trap is released (successfully or not), the creatures trapped within are freed and immediately attack the party.

#### Enemies
The raiding party that was trapped 50 years ago consisted of:
- **2 [Arcanaloths](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/5194899-arcanaloth)** - Jackal-headed fiends who serve as sorcerers and manipulators
- **5 [Dire Worgs](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/5194969-dire-worg)** - Enormous evil wolves

#### Tactics
- **Dire Worgs** will attempt to get behind fighters and harry spellcasters, using their mobility to disrupt the party's formation
- **Arcanaloths** will look for their best opportunities to cause mayhem with their spellcasting and cunning

#### Starting Conditions
- **If the party succeeded at unspringing the trap**: The demons and worgs are released in a state of stupor and will be **frightened** for the duration of the combat
- **If the party failed**: The party members who failed their saves will be **frightened** for the first round of combat

### Encounter 3: The Memorial Complex

The Memorial of Ozevriel consists of an above-ground tower and underground tomb complex, situated in the dense eternal twilight forest of the Feywild.

#### Architecture and Condition

The entire complex displays classic Fey architecture: carved stone adorned with glazed ceramic depicting natural motifs. While there aren't obvious patterns to Fey design, certain tendencies and repeated gestures emerge throughout. The color schemes are quite bright, intended to show even under twilight conditions or by firelight. Walking through a Fey structure with a light source causes many colors to reflect onto the lightbearer, creating a prismatic effect.

The entire complex has been neglected for at least a century, showing signs of abandonment and the forest's gradual reclamation.

#### Area 1: The Stone Patio

The party first encounters a stone patio at ground level. A stone staircase descends from here, leading underground to a tunnel entrance at least 50 feet below the surface.

#### Area 2: The Underground Tunnel & Antechamber

The tunnel extends 30 feet before opening into the antechamber—a quite large space that once held periodic memorial ceremonies for Ozevriel. In its undisturbed state, this chamber could comfortably hold about 100 medium creatures with minimal adornment. The antechamber has seen the worst decay of the entire facility and giant fissures split the room and open to the surrounding earth.

**This is where Encounters 1 and 2 take place.**

#### Area 3: Ozevriel's Tomb

At the end of the antechamber stands a great archway, 20 feet across and 20 feet tall. Beyond lies a smaller chamber containing Ozevriel's final resting place.

The hero himself is permanently preserved within a great crystal, stood upright as an eternal sentinel. Permanent magical lights hover in the corners of the room, providing enough illumination to see clearly while maintaining a somber mood.

Ozevriel's verse of the **Ode to Seven Lords** is inscribed on the stone platform where he stands—a testament to his role in the first rebellion.

#### Area 4: The Spiral Staircase & Observatory

Behind Ozevriel's crystal tomb, the base of a spiral staircase rises upward. The staircase leads up through the tower to its pinnacle.

At the top, Milicent herself built an observatory of the stars and moon in ages past. This is where the party will encounter Hootini, who awaits them on behalf of Xilo.

---

### Encounter 4: The Observatory

At the top of the spiral staircase, the party emerges into Milicent's observatory—a structure she built in ages past to study the stars and moon. This pinnacle chamber offers a commanding view of the eternal twilight forest stretching in all directions.

#### Meeting Hootini

Here the party finds **Hootini**, Aelira's fey companion, who has been waiting for them. Hootini greets them on behalf of **Xilo, Lord of Robins**, and delivers the news: Xilo has arrived at their airship and awaits them there.

Hootini can answer questions about:
- How Xilo learned of their arrival at the memorial
- Twi'Xu's message and Xilo's interest in meeting Milicent's allies
- The significance of Ozevriel's memorial and its connection to Milicent
- The current state of the Feywild and the various powers at play

Once the party is ready, Hootini can guide them back to the airship where the true meeting with Xilo will take place.
