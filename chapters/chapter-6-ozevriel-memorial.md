# Chapter 6: The Memorial of Ozevriel

## Overview

After securing Milicent's agreement to cease her mortal conscription, the party returns to Naeliste to convince Cetyri to join their cause. However, they discover that Cetyri has fled to the Feywild after Lomani's questioning revealed her knowledge of dragon multiplicity. With Bronik accompanying him out of friendship, Cetyri has run to protect the secret of his multiverse-spanning nature.

The party pursues them into the Feywild, where they find themselves in a scorched wasteland—the outer regions devastated by ancient Estaran influence. After encounters with hobgoblin bandits and a tense meeting with Twi'Xu (Animal Lord and lieutenant to Xilo, one of the Rebel Fey), they track Cetyri and Bronik to the settlement of Qu.

Through honest conversation, the party convinces Cetyri to stop running and accept his potential role in Milicent's plan. Not wanting to miss the opportunity to gain Xilo as an ally, the party commissions an airship journey deeper into the Feywild's eternal twilight forest. However, they are attacked mid-flight by hobgoblin sky-riders and Egedzoo, the adult blue dragon they encountered at Rinick's Diner. Though they repel the attack, their airship is damaged and crashes near a mysterious tower structure.

Now grounded in the heart of the Feywild, the party discovers the tower is the memorial tomb of Ozevriel—a hero slain in the first rebellion against the Estarans. Once a pilgrimage site, it has fallen into disrepair and been largely forgotten. The party must explore the memorial as they wait for repairs and seek passage to meet with Xilo.

## Key Events

### 1. Discovery of Cetyri's Flight
Upon returning to Naeliste after their alliance with Milicent, the party discovers that Cetyri has fled. Through conversation with Lomani Nae, they learn that her careful questioning about dragon multiplicity spooked the young gold dragon. Cetyri realized that Lomani had discovered the truth about his soul reaching across realities, and he ran to keep that knowledge from spreading. Bronik, Erol's half-dragon son, accompanied him out of simple friendship and loyalty.

### 2. Pursuit Into the Feywild
The party is only a day or so behind Cetyri and Bronik. They immediately set out in pursuit, crossing into the Feywild via one of the hidden paths through the Faedwood.

### 3. The Scorched Wasteland
The party emerges into a barren, desolate landscape cloaked in perpetual twilight. The outer regions of the Feywild—those closest to the Material Plane—were scorched and ruined during the Estaran occupation. There is a fundamental incompatibility between the wild, chaotic nature of the Fey world and the intensely ordered, geometric nature of Estaran reality. The clash left these borderlands dead.

The landscape is rocky desert dominated by enormous thorn vines the size of mountain ranges, visible on the horizon to the north and east—monuments to the violence done to this realm.

### 4. Hobgoblin Bandit Attack
Shortly after arriving, the party is attacked by hobgoblin bandits who operate in the wasteland. The party successfully repels them and scouts the area from the air, discovering what appears to be a structure a mile away along a ride leading northeast.

### 5. Rinick's Diner
The structure proves to be a roadside watering hole called Rinick's Diner—a surprisingly civilized establishment in the middle of the wasteland. Rinick, a human originally from the Allied Cities, has created a thriving neutral-ground business. All bandit factions and travelers respect the diner as a safe zone where violence is forbidden.

### 6. Twi'Xu and the Blue Dragon
Upon entering the diner, the party stumbles into a tense standoff. Two solitary patrons are watching each other with dangerous intensity. Aelira uses her Robe of Eyes to perceive their true forms:

- **Twi'Xu**, Animal Lord and Lieutenant of Bluebirds, sworn vassal to Xilo, Lord of Robins and one of the seven Rebel Fey.

- **Egedzoo**, an adult blue dragon seeking to establish territory in the wasteland and unite the hobgoblin bandits under his rule

Twi'Xu is observing the dragon on Xilo's orders, but neither wants to fight in Rinick's neutral territory. Egedzoo departs quietly to avoid direct confrontation with an Animal Lord.

### 7. Arranging Audience with Xilo
The party engages Twi'Xu in conversation and explains their search for Cetyri and Bronik. Twi'Xu confirms the two passed through heading to Qu and are likely already there. When the party asks for an audience with Xilo, Twi'Xu reveals he is close enough to Xilo's inner council to know that the Lord of Robins will want to meet anyone working with Milicent. All the Rebel Fey understand that without Milicent's drastic measures during the rebellion, the Estarans would never have been driven away.

Twi'Xu agrees to help arrange a meeting but cannot leave his post—doing so would give Egedzoo too much freedom to operate without surveillance.

### 8. Journey to Qu
The party continues southwest to the settlement of Qu, a rougher town on the edge of the wasteland. They encounter local robbers after arrival but handle them without serious trouble.

### 9. Confronting Cetyri
Using magic to locate them, the party finds Cetyri and Bronik just as they are preparing to leave Qu and travel deeper into the Feywild. The party arranges a heart-to-heart conversation and lays everything bare: Milicent's plan, the depths of the Estaran threat and their 54 previous apocalypses, the Dragon Singularity, and the critical role Cetyri can play in saving all existence.

Cetyri is overwhelmed but understands the stakes. He agrees not to run anymore. While he needs time to become comfortable with his role and the risks involved, he recognizes that joining the cause is the only path forward.

### 10. Commissioning the Airship
Not wanting to miss the opportunity to gain Xilo as a powerful ally—and hoping to corroborate what they've learned about the Estaran threat—the party commissions Captain Marvo and his airship to take them deeper into the Feywild, toward the eternal twilight forest at the continent's heart.

### 11. Aerial Attack
As the airship pushes north into the primal Feywild, it is attacked mid-flight by hobgoblin bandits riding wyverns, led by Egedzoo himself. The party successfully repels the attack and drives off the dragon without destroying him entirely. However, the airship's engine sustains critical damage during the battle.

### 12. Emergency Landing
Captain Marvo is nearly killed but manages to survive as the damaged airship makes an emergency landing. The party finds themselves grounded near a mysterious tower structure rising from the eternal twilight forest.

### 13. Discovering the Memorial
With their airship requiring repairs and unable to continue, the party sets out to explore the nearby tower. They soon realize this is no ordinary structure—it is the memorial tomb of Ozevriel, a hero slain during the first rebellion against the Estarans.

Once a pilgrimage site revered throughout Fey history, the memorial has fallen into disrepair over the centuries. Now only a few remember its existence or significance. The party prepares to explore the forgotten tomb.

## UPCOMING ENCOUNTERS: The Memorial of Ozevriel

The following encounters are planned but need detailed development. Each section includes design questions to help flesh out the encounter.

### Encounter 1: A Locked Curse

**Location**: The antechamber of Ozevriel's tomb

#### Background
The antechamber was once used as a place to hold periodic memorial ceremonies for Ozevriel. In its undisturbed state, this was a large, clean space with minimal adornment that could hold about 100 medium creatures comfortably. The site was quietly protected by its constructors with a powerful arcane trap to stop raiders and thieves. Anyone who entered the space with (unmasked) ill intentions would be subject to psychic assault and could be trapped in eternal confusion and madness.

About 50 years ago, a cluster of demons and slaves attempted to raid the tomb and sprung the trap. The trap has a personality and an emotional state but it does not communicate directly with creatures. Its purpose is to protect the tomb and only let in those who approach with appropriate reverence.

#### Goal
The characters must unspring the trap to make their way deeper into the tomb.

#### What the Party Sees
The space is fragmented in an illogical way. Light has refracted and it is difficult to make out the underlying physical structure of the space through rainbow rays and fey mist. It is equally difficult to make out that there are creatures trapped inside.

#### Learning About the Trap
The party can attempt the following actions to learn about the trap until they roll **3 failures total** or manage to learn everything they can.

**Search (DC 20)** to:
- Assess the original structure and understand the space
- Reveal the presence of creatures in the mist
- Intuit that the psychic disposition of creatures entering the antechamber can set or unset the trap

**Study (DC 20)** to:
- Understand physical mechanisms that can be manipulated to try to reset the machine
- Recognize the arcane fey nature of the trap, including the notion that it's intended to induce madness and trap trespassers
- Understand the kind of attitudes and ritual circumspection that could induce the trap to react positively
- Discover that the trap is sentient

**Influence (DC 20)** to:
- Improve the trap's disposition to the party
- Cause the trap to behave a certain way

#### Attempting to Reset the Trap
Once the party understands enough about the trap, they can attempt to reset it. They need **five successes or two failures** to resolve the challenge.

**Utilize (DC 15)** to:
- **(STR/DEX)** Leverage the mechanisms back into place
- **(INT/WIS)** Observe the trap's reactions and guide the party's effort
- **(CHA)** Entice and calm the trap and perform the appropriate rituals

#### Consequences
- **Five successes**: The trap releases properly. The trapped creatures are released but will be in a state of stupor and frightened for the coming fight.
- **Two failures**: The trap springs chaotically and expels a wave of terrifying psychic energy. All party members must make a **DC 15 Charisma save** or take **3d6 psychic damage**. The party will be frightened for one round to start the fight.

### Encounter 3: The Memorial Complex

The Memorial of Ozevriel consists of an above-ground tower and underground tomb complex, situated in the dense eternal twilight forest of the Feywild.

#### Architecture and Condition

The entire complex displays classic Fey architecture: carved stone adorned with glazed ceramic depicting natural motifs. While there aren't obvious patterns to Fey design, certain tendencies and repeated gestures emerge throughout. The color schemes are quite bright, intended to show even under twilight conditions or by firelight. Walking through a Fey structure with a light source causes many colors to reflect onto the lightbearer, creating a prismatic effect.

The entire complex has been neglected for at least a century, showing signs of abandonment and the forest's gradual reclamation.

#### Area 1: The Stone Patio

The party first encounters a stone patio at ground level. A stone staircase descends from here, leading underground to a tunnel entrance at least 50 feet below the surface.

#### Area 2: The Underground Tunnel & Antechamber

The tunnel extends 30 feet before opening into the antechamber—a quite large space that once held periodic memorial ceremonies for Ozevriel. In its undisturbed state, this chamber could comfortably hold about 100 medium creatures with minimal adornment. The antechamber has seen the worst decay of the entire facility and giant fissures split the room and open to the surrounding earth.

**This is where Encounters 1 and 2 take place.**

#### Area 3: Ozevriel's Tomb

At the end of the antechamber stands a great archway, 20 feet across and 20 feet tall. Beyond lies a smaller chamber containing Ozevriel's final resting place.

The hero himself is permanently preserved within a great crystal, stood upright as an eternal sentinel. Permanent magical lights hover in the corners of the room, providing enough illumination to see clearly while maintaining a somber mood.

Ozevriel's verse of the **Ode to Seven Lords** is inscribed on the stone platform where he stands—a testament to his role in the first rebellion.

#### Area 4: The Spiral Staircase & Observatory

Behind Ozevriel's crystal tomb, the base of a spiral staircase rises upward. The staircase leads up through the tower to its pinnacle.

At the top, Milicent herself built an observatory of the stars and moon in ages past. This is where the party will encounter Hootini, who awaits them on behalf of Xilo.

### Encounter 4: Milicent's Observatory

At the top of the spiral staircase, the party emerges into Milicent's observatory—a structure she built in ages past to study the stars and moon. This pinnacle chamber offers a commanding view of the eternal twilight forest stretching in all directions.

#### The Observatory Space

The observatory is a circular chamber approximately 40 feet in diameter with a domed ceiling that rises 30 feet at its peak. Large arched windows circle the entire room, each 8 feet tall and 4 feet wide, providing panoramic views of the Feywild's eternal twilight. The windows are made of exceptionally clear crystal that seems to amplify starlight.

The chamber is filled with the tools of astronomical study:
- **Star charts** covering the walls, depicting constellations unknown to Material Plane astronomy
- **Orrery devices** showing the movements of celestial bodies, some still slowly rotating
- **A great brass telescope** mounted on a swivel, pointed at the night sky
- **Moon phase diagrams** meticulously illustrated in vibrant inks
- **Tables covered with scrolls, journals, and calculation sheets**—decades of Milicent's research

Despite a century of neglect, the space remains remarkably preserved. The eternal twilight of the Feywild means no sun has beaten down on the materials, and the dry forest air has kept decay at bay. Dust covers everything, but the documents and instruments remain intact.

#### Meeting Hootini

As the party enters, they find **Hootini** examining the space with careful attention. He appears in his natural Fey form: a small humanoid creature standing about 3 feet tall, covered in soft brown and white feathers. His head is that of a great horned owl, with enormous golden eyes that reflect the starlight streaming through the windows. His hands are delicate but taloned, and he moves with an uncanny silent grace.

Hootini looks up as the party arrives, his head swiveling in that distinctive owl manner. "You've made it," he says, his voice a soft hooting tenor. "I sensed Aelira's arrival in the Feywild and have been searching for you. When I felt your presence here, at *this* place of all places..." He gestures around the observatory. "Do you know the significance of where you stand?"

#### Hootini's Background

If the party asks or shows interest, Hootini will explain:

**About himself**: "I am one of the Moon Fey—servants who once attended Milicent when she walked in twilight. When she departed, we were... lost. Scattered. I found solace in serving Aelira's family, but we remember our first liege."

**About Milicent**: "As I understand it, this was her sanctuary. Before she became the Shadow, Milicent was the most ordered mind among all the Fey—a scholar, a scientist, an astronomer. She studied the fixity of the stars and the precision of the moon's patterns. Her interest in order made her unique among Fey, and powerful."

**About Ozevrial** "He is the unifying hero of all Fey. We're a wild kind, you know. It takes a really singular story to bring us together. But we all honor his sacrifice. Milicent probably most of all. She brought him here to rest. I think it helped her to be reminded of the stakes..."

#### The Investigation

Hootini encourages the party to investigate the observatory thoroughly: "Xilo will want to meet with you, but he is still making his way here. We have some time if you want to look around..."

The investigation is structured as a **skill challenge** that tells the story of the party uncovering Milicent's research. The goal is not to gatekeep information but to create a narrative of discovery and synthesis.

**Time Required**: This investigation will take approximately **3-4 hours of in-game time**. The party should settle in, light lanterns, carefully examine documents, and piece together Milicent's findings.

**Skills Used**: Investigation, Arcana, Religion, Nature

#### Discovery Phase

During the first phase, the party searches through the observatory to find and catalog Milicent's research. Allow each character to make **one skill check** using any of the four skills. Set the DC at **12** to make success likely but not automatic.

For each success, reveal one of the following discoveries:

**Investigation Success**: "You find a series of star charts that map constellations across multiple planes of existence—not just the Material Plane and Feywild, but dozens of others. Milicent was tracking celestial patterns across the multiverse."

**Arcana Success**: "Among her papers, you discover detailed diagrams of a device labeled 'Radiant Projector.' The notes suggest it's a weapon designed to channel concentrated radiant energy. Milicent's annotations wonder: 'How do they actually function? What is the power source? Can one be repurposed?'"

**Religion Success**: "You find theological treatises comparing the Estarans to various divine entities. Milicent's conclusion is chilling: 'They are not gods. They do not require worship or belief. They exist as fundamental forces, like gravity or entropy. This makes them more dangerous, not less.'"

**Nature Success**: "Her notes on the Feywild's ecosystem include observations about how Estaran corruption affects natural systems. One passage stands out: 'The thorn vines persist even after the Estarans departed. The corruption doesn't heal naturally. It requires active intervention—transformation, not restoration.'"

For each failure, the character still finds relevant material, but it takes longer to understand: "You find promising documents but need more context to make sense of them. Set them aside for now."

#### Synthesis Phase

Once the party has made their initial discoveries (after everyone has rolled), move to the synthesis phase. This is where they piece together Milicent's most important findings.

Have the party make another round of checks, **DC 15 this time**. They need **3 total successes** (not per person) to fully understand Milicent's research. Allow creative approaches and reward players who connect what they've already found.

For each success, reveal one of these critical insights:

**First Success - Necrotic Vulnerability**: "You find detailed experimental notes testing different energy types against Estaran fragments. The results are unambiguous: necrotic energy causes structural degradation where nothing else works. Milicent's analysis suggests this is because necrotic energy represents entropy and decay—the philosophical opposite of Estaran order. This could be weaponized."

**Second Success - Cross-World Transit Theory**: "You discover correspondence between Milicent and someone named Yrk, discussing how the Estarans travel between worlds. They theorize the existence of devices that anchor Estaran reality in foreign worlds while projecting massive amounts of energy. The theory is compelling but lacks direct evidence—no such device has ever been captured intact."

**Third Success - Outworld Origin**: "Milicent's analysis proves the Estarans are not native to this world. Their biology, language, ecology, and technology are all fundamentally incompatible with natural development here. They come from somewhere else entirely. And if they can reach this world, they may be reaching others as well."

**Fourth Success (if needed) - Escalating Patterns**: "You find chronological analysis showing Estaran incursions follow a mathematical pattern: each incursion is stronger and longer than the last, each dormant period is shorter. Milicent calculated that within a few more cycles, the dormancy periods will disappear entirely and the Estarans will achieve permanent presence."

#### Primary Source Documents

After the party has investigated and synthesized the information, Hootini will help them identify **four key documents** they can take with them. These are brief excerpts that the party can reference later:

**Document 1: "Systematic Testing of Energetic Applications to Estaran Artifacts"**

"Trial Series 1,847-1,852 on fragment Est-7. Radiant energy: no effect. Divine magic: zero degradation. Elemental damage: 0.3% integrity loss, not viable. Necrotic energy: significant results—23% edge precision reduction, patterns destabilized. Sustained exposure caused 67% structural degradation. Analysis: necrotic energy uniquely disrupts Estaran material. Estaran affinity for  order appears essential, not preferential. Deathly energy presents a fundamental incompatibility—could be called an ontological contradiction. Question: How do we orient ourselves along this axis?"

**Document 2: Letter from Yrk to Milicent**

"Milicent, regarding cross-world transit: Estarans must possess some transit method. I think these are the necessary features: 

First, reality anchoring—continuous field locally overwriting natural law. 

Second, directed energy projection—likely radiant energy, geometrically ordered, focusable.

Third, a permanent or long-term installation. Assuming a targeted beam, movement at either end would break the connection.

My guess: devices projecting radiance through void, creating paths from their origin. Seems to me we could find one in the north, maybe near Metgal. That's where the Estarans arrived. Much could be explained if we find one intact. —Yrk"

**Document 3: "Evidence for Outworld Origin"**

"Estarans did not evolve here. They appear engineered, not evolved. 

Linguistic—inscriptions use alien mathematical notation with no correspondence to known systems. Ecological—no reproduction, consumption, or waste in three centuries. Not adapted to our reality. Their technology shows an uncanny uniformity--are they really so consistent? Could it be that they are more ancient than we realize? Critical question: if they cross to our world, how many others have they visited? I worry at the scale of what we are up against."

**Document 4: "Analysis of Estaran Temporal Patterns"**

"312-year analysis reveals cyclical behavior. Phase 1: 47 years, 23 sites. Dormancy: 89 years. Phase 2: 71 years, 67 sites. Dormancy: 52 years. Phase 3: 53 years ongoing, 140+ sites. Pattern: each incursion doubles intensity and lengthens duration; each dormancy shortens by 40%. Projection: next dormancy 31 years, following incursion 280+ sites lasting 80-100 years. Not random raids—systematic escalating pressure. Either testing defenses or incrementally transforming reality for permanent presence. Question: what would stop this escalation? Current strategies achieve only temporary withdrawal."

#### Hootini's Reaction

After the party has investigated and read these documents, Hootini will speak quietly: "This is how she worked. Pure analysis, methodical research. No emotion clouding the data." He gestures at the documents with one taloned hand. "But you can see the questions she was asking. The patterns she was tracking. She calculated what was coming—the escalation, the inevitability."

He looks at Aelira specifically. "Somewhere between these research notes and her departure for the Shadowfell, she found her answer to that final question: 'What would stop this escalation?' She decided *she* would. That is the mistress I once served. That is the mind that works in the Shadowfell even now."

#### Xilo's Arrival

After the investigation is complete, Hootini's head suddenly swivels toward one of the windows. "Xilo approaches. I can sense him. He is at your airship now, speaking with your crew."

Hootini stands. "We should go. The Lord of Robins is waiting, and you will want his alliance. He was Milicent's closest ally. The rumor is they are still in touch."

The party can now descend from the observatory and return to their airship, where Xilo awaits them.

### Encounter 5: Xilo, Lord of Robins

When the party returns to their grounded airship, they find it in better condition than when they left. Captain Marvo and his crew have made significant progress on repairs, and the mood is lighter. But the reason becomes immediately clear: perched on the ship's railing is a figure of unmistakable power and presence.

#### Xilo's Appearance

**Xilo, Lord of Robins** appears as a humanoid figure approximately 5 feet tall, with the bearing of a warrior-prince. His features are sharp and avian, with bright, intelligent eyes that miss nothing. Red-breasted feathers form a natural cloak across his shoulders and down his back, and his arms bear wing-like patterns of russet and brown feathers. Unlike Hootini's complete owl head, Xilo's face is humanoid but with distinctly bird-like qualities: a sharp nose like a beak, eyes that can focus with predatory intensity, and movements that are quick and precise.

He wears light armor made of interlocking Fey materials—something between leather and scale—and carries a rapier at his side that gleams with enchantment. Everything about him suggests quick wit, faster reflexes, and absolute confidence.

#### The Meeting

As the party approaches, Xilo hops down from the railing with effortless grace. "So you're the ones working with Milicent," he says, his voice clear and carrying a note of wry amusement. "Twi'Xu sent word. Said you were chasing a young dragon through the wasteland and asking for an audience with me. That alone would have piqued my interest. But then I learned you'd crashed near Ozevriel's memorial, of all places." He gestures toward the tower. "And now you've seen Milicent's observatory? What did you think?"

He studies each party member with those sharp eyes. "I'm Xilo. I was one of six who joined milicent Milicent to make seven. Back when most Fey thought she was being dramatic." His expression darkens slightly. "We've lost two since then. Ozevriel was one. Milicent went to the shadow and now we're five." He shakes his head. "But I'm not here to reminisce. You're working with her. You must understand what's at stake. So—what do you need from me?"

#### Xilo's Attitude

Xilo is **helpful by default**. He genuinely wants to assist the party because:
1. He respects anyone Milicent trusts enough to work with
2. He understands the Estaran threat is existential and requires alliance
3. He's pragmatic and sees opportunity in cooperation
4. He's curious about what Milicent is planning and wants to stay informed

However, he's also **quick-witted and direct**. He doesn't suffer fools, appreciates cleverness, and will point out poor logic or weak arguments. He's not rude, but he's not diplomatic either—he says what he thinks.

#### Information Xilo Can Share

The party can ask Xilo questions, and he will answer honestly. Some key topics:

**About Milicent Before the Shadow:**

"Milicent was... singular. Most Fey embrace chaos—we're creatures of whim and impulse, of seasonal mood and artistic passion. But Milicent was different. She studied the fixed patterns: stars, moons, tides, mathematical progressions. She found beauty in predictability, power in understanding cycles.

It made her the most brilliant mind among us. And it made her the first to recognize what the Estarans truly were. While others saw invaders, she saw an existential contradiction—beings of pure order trying to overwrite realities of organic chaos. She understood that compromise was impossible. Coexistence was impossible. It was victory or annihilation.

She and Ozevrial were the most powerful among the Rebel Fey. When Ozevrial fell, it tore at her. She realized raw power wasn't enough—we needed to become what the Estarans feared. And she worked it out. She transformed herself into the antithesis of their order."

**About Ozevrial's Death and Its Consequences:**

"Ozevrial's death weakened the Feywild fundamentally. He was a pillar—his presence stabilized the realm's chaotic nature, gave it structure without rigidity. When he fell, that stability collapsed. The Feywild became... sick. More volatile, less coherent.

And Milicent's departure made it worse. She was the other pillar—the ordered mind that could counterbalance pure chaos. With both of them gone, the Feywild lost its equilibrium. That's part of why the Estaran corruption hasn't healed. The scorched wastelands, the thorn vines, the dead zones—they persist because the realm lacks the strength to purge them.

If the Estarans return in force, I don't know if the Feywild can resist again. Not without Milicent and Ozevrial. Not without something to replace what we lost."

**About the Estaran Threat:**

"The Estarans don't conquer. They *replace*. They take living, breathing, chaotic life and reduce it to linear order—dead, ordered, eternal. They're not cruel; they don't hate us. To them , we're simply... incorrect. Messy data that needs correction. Or erasure."

**About His Current Concerns:**

"Right now? My concern is Egedzoo. That dragon is trying to raise an army of Hobgoblin pirates, and be the King of the Wasteland. And then he'll push into the twilight forest, trying to carve out territory in the living Feywild.

I've been watching him with the help of Twi'Xu and others. He's powerful and cunning. He's also vulnerable—he's still establishing himself, still building alliances. This is the time to remove him, before he becomes entrenched."

#### The Proposal

Xilo proposes slaying the dragon. 

"You're leaving the Feywild soon, yes? Back to the Mortal realm and then...

Before you go, help me end Egedzoo. Slay him, destroy his growing power, and scatter his hobgoblins back to feuding factions.

Here's my pitch: I'll arrange for his little army to be engaged. You'll be able to enter his lair with minimal obstruction. Kill the monster. Take his treasure. Leave the Feywild as heroes.

And get yourselves good and ready for whatever you're doing next  for Milicent..."

#### Roleplaying Xilo

When roleplaying Xilo, keep these traits in mind:

**Quick-Witted**: He responds rapidly to questions, often with a touch of dry humor. He anticipates where conversations are going and cuts to the point.

**Pragmatic**: He's not sentimental about violence or difficult choices. If something needs to be done, he'll do it. He respects the same attitude in others.

**Respectful of Competence**: He admires skill, cleverness, and courage. The party has demonstrated all three by making it this far, and he'll acknowledge that.

**Loyal to Milicent's Memory**: He may disagree with her methods now, but he remembers who she was and respects what she's trying to do. Anyone working with her has his default respect.

**Direct**: He doesn't dance around topics or use excessive diplomacy. He says what he means and expects others to do the same.

#### Questions the Party Might Ask

**"Why not just kill Egedzoo yourself?"**

"Fair question. I *could* face him directly, but it would be costly. Dragon versus Fey is a spectacular fight, but it's also unpredictable—especially in the wasteland where his hobgoblin forces can support him. I'd win, probably, but I'd be weakened for months afterward. And I have other enemies who would love to see me vulnerable.

But if you face him while my forces occupy his army? That changes the equation. He's isolated, you're fresh, and I can support you. It's strategy, not cowardice."

**"What can you tell us about Egedzoo's lair?"**

"His lair is in the wasteland, nestled in a canyon about fifteen miles northwest of Qu. He's taken over a natural cave system and fortified it with hobgoblin labor. Traps, chokepoints, defensible positions—he's not stupid.

The lair itself will have hazards: electric storms, unstable terrain... likely some hobgoblin guards even if we draw most of them away.

The good news is that he's not as entrenched as he could be. He's only been here a matter of months. Another year and we would see the earth itself start to change around him. His lair is dangerous, but it's still mundane."

#### Moving Forward

Once the party agrees to Xilo's proposal (or negotiates terms), he'll provide them with:
- **A map to Egedzoo's lair** marked with known entrances and hazards
- **Details about the hobgoblin forces** and when his assault will begin
- **Potions and supplies** (DM's discretion based on party needs)
- **A sending stone** to coordinate the assault timing

Xilo will make it clear: "Rest tonight. Prepare yourselves. Tomorrow, we strike. I'll draw the army away, and you'll face the dragon. Make it count."

The stage is now set for the final confrontation of Chapter 6.

### Encounter 6: The Dragon's Canyon

This is the culmination of Chapter 6—the party's assault on Egedzoo's lair while Xilo's forces engage the bulk of the hobgoblin army. This should be the most challenging dragon encounter the party has faced, combining environmental hazards, defensive fortifications, remaining guards, and a cunning opponent who fights on his home ground.

#### Encounter Overview

**Objective**: Infiltrate Egedzoo's canyon lair and slay the adult blue dragon before he can consolidate his power in the Feywild.

**Challenge Level**: This encounter is designed for a party at the upper end of their current level range. Egedzoo is an Adult Blue Dragon (CR 16), and the lair's hazards and remaining defenders will push the party to their limits.

**Timing**: The assault begins at dawn. Xilo's forces will engage the hobgoblin army approximately one mile north of the canyon, drawing most defenders away. However, Egedzoo will have kept elite guards and prepared defenses within the lair itself.

#### The Canyon Approach

The lair is located in a deep canyon carved into the wasteland's rocky terrain. The canyon is approximately 200 feet deep and 100 feet wide at the top, narrowing to 40 feet at the bottom where a dry riverbed leads to the cave entrance.

**Approaching Options:**

1. **From Above (Stealth Approach)**: The party can descend the canyon walls using climbing gear or magic. The walls are rough stone with handholds but require **DC 12 Athletics checks** to climb safely. Failure means slipping and taking 2d6 bludgeoning damage per 20 feet fallen (use the reduced falling damage rules for catching yourself).

2. **Through the Canyon Floor (Direct Approach)**: The dry riverbed provides a clear path but offers no cover. Defenders will spot the party easily.

3. **By Air (Bold Approach)**: Flying directly in is possible but triggers an immediate alarm and allows defenders to prepare.

#### Lair Features

**Weather: Artificial Storm**

Egedzoo has used his innate connection to storms to create a localized electrical tempest over the canyon. Dark clouds roil overhead, and occasional lightning strikes illuminate the scene. This storm serves both as defense and atmosphere.

**Storm Effects:**
- **Lightning Strikes**: At the start of each round, roll 1d6. On a 1, a random creature in an exposed area (canyon floor, entrance plateau, or flying) must make a **DC 15 Dexterity save** or take **4d10 lightning damage** (half on success).
- **Dim Light**: The storm clouds block natural twilight, creating dim light conditions throughout the canyon.
- **Wind**: Strong winds impose disadvantage on ranged weapon attacks (but not spells).

**Canyon Hazards:**
- **Loose Rock**: Several sections of the canyon walls are unstable, marked by Egedzoo's forces with subtle signs. A creature that steps on or near loose rock must make a **DC 14 Dexterity save** or trigger a rockslide. All creatures in a 10-foot radius must make **DC 15 Dexterity saves** or take **3d10 bludgeoning damage** and be knocked prone.
- **Electrified Water Pools**: Small pools of water collect in the canyon from previous storms. These pools are electrified by residual lightning. A creature that enters or starts its turn in a pool takes **2d8 lightning damage**.

#### Area 1: The Entrance Plateau

The cave entrance sits on a natural plateau 30 feet above the canyon floor, accessible by a crude ramp carved into the rock. This area serves as Egedzoo's first line of defense.

**Defenders:**
- **2 Hobgoblin Devastators** ([Hobgoblin Devastator](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560854-hobgoblin-devastator)) - Elite spellcasters loyal to Egedzoo
- **4 Hobgoblin Iron Shadows** ([Hobgoblin Iron Shadow](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560857-hobgoblin-iron-shadow)) - Stealthy assassins positioned to ambush climbers

**Fortifications:**
- **Stone Barricades**: Provide half cover for defenders
- **Alarm Glyph**: A magical alarm at the ramp's base alerts defenders if triggered (**DC 16 Investigation** to notice, **DC 16 Arcana** to disable)
- **Caltrops**: Scattered across the ramp (difficult terrain, damage on movement)

**Tactics:**
- Devastators stay behind barricades, using area spells to catch multiple party members
- Iron Shadows wait until party members are climbing, then strike at disadvantaged targets
- If overwhelmed, survivors retreat into the cave to warn Egedzoo

#### Area 2: The Narrow Passage

Beyond the entrance, a 60-foot passage leads deeper into the mountain. This passage is only 10 feet wide and 15 feet tall—tight quarters designed to negate numbers advantage and create chokepoints.

**Traps:**
- **Pressure Plate (30 feet in)**: Triggers a **lightning bolt trap** that fires down the corridor. **DC 16 Perception** to spot, **DC 16 Dexterity save** to avoid. On failure, take **8d6 lightning damage** (half on success).
- **Collapsing Ceiling (45 feet in)**: Triggered by a hidden lever Egedzoo can activate from the main chamber, or by a tripwire. **DC 17 Perception** to spot tripwire, **DC 17 Dexterity save** to avoid. Creatures in a 10-foot section must save or take **6d10 bludgeoning damage** and be restrained under rubble (requires **DC 15 Athletics** check to escape).

**Features:**
- **Murder Holes**: Small openings in the ceiling allow defenders in the chamber beyond to fire arrows or drop alchemist's fire on intruders
- **Narrow Width**: Only two Medium creatures can fight abreast; Large creatures move at half speed

#### Area 3: The Guard Chamber

This natural cavern (40 feet wide, 60 feet long, 20 feet high) serves as a rallying point for Egedzoo's elite guards. Rough stone walls glitter with embedded quartz that reflects light eerily.

**Defenders:**
- **1 Hobgoblin Warlord** ([Hobgoblin Warlord](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560861-hobgoblin-warlord)) - Egedzoo's most trusted lieutenant
- **6 Hobgoblin Soldiers** ([Hobgoblin](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560856-hobgoblin)) - Disciplined warriors in formation

**Features:**
- **Pillars**: Natural stone columns provide cover and break line of sight
- **Elevated Ledge**: A 10-foot-high ledge on the eastern wall provides high ground for ranged attackers
- **Murder Holes**: Accessible from above, allowing guards to attack into the narrow passage

**Tactics:**
- Warlord uses tactical commands to coordinate soldiers
- Soldiers form defensive line at the far end, protecting passage to Egedzoo's chamber
- If battle goes poorly, warlord calls for Egedzoo (who may arrive in 2-3 rounds)

**Treasure:**
- Warlord carries a **+1 longsword** with Egedzoo's mark
- Footlocker contains **500 gp** and a **potion of superior healing**

#### Area 4: Egedzoo's Sanctum

The final chamber is a massive natural cavern (100 feet wide, 120 feet long, 40 feet high) that Egedzoo has claimed as his throne room and treasure vault. Stalactites hang from the ceiling like stone teeth, and the floor is uneven with outcroppings and depressions.

**Chamber Features:**

**Hoard**: Egedzoo's treasure is piled in the northwestern corner—coins, gems, weapons, armor, and art objects looted from caravans and settlements. The hoard is worth approximately **25,000 gp** in mixed currency and goods, plus specific magic items (DM's discretion based on party level and needs).

**Elevated Platform**: A natural rock formation in the center rises 15 feet high, serving as Egedzoo's perch. He can survey the entire chamber from here.

**Lightning Rod**: A crude but effective metal rod extends from the hoard toward an opening in the ceiling. Egedzoo can use a bonus action to call lightning to strike the rod, creating an electrical discharge in a 20-foot radius around the hoard. Creatures in the area must make **DC 16 Dexterity saves** or take **6d6 lightning damage** (half on success). He can do this once per short rest.

**Unstable Floor**: Several sections of the floor (marked on DM's map) are thin stone over hollow pockets. A creature standing on unstable floor when significant force is applied (such as Egedzoo landing, an explosion, or intentional stomping) must make a **DC 14 Dexterity save** or fall 20 feet into a hollow cavity, taking falling damage and becoming difficult to reach.

**Stalactites**: Egedzoo can use his tail attack or breath weapon to shatter stalactites, causing them to fall on creatures below. When a stalactite falls, creatures in a 5-foot radius must make **DC 15 Dexterity saves** or take **3d10 piercing damage**.

#### Egedzoo's Statistics

Use the **Adult Blue Dragon** stat block from the Monster Manual or D&D Beyond:
- **Hit Points**: 225 (18d12 + 108)
- **Armor Class**: 19 (natural armor)
- **Legendary Actions**: 3 per round
- **Lightning Breath** (Recharge 5-6): 12d10 lightning damage, 90-foot line, DC 19 Dexterity save for half
- **Frightful Presence**: DC 19 Wisdom save or be frightened for 1 minute

**Link**: [Adult Blue Dragon](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/5194870-adult-blue-dragon)

#### Egedzoo's Tactics

Egedzoo is cunning, experienced, and fights to win. He will not engage in honorable single combat—he uses every advantage the lair provides.

**Round 1-2 (Opening)**:
- Uses **Frightful Presence** to shake the party's resolve
- Positions on elevated platform for aerial advantage
- Uses **Lightning Breath** to hit as many party members as possible
- Calls out taunts: "You dare invade my domain? Xilo sends mortals to do his work because he fears facing me himself!"

**Round 3-4 (Adaptation)**:
- Flies to different positions, using mobility to avoid being surrounded
- Targets spellcasters and healers with focused attacks
- Uses **Lightning Rod** if party clusters near the hoard
- Breaks stalactites to rain debris on grouped enemies

**Round 5+ (Desperation)**:
- If bloodied (below 112 HP), becomes more aggressive and reckless
- Attempts to collapse unstable floor sections under dangerous enemies
- Uses legendary actions more defensively (Detect, Wing Attack to reposition)
- If reduced to 50 HP or less, attempts to negotiate: "Wait! We can make a deal! I have information about the Estarans—secrets worth more than my death!"

**Secrets Egedzoo Knows (If Party Negotiates)**:
- He encountered Estaran ruins deeper in the wasteland while establishing his territory
- There's a dormant Radiant Projector in those ruins, partially buried
- He's been studying it, trying to understand how it works
- He'll reveal the location if the party spares him (though Xilo will not be pleased)

**If Egedzoo Escapes**:
- At 25 HP or less, if he has a clear path, he will attempt to flee via the ceiling opening
- This creates a recurring villain for future chapters
- Xilo will be disappointed but understanding: "Dragon hunting is rarely clean. We'll get another chance."

#### Conclusion

Once Egedzoo is defeated (slain or driven off), the party can:

**Loot the Hoard**:
- **Treasure**: 25,000 gp worth of coins, gems, art objects, and trade goods
- **Magic Items** (DM's choice, but suggestions):
  - *Javelin of Lightning*
  - *Ring of Resistance (lightning)*
  - *Wand of Lightning Bolts*
  - *Amulet of Health*
  - One uncommon item per party member
  - One rare item for the party to share

**Meet with Xilo**:
- Xilo arrives within an hour, his forces having scattered the hobgoblin army
- He congratulates the party: "Well done. The Feywild is safer with that tyrant gone. And I'm in your debt."
- He provides:
  - **Safe passage** back to the Material Plane via a Fey crossing he controls
  - **Gift**: A *Feywild Compass*—a magical item that always points toward the nearest crossing between the Material Plane and Feywild (useful for future travel)
  - **Alliance pledge**: "When the Estarans return, call on me. The Rebel Fey will answer."

**Return to the Material Plane**:
- Xilo guides the party to a stable Fey crossing a day's travel away
- Cetyri and Bronik accompany them, Cetyri now resigned to his role
- Hootini remains with Aelira, serving as a link to the Feywild
- The party emerges back in the Faedwood, not far from where they entered

#### Experience and Advancement

This encounter should represent a major milestone. Consider awarding:
- **Full XP** for all defeated enemies and traps overcome
- **Story XP** for successfully gaining Xilo as an ally
- **Bonus XP** for creative tactics or particularly clever solutions
- Potential **level-up** if the party is due for advancement

#### Chapter 6 Conclusion

With Egedzoo defeated and Xilo's alliance secured, the party returns to the Material Plane with:
- Cetyri's commitment to the cause
- Knowledge of Milicent's research and the Estaran threat's true scope
- A powerful Fey ally in the coming conflict
- Significant treasure and magical resources
- Hootini as a permanent companion

The stage is now set for Chapter 7, where the party must continue building alliances and preparing for the Estarans' inevitable return.
