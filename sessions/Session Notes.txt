# Chapter 8 Session Notes

session 1

Party travels to Metgal and Cetyri uses the Radiant Lance for the first time. The party crosses the void and finds themselves in the grand blizzard. Thinking quickly, they deploy their magic fortress and immediately find shelter.

They notice the feeling of something missing and put their minds to work trying to solve it. Is it heat? No, they can light fires. Does magic work here? Yes, it seems to work normally. For now, it's a mystery. They use druidic magic and determine that the storm will pass soon, and it does.

Once they get a clear look, they see they are on a frozen mountain ridge above a valley. A frozen river traces through to the sea, and there is a settlement. They head down the slope.

They are attacked by hungry sabretooth cats, who stun the party with their ferocity. The party fights back with steel and magic, slaying one and driving the others away. As the pride retreats, they see that they have been watched by a frost giant scout. The scout retreats north along the ridge and the party resumes their path west into the valley.

Babas takes an aerial survey and spots the ring a mile from the center of the town. They can see that some fires are lit but the town is not full. She sees the giant continuing north, and far in the distance she sees a great *something* move briefly, then blend back into the snow (DM note: this is Gullux, child of Torma)

At the ring, the party quickly recognize this is an Estaran artifact. So they have been here. Sylvaris scouts ahead to the center of the settlement, passing abandoned farmsteads that have clearly been stripped of their supplies. They notice it is very, very quiet.

Finally reaching the town center, Sylvaris finally finds someone. A few residents are out, clearing snow from the blizzard. Three humans and a Goliath. They are dressed like this is the climate they expect. Sylvaris peaks in a window and sees a family huddled around a fire. No guards. No warriors. Maybe a third of the town is deserted.

Sylvaris retreats, rejoining the party so they can plan their next steps...
