# Chapter 8: Across The Void — Session Reference

## Setup

**Premise:** The party crosses to an alien ice world for the first time, confronts Torma and her dragon children, and frees a hatchling Cetyri imprisoned in the dead Feywild.

**Who crosses:** Party + Felicity + Cetyri
**Who stays behind:** Xilo and Yrk (stop at the Metgal border — no Feywild Fey can safely enter Metgal), Gallifax, Maelric

**The world:** A planet in a centuries-long ice age caused by the Estarans deliberately destroying its Feywild. The Estarans withdrew approximately one year ago — recalled by central command — leaving mortals without support or explanation.

**Chapter goal:** Break Torma's siege of Farthim → enter the dead Feywild → free the hatchling → complete the first merge.

---

## E01 — A Word with Xilo
*On the road to Metgal, the evening before arrival*

Xilo pauses the group. He has something to say before they reach the moment of departure. He does not expect to see any of them again.

### Xilo's Words

> "None of us except the dragon have any idea where you'll go. And he doesn't know what waits for you. I expect we'll never meet again.
>
> But hear this: heroism requires no recognition. You can save the world whether you'll be known for it or not.
>
> We don't take wing for the first time expecting to soar. We leave the nest hoping for a soft landing."

**Notes:** Give players room to respond — this is a genuine farewell from a character they fought for. Xilo is not being grim; he is being honest in his whimsical way. This is also the last night with Yrk, Gallifax, and Maelric. If players want other goodbye scenes, give them room.

## E02 — The Radiant Lance
*Metgal crater — final farewell, the crossing*

### Read-Aloud — The Heart of Metgal

> The center of Metgal is a wound that has never closed. A crater a quarter mile across marks where the Estaran stronghold once stood — the tallest towers, the deepest vaults, the greatest concentration of their power on this world. What remains is scorched stone that melted and re-hardened into shapes no mason intended, and air that is wrong in a way you feel before you can name it. Too still. Too dry. Charged.
>
> The ruins extend in every direction beyond the crater's rim. Metgal was a great city once. It no longer resembles one. Buildings fused together into masses. Streets that end in sheer drops. Passages that lead somewhere else. The demons that the Estarans left behind — or that grew in the Estarans' absence — have drawn back from the path your allies cleared, but they have not left. You can hear them in the dark corridors beyond the light.
>
> Gallifax circles overhead, broad and slow. Maelric stands at the ridge with Felicity, looking down into the crater. Neither speaks. They gave you this — the silence, the cleared path, the moment. Whatever happens next belongs to you.
>
> At the center of the crater, the Radiant Lance stands ready. Felicity stands beside it, waiting. Cetyri crouches nearby, eyes closed, head low. His breath mists in the dead air. He is reading the paths.

**Notes:** Felicity demonstrates the Lance's alignment first. Then Cetyri takes over — he will ask for quiet. Give the meditation room. He prefers the path that is shortest, strongest, and cleanest. The party can debate or defer; he will have an opinion.

### Read-Aloud — The Crossing

> The Radiant Lance hums at a frequency you feel in your jaw and behind your eyes. The light it produces is the same radiance you've seen before — Estaran light — but here it expands outward until there is nothing else. No crater. No ruins. No sky.
>
> You are nowhere, briefly. The sensation is not painful, but it is deeply strange — as if the self that you are is held in suspension while something vast and indifferent considers whether to accept it. Cetyri's voice reaches you, low and steady, though you cannot see him. You have the impression of a road, but the road is not made of anything.
>
> Then: cold.

## E03 — Shock of Cold
*Glacier arrival, blizzard, combat*

### Read-Aloud — Arrival

> Cold arrives all at once, from every direction, so immediate and total that the first breath you draw feels like swallowing ice. Your feet find ground — dark stone beneath a skin of wind-packed snow. The light is gone.
>
> The blizzard hits you before you can orient yourselves. Wind screams across the glacier. You can see perhaps ten feet in any direction, and what you see is white.
>
> The sky, in the gaps between gusts, is wrong. You cannot immediately say how. The color is right — grey, overcast — but something that should be there isn't. The world feels thinner than it should. Quieter in a way that has nothing to do with sound.

**The wrongness of the sky:** Don't name it. It's an absence — the dead Feywild. Let it sit. To Fey party members, the absence is more acute; it was killed, and the wound remains.

**Survival first:** They must find shelter before they can do anything else. Once the storm breaks, the fight begins.

### Combat: 4 Dire Sabretooths

Large predators that have learned the glacier is a place where disoriented prey appears. The party suffers environmental hazards; the cats do not.

| Hazard | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| Weak ice | DC 13 Dex save or fall prone + cold damage |
| Wind | Disadvantage on ranged attacks; DC 12 Str save when moving more than half speed or prone |
| Visibility | Half cover beyond 30 ft even after the blizzard |

**Frost Giant scout:** During or shortly after the fight, a Frost Giant scout watches from a ridge and withdraws. His report reaches Torma before the party reaches Farthim. She already knows they're coming.

## E04 — The Settlement
*Arrival in Farthim — roleplay and investigation*

After getting their bearings, the party spots Farthim down slope in the valley adjacent to a frozen river.

### First Contact

**Village's first question:** *"Did the Estarans send you?"* They don't know whether to be hopeful or terrified by the answer.

**If Fey party members are visible:** Wonder, hushed voices, reaching out carefully. To the people of Farthim, Fey are myth — creatures from a world that no longer exists. Their reaction is the first thread: why are Fey legend here? Let players ask. The elders can tell the history, though it's legend by now. The core truth is buried in it: something happened to the Fey long ago, the sky changed, and the cold came and never left.

**Language:** Common tongue, strange accents but understandable. Estarans spread it throughout the cosmos. Fey languages are completely undecipherable here.

### Farthim — Key Details

- ~150-200 people (humans and goliaths); once larger
- A third of the population has died in the year since the Shield failed
- Giants have been raiding hunting parties for months — taking the meat, leaving the dead

**The Shield:** Estarans maintained a 1-mile climate barrier around the town. When they vanished, it failed overnight. The frozen farmland ring at the old boundary is still visible — dead orchards, collapsed outbuildings. The current residents lived through that transition; they remember the green and the warmth.

**The Estaran disappearance:** Whole village woke up mid-day as if returning to awareness, Estarans simply gone. No warning, no farewell, no sign of struggle. Three camps on why:
- *They left* — finished their work and departed; cold reading
- *They were taken* — something powerful enough removed them; frightening reading
- *They were driven away* — what drove them, and is it still out there?

> **DM truth:** Ordered recall by Central Command. Outposts across multiple worlds are being pulled back — preparations for an existential reset in the Estaran core. The village's fear and speculation are based on incomplete information.

### Estaran Structures in Town (worth exploring)

**Generator Hall:** Housed the Shield mechanism. Machinery still present but inert. Nobody understands how it worked.

**Projector Hall:** Wall diagrams depict three other Estaran worlds — destinations or counterparts to this installation. Names are written in inscrutable Estaran runes, but the diagrams are detailed enough that the runes can be copied for later translation. (The party's home world code is 03b-11.)

Both structures are treated with reverence and unease. Not looted.

### Village Leaders

| Name | Role |
|------|------|
| **Gorven** | Elder |
| **Amalia** | Hunt master and lore keeper; knows the Feywild history (as legend) |
| **Marx** | Quartermaster |

**Pacing note:** Torma already knows the party is here. Giants may probe the village before the party is ready to push out. Give players time to invest before the fighting starts.

## E05 — The Giant Tribe
*Breaking the siege — series of engagements*

Not a single fight — a small campaign to dismantle the raiding force. Should feel earned.

### The Tribe
- ~20 Frost Giants total; a raiding confederation loyal to Torma by fear and kin
- Camped in the valley approaches north of Farthim, spread across 2-3 positions
- Not desperate — they have been at this for months and can wait
- They do not assault the village directly; they ambush hunting parties

**Gullux (CR12):** Torma's older child. Steady, disciplined, knows the terrain. The more dangerous of the two. Flies point on raids.

**Bronik (CR12):** Torma's younger child. Erratic, reckless. The potential weak point — his rashness may be exploitable.

**The uncanniness:** Gullux and Bronik are mirrors of characters the party knows from home — but younger here, and loyal to a very living mother. Play that strangeness.

**Cetyri's sense:** He can feel the hatchling's general direction but his read is confused — the hatchling is not on the mortal plane. He knows something is close and gold. He cannot place it clearly until the party crosses into the dead Feywild.

**Defeating the tribe** opens access to the dead Feywild. Torma will not receive visitors while her forces are intact.

## E06 — Torma's Realm
*The dead Feywild, the boss fight, the merge*

### The Dead Feywild

Cold. Grey. Still. Beautiful in a ruined way, like frost on a corpse. Unlike the Shadowfell, this place has no life in it — something was killed here and the wound has gone cold.

To Fey or those with Fey sensitivity: the Feywild is not simply absent, it was killed. The Estarans destroyed it deliberately as part of their campaign to control and reshape this world. This is a recurring Estaran pattern — suppression, exploitation, or outright destruction on every world they touch.

### Torma

The boss. She has chosen to live in the ruin of what the Estarans destroyed — that choice says something about her. She may use the hatchling as leverage.

> **TBD:** Stat block and tactics.

### The Hatchling

Taken as a prize. Torma regards it as a trophy of significance without fully understanding why. It has known nothing but captivity and has no world attachment.

Cetyri's read resolves once the party crosses — he will know where the hatchling is.

### The Merge

Safe: the party's Cetyri is the stronger dragon; the hatchling has no deep world attachment. After the merge, Cetyri grows in power.
