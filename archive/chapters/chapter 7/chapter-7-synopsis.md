# Chapter 7: Aris - The Fracturing Capital

## Aris: The Capital City

### Physical Description

Aris sprawls across seven hills at the confluence of two rivers, a city of contrasts and contradictions. It is built atop—and from—the bones of an ancient Estaran stronghold, the largest such complex in the known world. The Estaran foundations remain: rigid avenues laid in perfect geometric patterns, soaring towers of dark stone with crystalline windows, and the monumental Hall of Allies at the city's heart.

But a century of mortal habitation has transformed the austere Estaran architecture into something alive and chaotic. Wild paths have been carved between the ordered streets. Colorful market stalls cluster in the shadows of severe towers. Gardens burst from rooftops and balconies. Bridges—some elegant, some ramshackle—connect buildings at every level, creating a three-dimensional maze of commerce and community.

The city is home to perhaps 50,000 souls: humans, dwarves, elves, halflings, gnomes, dragonborn, tieflings, and more. It is the most cosmopolitan settlement in the Allied Cities, a place where you can hear a dozen languages in a single market square. The diversity is both strength and tension—different peoples with different histories trying to build something new together.

### The Hall of Allies

The central feature of Aris, the Hall of Allies dominates the highest hill. Originally an Estaran command nexus, it has been transformed into the seat of the Allied Cities' government. The vast central chamber—large enough to house Gallifax comfortably—serves as assembly hall, throne room, and symbolic heart of the alliance.

The Hall's architecture speaks to its dual nature. Estaran crystal matrices still pulse with faint light in the walls. Ancient command glyphs have been covered by tapestries depicting the Rebellion. The throne where Maelric presides is carved from wood and stone, deliberately humble against the Hall's austere grandeur—a statement that mortal leadership serves the people, not the other way around.

Gallifax has made his lair in the vast chamber. The ancient dragon's hoard is surprisingly modest for one of his age—he values knowledge and historical artifacts over gold. Maelric's throne sits at the center of a careful arrangement of cushions and protective wards where the Chief Exarch remains in his trance.

### Districts of Note

**The High Ward** - Where the old Estaran towers cluster most thickly. Home to government offices, wealthy merchants, and the established families. The Veterans who dominate the Citizen Conference often gather in the High Ward's many formal meeting halls.

**The Living Districts** - The vast majority of Aris, where people actually live and work. A chaotic mix of old and new, planned and improvised. Here the Young Bastards have their strongest support.

**The Underworks** - The Estaran structures extend deep underground. Much remains sealed or unexplored. Some sections have been converted to storage, workshops, or housing. Rumors persist of deeper levels that contain dangerous Estaran relics.

**The River Markets** - Where the two rivers meet, massive floating market platforms serve merchants from across the Allied Cities. Goods flow in and out of Aris constantly, making it the economic heart of the alliance.

## Recent History: Setting the Stage

### Two Months Ago (Mid-February)

Chief Exarch Maelric Sturmwald entered a meditative trance to explore his subconscious mind, attempting to recover lost memories from his time fighting in Estaran wars on other worlds. He had entered such states before—brief journeys lasting hours or a day at most. This time was different.

Maelric did not wake. After three days, Gallifax summoned the most trusted Veterans, including Aldrin Stoneward from Oechenfor. The diagnosis was grim: Maelric had triggered an Estaran psychic trap, a snare laid in the minds of all veterans to prevent exactly this kind of investigation. He was alive but unreachable, lost in his own trapped consciousness.

### The Citizen Conference

For the first time in a century, the Allied Cities had no clear leader. The Counsel of Citizens—representatives from all member cities—convened in emergency session. What began as crisis management quickly exposed deeper fractures.

The Veterans, who have dominated politics since the Rebellion, closed ranks. They insisted Maelric would recover and urged patience. In the interim, they proposed a Council of Regency drawn from senior Veterans to make decisions in his name.

The Young Bastards see this as intolerable. Nava Kurn and her allies argued that Maelric's collapse proved the need for new leadership, that the Veterans' supernatural longevity had created an unnatural political stagnation. They demanded immediate reforms: veteran retirements, new public offices so the , redistribution of resources away from "Estaran paranoia" toward present needs.

### Aldrin's Mission

Aldrin arrived hoping to help his friend Maelric and stabilize the alliance. Instead, he found himself caught in an escalating political crisis. His reputation as a fair-minded moderate made him valuable to both factions—and trapped him in the middle.

Erol arrived shortly after Aldrin, drawn by the same concerns. Together they have spent two months trying to hold things together through persuasion and compromise. But the situation deteriorates daily:

- Young Bastard protests in the streets grow larger and more confrontational
- Veteran councilors grow more rigid and defensive
- Ardo Manessi plays both sides, gathering power while appearing conciliatory
- Gallifax refuses all attempts to reach Maelric, trusting no one
- Rumors spread: Maelric is dead, Maelric is already awake and being held prisoner, Maelric was assassinated by [insert faction here]
