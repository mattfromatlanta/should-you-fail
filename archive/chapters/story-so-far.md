# The Story So Far

## Chapter 1: The Invaders

**March 14-19**

When spring thaw arrived and no word came from the highland village of Lome, Chief Citizen Aldrin Stoneward called upon the party to investigate. The journey north through the Faedwood revealed immediate trouble—a massive carcass discarded unnaturally, and terrified fey who spoke of attackers from the Uplands occupying the Nest.

On the evening of March 16th, the party assaulted the Nest and defeated four Dragonborn invaders, rescuing a captured pixie named Elmleaf and two travelers: Peno the Goliath and Sylvaris, an Eladrin from Naeliste. A captured Dragonborn revealed their leader—a monster called Gullux—commanded a larger force at the Upland Stair.

The next day, the party drove the invaders from the Upland Stair and reopened the path to Lome. At the manor of Erol the Faed and her companion Lamat, they found signs of a hurried departure. The protectors of Lome were nowhere to be found.

Arriving in Lome on March 19th, the party met Mayor Kruftus and the town leaders. The village had been under siege since mid-winter. Many citizens had died fighting back against the raiders. With a troop of volunteers, the party set out for the invaders' cave at the foot of the East Hills.

The battle with Gullux's forces led them into the cave, where they were suddenly trapped in a shadowy projection of reality—a twisted reflection where shrill voices urged them on. When they defeated Gullux in this strange space, they found themselves returned to the natural cave. Searching the area, they discovered three white dragon eggs. The party burned them, ensuring no more dragons would threaten the region.

## Chapter 2: Into Darkness

After a brief celebration, reality set in: a white dragon must be coming for those stolen eggs. While scouting the East Hills for potential shelter, the party encountered Bronik—a half-dragon on a wyvern, searching for his brother Gullux. Bronik recognized items from his mother's hoard. The threat was real, and her name was Torma the Unforgiving.

The party captured Bronik, and the people of Lome chose to evacuate to Oechenfor. The party remained behind to explore the cave system, discovering an entrance to the Underdark. Within the depths, they found kobold ruins—remnants of a tribe that had fled from "Shadowdame M." The kobolds had been hunted and destroyed.

Deeper still, they entered a massive underground canyon shared by two beholder twins: Maxxa'Valien and Maxxa'Volien. An intruder in their neutral territory had upset their careful balance. The party struck a deal to remove this intruder.

Through the Mushroom Forest, past zombies and sentient fungus, they found the intruder in the canyon's exact center: Cetyri, a blind gold dragon revenant. Once imprisoned and tortured by Milicent—the same Shadowdame who destroyed the kobolds—Cetyri had washed into the canyon by chance, unaware of where he was. The party resolved to lead him out.

Returning to settle their contract, the party was pulled back into Milicent's Shadow, facing a corrupted version of Maxxa'Valien—first as a zombie beholder, then as an Eye Shadow. With the balance between the beholder twins broken, the party fled the Underdark with Cetyri and Bronik.

At the cave mouth, they found their exit collapsed. Torma had attacked from above, sealing them in. Then Erol and Lamat arrived, slaying the white dragon and freeing the party from their tomb.

## Chapter 3: The Shadow Pursuit

**March 25-27**

Erol explained her absence: she and Lamat had discovered they were targets of a kidnapping plot by Milicent, Erol's former patron. The Shadowdame had not forgotten their bond. Erol and Lamat fled into the Feywild, where they gathered troubling intelligence: Milicent had reactivated the same ritual that created the Dread Legion during the First Rebellion. She was collecting bound souls, conscripting them into something vast. For what purpose, Erol did not know.

Before parting, Erol urged the party to seek the Trickster's Blessing, which would protect them from Milicent's gaze. She directed them to Naeliste, the Fey city where Milicent could not track them.

For two days through the Faedwood, the party was pursued by Milicent's quicklings, harried at every step. They were attacked by wraiths and giants. During the journey, both Bronik and Cetyri opened up, each questioning their place in what was to come.

The party arrived in Naeliste on March 27th. Sylvaris went off with his friend Chevin the Silkstrand and hasn't been seen since. The party joined the Goblin stories and met Eigitch the Old, who spoke of the Trickster's Blessing and the Drekklands far to the south. He recommended they consult Bo'Lug Longtongue, who knew a path Milicent could not easily track.

The next day brought the Festival of the Last Frost. While Peno and Bronik helped with setup, others brought Cetyri to Lomani Nae, an Eladrin cleric whose family founded Naeliste two millennia ago. Lomani agreed to examine Cetyri and discovered something remarkable: the dragon's soul reached across space, existing in multiple places simultaneously.

Meanwhile, others visited Bo'Lug in the Foot of the Falls. The old Bullywug shared a cryptic riddle about swamps, old roots, and forgotten lords listening "still wet with sorrow." When the party sought clarification from Eigitch, he revealed his secret: a submarine called "The Rainbow Trout" that could travel the Underwet—an underground river system connecting distant places across the world.

At the Library of Rhymes and Antiquities, the party discovered crucial documents: the Testament of Embers (an Estaran epic about Varkhasia the red Unary Great Wyrm), the Ode to Seven Lords (a poem about the Rebel Fey), a letter from a goblin soldier named Grib, and a personal letter from Caera written during the Rebellion. These texts revealed the deep history of the world—including hints that the Material Plane had been destroyed and recreated before.

A speak with dead ritual revealed information about Milicent's "Shadow Projector"—a device using the Material Plane itself as a lens to capture and transform beings. 

When Lomani shared his findings about Cetyri's multiversal soul, the dragon became worried. This might be what Erol meant by "his choice"—connecting his fragmented souls could grant him strength to destroy Milicent, but with disastrous effects.

Concerned, Cetyri and Bronik fled together into the Feywild.

## Chapter 4: Journey to the Trickster

Eigitch took the party through the Underwet in his submarine. After fighting chuul along the way, they surfaced in a cave where they battled kua-toa and rescued four bullywugs from the mental grip of an Aboleth named Mr'Hrm'N'Ahir. Before they defeated it, the Aboleth asked a cryptic question: "How many moments has this world existed?" The question was a subtle hint that there have been worlds before this one.

The party surfaced with the rescued bullywugs at Bullhome, a ruined keep by a vast lake in the Drekklands. There they met the rest of the Gulpfen Brood, separated when the strongest members set out through water passages while others waited at the keep. The party reunited the tribe. The Gulpfen survivors warned about Kriv the Blacktongue, the greatest black dragon in the eastern Drekklands.

The party set out southwest to avoid Kriv's territory, traveling through the Drekklands' strange and dangerous landscape. The swamp exhibited magical instability: shifting channels, temporal slippage, illusion-rooted flora, mood-inducing weather, and terrain that folded back on itself. They encountered dinosaurs, trolls, and worse.

In the southern Drekklands, they discovered a sunken Estaran tower at the bottom of a lake. The interior was larger than the exterior, with fragmented space and random portals linking different areas. The party became separated as they explored and faced two young black dragons—but something was wrong. The dragons appeared to be exactly the same dragon in two bodies, a hint at the multiverse's nature.

Realizing they had to go east to find the Trickster, the party cautiously entered Kriv's territory. When they arrived at the great dragon's lair, they found Kriv already dead—slain by an unknown force.

A lone goblin attacked them. This was Yrk the Trickster, and despite being nearly 2000 years old, his power was remarkable. He defeated the party easily, slaying them one by one.

The party awoke with Yrk in a strange space—like a shadow projection but somehow calmer. Yrk apologized. This was the Trickster's Blessing. Through their deaths in his calm shadow space, they had been severed from their mortal attachments to the Material Plane. They could no longer be followed by Milicent or tracked on the Material Plane.

They returned with Yrk to the material world and reunited with Eigitch. The ancient goblin and his great-great-great-grandson greeted each other with dry, understated affection. The party asked Yrk to reach out to Milicent seeking an audience, and he agreed to contact the Shadowdame.

Eigitch and the party made their way back to Naeliste by submarine, now invisible to Milicent's gaze and ready for what comes next.

## Chapter 5: Return to Oechenfor

Protected by the Trickster's Blessing, the party returned from Naeliste to Oechenfor, where they discovered a disturbing pattern: promising young citizens had been vanishing since they left. Centaurs in the Faedwood warned of dark omens over the city.

Investigation led to Gord Lankenmangled, the imprisoned dwarf merchant, who had been crafting specialized tools from star metal fragments—the same material found at the Estaran tower in the Drekklands. These tools were designed for quicklings conducting kidnapping operations for Milicent.

The party raided the quicklings' safehouse and captured the tools. The next evening, quickling leader Felicity burned the safehouse to lure them back. When the party returned, they were pulled into shadow space once more. After defeating Felicity's forces, they were offered something different: an invitation to go deeper.

The party accepted and found themselves fully in the Shadowfell, greeted by Yrk and Milicent's honor guard. As promised guests, they were escorted to Milicent's mountain fortress.

In her audience hall, Milicent revealed the full truth. She brought them to a sealed chamber holding an imprisoned Estaran and explained the horrifying reality: the Estarans have destroyed and recreated the universe 54 times before. Each time a Dragon Singularity emerged or rebellion threatened their empire, they enacted total annihilation and reconstruction. The current world is the 55th iteration.

Milicent's plan: guide Cetyri to become a Unary Great Wyrm—a Dragon Singularity synthesized from all versions of his soul across realities. This cosmic being is what the Estarans fear most, powerful enough to defeat them permanently before they complete their next apocalypse.

The party agreed to ally with Milicent on one condition: she must cease enslaving mortals and put her hopes entirely in their hands. After centuries building the Dread Legion, Milicent accepted. She returned them safely to Oechenfor.

Now the party must return to Naeliste and have a conversation with Cetyri...

## Chapter 6: Into the Wild

Upon returning to Naeliste, the party discovered that Cetyri and Bronik had fled into the Feywild. Lomani Nae's careful questioning had revealed she understood the truth about Cetyri's world-spanning soul, and the young dragon ran to protect that secret.

The party pursued them through the Faedwood into the Feywild's scorched wasteland—outer regions devastated by ancient Estaran invasion. After encounters with hobgoblin bandits and a tense meeting at Rinick's Diner with Twi'Xu (Animal Lord and lieutenant to Xilo)--where they also learned of the Blue Dragon Egedzoo--they tracked Cetyri to the settlement of Qu.

Through honest conversation, the party convinced Cetyri to stop running and accept his role in stopping the Estarans. Understanding the survival of all worlds is at stake, Cetyri committed to the cause.

Hoping to gain Xilo as an ally and connect to their fey roots, the party commissioned an airship deeper into the Feywild. Mid-flight, they were attacked by Egedzoo, the adult blue dragon seeking to rule the wasteland. Though they repelled the attack, their airship crashed near a mysterious tower structure.

The tower proved to be the memorial tomb of Ozevriel, the Rebel Fey hero slain in the first rebellion against the Estarans. The party defeated the trapped demons and ascended to the top of the tower. Here the discovered Ozevrial's tomb was created at the base of Milicent's ancient lab. They encountered Hootini there and spent a few hours thoroughly reviewing everything they could find—critical research on the Estarans and cross-world transit theories developed with Yrk. Hootini then let them know that Xilo had arrived and was waiting back at the airship. They started thinking about Metgal as a place to go next.

Xilo was pleased to meet them and supports their grand quest. To begin, he asked them for help slaying Egedzoo. The dragon is building power in the wasteland and must be stopped. The party agreed.

The assault on Egedzoo's lair was decisive. While Xilo's forces drew away the hobgoblin army, the party infiltrated the canyon stronghold. Cormin's illusions wasted Egedzoo's ambush, Aelira's webs pinned the dragon, and Peno's might finished him. With Egedzoo slain and his hoard claimed, Xilo's alliance was secured.

Xilo agreed to travel with them as an envoy of the Rebel Fey. The party now returns to the Material Plane with Cetyri, Bronik, Xilo, and Hootini—ready to journey to Aris and then to Metgal, where they hope to find the means to travel beyond this world.
