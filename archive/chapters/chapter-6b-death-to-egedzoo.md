# Chapter 6b: Death to Egedzoo

## Overview

With Xilo's alliance contingent on removing the threat of Egedzoo, the party prepares for a dangerous assault on the adult blue dragon's lair. Xilo will draw the bulk of the hobgoblin army away, but the party must still infiltrate a fortified canyon stronghold, navigate deadly traps and hazards, defeat elite guards, and face a cunning dragon who fights on his home ground.

This is the culmination of Chapter 6—a high-stakes battle that will test the party's tactical abilities, teamwork, and raw power. Success will secure Xilo's alliance and allow them to return to the Material Plane with Cetyri, ready to continue building the coalition against the Estarans.

## The Dragon's Canyon

This is the culmination of Chapter 6—the party's assault on Egedzoo's lair while Xilo's forces engage the bulk of the hobgoblin army. This should be the most challenging dragon encounter the party has faced, combining environmental hazards, defensive fortifications, remaining guards, and a cunning opponent who fights on his home ground.

### Encounter Overview

**Objective**: Infiltrate Egedzoo's canyon lair and slay the adult blue dragon before he can consolidate his power in the Feywild.

**Challenge Level**: This encounter is designed for a party at the upper end of their current level range. Egedzoo is an Adult Blue Dragon (CR 16), and the lair's hazards and remaining defenders will push the party to their limits.

**Timing**: The assault begins at dawn. Xilo's forces will engage the hobgoblin army, drawing most defenders away. However, Egedzoo will have kept elite guards and prepared defenses within the lair itself.

### The Canyon Approach

The lair is located in a deep canyon carved into the wasteland's rocky terrain. The canyon is approximately 200 feet deep and 100 feet wide at the top, narrowing to 40 feet at the bottom where a dry riverbed leads to the cave entrance.

**Approaching Options:**

1. **From Above (Stealth Approach)**: The party can descend the canyon walls using climbing gear or magic. The walls are rough stone with handholds but require **DC 12 Athletics checks** to climb safely. Failure means slipping and taking 2d6 bludgeoning damage per 20 feet fallen (use the reduced falling damage rules for catching yourself).

2. **Through the Canyon Floor (Direct Approach)**: The dry riverbed provides a clear path but offers no cover. Defenders will spot the party easily.

3. **By Air (Bold Approach)**: Flying directly in is possible but triggers an immediate alarm and allows defenders to prepare.

### Lair Features

**Weather: Artificial Storm**

Egedzoo has used his innate connection to storms to create a localized electrical tempest over the canyon. Dark clouds roil overhead, and occasional lightning strikes illuminate the scene. This storm serves both as defense and atmosphere.

**Storm Effects:**
- **Lightning Strikes**: At the start of each round, roll 1d6. On a 1, a random creature in an exposed area (canyon floor, entrance plateau, or flying) must make a **DC 15 Dexterity save** or take **4d10 lightning damage** (half on success).
- **Dim Light**: The storm clouds block natural twilight, creating dim light conditions throughout the canyon.
- **Wind**: Strong winds impose disadvantage on ranged weapon attacks (but not spells).

**Canyon Hazards:**
- **Loose Rock**: Several sections of the canyon walls are unstable, marked by Egedzoo's forces with subtle signs. A creature that steps on or near loose rock must make a **DC 14 Dexterity save** or trigger a rockslide. All creatures in a 10-foot radius must make **DC 15 Dexterity saves** or take **3d10 bludgeoning damage** and be knocked prone.
- **Electrified Water Pools**: Small pools of water collect in the canyon from previous storms. These pools are electrified by residual lightning. A creature that enters or starts its turn in a pool takes **2d8 lightning damage**.

### Area 1: The Entrance Plateau

The cave entrance sits on a natural plateau 30 feet above the canyon floor, accessible by a crude ramp carved into the rock. This area serves as Egedzoo's first line of defense.

**Defenders:**
- **2 Hobgoblin Devastators** ([Hobgoblin Devastator](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560854-hobgoblin-devastator)) - Elite spellcasters loyal to Egedzoo
- **4 Hobgoblin Iron Shadows** ([Hobgoblin Iron Shadow](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560857-hobgoblin-iron-shadow)) - Stealthy assassins positioned to ambush climbers

**Fortifications:**
- **Stone Barricades**: Provide half cover for defenders
- **Alarm Glyph**: A magical alarm at the ramp's base alerts defenders if triggered (**DC 16 Investigation** to notice, **DC 16 Arcana** to disable)
- **Caltrops**: Scattered across the ramp (difficult terrain, damage on movement)

**Tactics:**
- Devastators stay behind barricades, using area spells to catch multiple party members
- Iron Shadows wait until party members are climbing, then strike at disadvantaged targets
- If overwhelmed, survivors retreat into the cave to warn Egedzoo

### Area 2: The Narrow Passage

Beyond the entrance, a 60-foot passage leads deeper into the mountain. This passage is only 10 feet wide and 15 feet tall—tight quarters designed to negate numbers advantage and create chokepoints.

**Traps:**
- **Pressure Plate (30 feet in)**: Triggers a **lightning bolt trap** that fires down the corridor. **DC 16 Perception** to spot, **DC 16 Dexterity save** to avoid. On failure, take **8d6 lightning damage** (half on success).
- **Collapsing Ceiling (45 feet in)**: Triggered by a hidden lever Egedzoo can activate from the main chamber, or by a tripwire. **DC 17 Perception** to spot tripwire, **DC 17 Dexterity save** to avoid. Creatures in a 10-foot section must save or take **6d10 bludgeoning damage** and be restrained under rubble (requires **DC 15 Athletics** check to escape).

**Features:**
- **Murder Holes**: Small openings in the ceiling allow defenders in the chamber beyond to fire arrows or drop alchemist's fire on intruders
- **Narrow Width**: Only two Medium creatures can fight abreast; Large creatures move at half speed

### Area 3: The Guard Chamber

This natural cavern (40 feet wide, 60 feet long, 20 feet high) serves as a rallying point for Egedzoo's elite guards. Rough stone walls glitter with embedded quartz that reflects light eerily.

**Defenders:**
- **1 Hobgoblin Warlord** ([Hobgoblin Warlord](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560861-hobgoblin-warlord)) - Egedzoo's most trusted lieutenant
- **6 Hobgoblin Soldiers** ([Hobgoblin](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/2560856-hobgoblin)) - Disciplined warriors in formation

**Features:**
- **Pillars**: Natural stone columns provide cover and break line of sight
- **Elevated Ledge**: A 10-foot-high ledge on the eastern wall provides high ground for ranged attackers
- **Murder Holes**: Accessible from above, allowing guards to attack into the narrow passage

**Tactics:**
- Warlord uses tactical commands to coordinate soldiers
- Soldiers form defensive line at the far end, protecting passage to Egedzoo's chamber
- If battle goes poorly, warlord calls for Egedzoo (who may arrive in 2-3 rounds)

**Treasure:**
- Warlord carries a **+1 longsword** with Egedzoo's mark
- Footlocker contains **500 gp** and a **potion of superior healing**

### Area 4: Egedzoo's Sanctum

The final chamber is a massive natural cavern (100 feet wide, 120 feet long, 40 feet high) that Egedzoo has claimed as his throne room and treasure vault. Stalactites hang from the ceiling like stone teeth, and the floor is uneven with outcroppings and depressions.

**Chamber Features:**

**Hoard**: Egedzoo's treasure is piled in the northwestern corner—coins, gems, weapons, armor, and art objects looted from caravans and settlements. The hoard is worth approximately **25,000 gp** in mixed currency and goods, plus specific magic items (DM's discretion based on party level and needs).

**Elevated Platform**: A natural rock formation in the center rises 15 feet high, serving as Egedzoo's perch. He can survey the entire chamber from here.

**Lightning Rod**: A crude but effective metal rod extends from the hoard toward an opening in the ceiling. Egedzoo can use a bonus action to call lightning to strike the rod, creating an electrical discharge in a 20-foot radius around the hoard. Creatures in the area must make **DC 16 Dexterity saves** or take **6d6 lightning damage** (half on success). He can do this once per short rest.

**Unstable Floor**: Several sections of the floor (marked on DM's map) are thin stone over hollow pockets. A creature standing on unstable floor when significant force is applied (such as Egedzoo landing, an explosion, or intentional stomping) must make a **DC 14 Dexterity save** or fall 20 feet into a hollow cavity, taking falling damage and becoming difficult to reach.

**Stalactites**: Egedzoo can use his tail attack or breath weapon to shatter stalactites, causing them to fall on creatures below. When a stalactite falls, creatures in a 5-foot radius must make **DC 15 Dexterity saves** or take **3d10 piercing damage**.

### Egedzoo's Statistics

Use the **Adult Blue Dragon** stat block from the Monster Manual or D&D Beyond:
- **Hit Points**: 225 (18d12 + 108)
- **Armor Class**: 19 (natural armor)
- **Legendary Actions**: 3 per round
- **Lightning Breath** (Recharge 5-6): 12d10 lightning damage, 90-foot line, DC 19 Dexterity save for half
- **Frightful Presence**: DC 19 Wisdom save or be frightened for 1 minute

**Link**: [Adult Blue Dragon](https://www.dndbeyond.com/monsters/5194870-adult-blue-dragon)

### Egedzoo's Tactics

Egedzoo is cunning, experienced, and fights to win. He will not engage in honorable single combat—he uses every advantage the lair provides.

**Round 1-2 (Opening)**:
- Uses **Frightful Presence** to shake the party's resolve
- Positions on elevated platform for aerial advantage
- Uses **Lightning Breath** to hit as many party members as possible
- Calls out taunts: "You dare invade my domain? Xilo sends mortals to do his work because he fears facing me himself!"

**Round 3-4 (Adaptation)**:
- Flies to different positions, using mobility to avoid being surrounded
- Targets spellcasters and healers with focused attacks
- Uses **Lightning Rod** if party clusters near the hoard
- Breaks stalactites to rain debris on grouped enemies

**Round 5+ (Desperation)**:
- If bloodied (below 112 HP), becomes more aggressive and reckless
- Attempts to collapse unstable floor sections under dangerous enemies
- Uses legendary actions more defensively (Detect, Wing Attack to reposition)
- If reduced to 50 HP or less, attempts to negotiate: "Wait! We can make a deal! I have information about the Estarans—secrets worth more than my death!"

**Secrets Egedzoo Knows (If Party Negotiates)**:
- He encountered Estaran ruins deeper in the wasteland while establishing his territory
- There's a dormant Radiant Projector in those ruins, partially buried
- He's been studying it, trying to understand how it works
- He'll reveal the location if the party spares him (though Xilo will not be pleased)

**If Egedzoo Escapes**:
- At 25 HP or less, if he has a clear path, he will attempt to flee via the ceiling opening
- This creates a recurring villain for future chapters
- Xilo will be disappointed but understanding: "Dragon hunting is rarely clean. We'll get another chance."

## Conclusion

Once Egedzoo is defeated (slain or driven off), the party can:

### Loot the Hoard

- **Treasure**: 25,000 gp worth of coins, gems, art objects, and trade goods
- **Magic Items** (DM's choice, but suggestions):
  - *Javelin of Lightning*
  - *Ring of Resistance (lightning)*
  - *Wand of Lightning Bolts*
  - *Amulet of Health*
  - One uncommon item per party member
  - One rare item for the party to share

### Meet with Xilo

- Xilo arrives within an hour, his forces having scattered the hobgoblin army
- He congratulates the party: "Well done. The Feywild is safer with that tyrant gone. And I'm in your debt."
- He provides:
  - **Safe passage** back to the Material Plane via a Fey crossing he controls
  - **Gift**: A *Feywild Compass*—a magical item that always points toward the nearest crossing between the Material Plane and Feywild (useful for future travel)
  - **Alliance pledge**: "When the Estarans return, call on me. The Rebel Fey will answer."

### Return to the Material Plane

- Xilo guides the party to a stable Fey crossing a day's travel away
- Cetyri and Bronik accompany them, Cetyri now resigned to his role
- Hootini remains with Aelira, serving as a link to the Feywild
- The party emerges back in the Faedwood, not far from where they entered

## Experience and Advancement

This encounter should represent a major milestone. Consider awarding:
- **Full XP** for all defeated enemies and traps overcome
- **Story XP** for successfully gaining Xilo as an ally
- **Bonus XP** for creative tactics or particularly clever solutions
- Potential **level-up** if the party is due for advancement

## Chapter 6 Conclusion

With Egedzoo defeated and Xilo's alliance secured, the party returns to the Material Plane with:
- Cetyri's commitment to the cause
- Knowledge of Milicent's research and the Estaran threat's true scope
- A powerful Fey ally in the coming conflict
- Significant treasure and magical resources
- Hootini as a permanent companion

The stage is now set for the next phase of the campaign, where the party must continue building alliances and preparing for the Estarans' inevitable return.
