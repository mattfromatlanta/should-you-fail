# Chapter 4: Journey to the Trickster

## Overview

With Bronik choosing to stay in Naeliste with the Goblins, the party embarks on Eigitch's submarine journey through the Underwet—the underground river system flowing beneath the Allied Cities. Their goal: reach the Drekklands and find Yrk the Trickster to receive his blessing, which will sever their connection to the Material Plane and make them invisible to Milicent's tracking.

The journey takes them through dangerous encounters in the Underwet, a reunion of separated Bullywugs, strange travels through the magically unstable Drekklands, discovery of an ancient fragmented Estaran ruin, and ultimately to a confrontation with Yrk himself. The Trickster slays the party in combat, but this death is revealed to be the very process by which the blessing is conferred.

## Key Events

### 1. The Underwet Journey
The party travels by submarine ("The Rainbow Trout") through underground rivers. Their map contains a mistake that leads them into unexpected territory.

### 2. The Aboleth's Domain
The party encounters a Kua-Toa village where Bullywugs have been enslaved by the Aboleth Lord **Mr'Hrm'N'Ahir**. The Aboleth attempts to establish "how many moments" this world has existed—a subtle hint that there have been worlds before this one. The party defeats the Aboleth in combat and frees its thralls.

### 3. Bullhome Reunion
The party surfaces in a vast lake near a ruined keep called Bullhome. They meet survivors from the **Gulpfen Brood**—Bullywugs who fled the Drekklands and have been waiting for safety. The party reunites the tribe by freeing those enslaved by the Aboleth. The Gulpfens warn about **Kriv the Blacktongue**, the greatest black dragon in the region who resides in the eastern Drekklands.

### 4. Drekklands Travel
The party sets out southwest to avoid Kriv's territory. They experience the Drekklands' strange properties: shifting channels, temporal slippage, illusion-rooted flora, mood-inducing weather, and folded terrain. They encounter various creatures from dinosaurs to trolls to mysterious phenomena.

### 5. The Sunken Fortress
The party discovers an Estaran tower ruin at the bottom of a lake. The interior is larger than the tower appears from outside, with fragmented or damaged space. Portals randomly link different interior areas, causing the party to become separated as they explore. They face and defeat **two young black dragons** that appear to be exactly the same dragon in two bodies—a hint at the multiverse's nature.

### 6. Into Kriv's Territory
Realizing they must go east to find the Trickster, the party cautiously enters Kriv's domain, approaching by boat then by land. When they arrive at the dragon's lair, they find **Kriv has been slain**.

### 7. The Trickster's Test
A lone goblin with remarkable power attacks the party. This is **Yrk the Trickster**. He defeats the party easily, slaying them one by one.

### 8. The Blessing Process
The party awakens with Yrk in a strange space that resembles a shadow projection but somehow calmer. Yrk apologizes and explains this is how the Trickster's Blessing works. Through their deaths in his calm shadow space, they have been severed from their mortal attachments. They can no longer be followed by Milicent or tracked on the Material Plane.

### 9. Return to the Material World
The party returns with Yrk to the material world and reunites with Eigitch in an understated, affectionate greeting between the ancient goblin and his great-great-great-grandson. The party asks Yrk to reach out to Milicent seeking an audience, and he agrees to contact her.

### 10. Journey Back
Eigitch and the party make their way back to Naeliste by submarine, now invisible to Milicent's gaze.

## Major Locations

### The Underwet
Eigitch's name for the underground river system flowing beneath the Allied Cities. A secret path protected from Milicent's sight while traveling through it.

### Kua-Toa Village
Underground settlement where the Aboleth Mr'Hrm'N'Ahir dominated the minds of Bullywugs, enslaving them.

### Bullhome
A ruined keep at the edge of a vast lake in the Drekklands. Once had a name but has been unmanned and forgotten since the Rebellion. Temporarily occupied by Gulpfen Brood survivors protected by ogre zombies. Features a dry courtyard, broken tower, rain catchers, and scavenged armory.

### The Drekklands
Vast, uncomfortable swamp far south of Oechenfor. Home to black dragons, dinosaurs (tyrannosaurus, triceratops, brontosaurus), trolls, giant crocodiles, froghemoths, bodaks, ghasts, and other dangers. Populated by Lizardfolk and Svirfneblin hunters. The region is magically unstable with properties that defy normal reality.

### The Sunken Fortress
An Estaran tower ruin at the bottom of a lake in the southern Drekklands. The structure exhibits impossible spatial properties—interior is larger than exterior, space is fragmented or damaged, and portals randomly connect different areas. Evidence of the Estara's reality-bending architecture.

### Kriv's Lair
Territory of Kriv the Blacktongue in the eastern Drekklands, north of the bay in the Moldrum region.

## Major NPCs

### Mr'Hrm'N'Ahir
**Aboleth Lord** who dominated Kua-Toa and enslaved Bullywugs in the Underwet. Attempted to determine "how many moments" the current world has existed, hinting at knowledge of previous realities. Defeated by the party.

### Gulpfen Brood Survivors (at Bullhome)
- **Gu'Rup** - The leader, older and always damp, wears a reed hat he never removes
- **Preep** - Anxious and twitchy, keeps lookout from broken tower, terrified of echoes
- **Tuckle** - Broad-faced and quietly competent, tends rooftop moss garden, makes tools from scrap
- **Nish-Nish** - Youngest, nosiest, carries bundle of odd tokens for mystery games

### Rescued Bullywugs
Vardo, Diniferous, Fub, Malaki - Freed from the Aboleth's domination and reunited with their tribe.

### Kriv the Blacktongue
The greatest black dragon in the Drekklands, residing in the eastern Moldrum north of the bay. Found dead when the party arrives (likely slain by Yrk).

### Yrk the Trickster
Nearly 2000-year-old goblin who grants the party his blessing through ritual combat death in a calm shadow space. Great-great-great-grandfather to Eigitch. Agrees to contact Milicent for an audience.

### Eigitch the Old
Returns as guide and submarine captain. Reunites with Yrk with dry, understated affection.

## Key Revelations

### Multiple Worlds Have Existed
The Aboleth's question about "how many moments" this world has existed hints that the current Material Plane is not the first. Combined with knowledge from the Testament of Embers (Estara annihilated the Material Plane 54 times), this suggests repeated cycles of destruction and recreation.

### Twin Dragons and Multiverse
The two identical young black dragons in the Sunken Fortress—the same dragon in two bodies—provide concrete evidence of the multiverse. This reinforces the concept underlying Cetyri's potential as a Unary Great Wyrm.

### The Trickster's Blessing
The blessing severs recipients' spiritual ties to their birth world through ritual death in a calm shadow space. This makes them:
- Invisible to Milicent's tracking
- Unable to be followed on the Material Plane
- Able to travel between worlds more easily (as mentioned in earlier lore)

### Estaran Architecture
The Sunken Fortress demonstrates the Estara's ability to create fragmented, impossible spaces that violate normal physical laws—portals, size distortions, spatial folding.

### The Drekklands' Nature
The swamp exhibits magical instability including: shifting channels, temporal slippage, illusion-rooted flora, mood-inducing weather, and folded terrain that loops back on itself with subtle differences.

## Environmental Dangers

The Drekklands present unique hazards requiring navigation checks (Survival DC 15 or Arcana DC 17):
- **Shifting Channels** - Rivers and trails alter course overnight
- **Temporal Slippage** - Time misbehaves; events repeat, echoes arrive before sounds
- **Illusion-Rooted Flora** - Plants trigger localized illusions masking hazards
- **Mood-Inducing Weather** - Swamp reacts to emotional states
- **Folded Terrain** - Space loops in subtle ways with small details wrong

## Chapter Outcomes

The party successfully receives the Trickster's Blessing, becoming invisible to Milicent's tracking. Yrk agrees to arrange a meeting with Milicent. The Gulpfen Brood is reunited and safe. The party has witnessed evidence of multiple previous worlds and the multiverse's reality. They return to Naeliste with newfound freedom of movement and Milicent's audience arranged.

The stage is set for direct confrontation or negotiation with the Shadowdame.
