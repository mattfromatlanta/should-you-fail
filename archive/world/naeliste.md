# Naeliste

## Location

Naeliste lies in the shadow of the Teeth, a towering cliff face that marks the northern border of the Faedwood. At the base of these sheer stone walls, the West Falls thunder down in veils of mist, feeding a wide basin of marshland, river delta, and shallow lake.

## Geography

The sound of the falls is constant—distant and roaring in some places, quiet and musical in others—woven into every part of Naeliste's life. The city is built around this dramatic geography, with its three peoples adapting to the land in strikingly different ways.

## The Three Peoples

### Eladrin
Native to the Feywild and shaped by its changing seasons. They have raised slender towers that stretch hundreds of meters into the air, shimmering with color, texture, and form that shift subtly with the moods and seasonal aspects of those who live within them. Some lean like trees in wind; others reflect the sky or shine like hoarfrost.

### Bullywugs
Live at the water's edge in low, round houses with thatched domes and open courtyards, arranged in wide, irregular clusters like lily pads on a pond. They move easily between land and water, and their songs rise with the mist in the morning.

### Goblins
Have carved their homes directly into the stone of the Teeth. Their tunnels wind behind the waterfalls and through the cliffs, forming warm-lit chambers where music echoes off wet stone. One of the largest spaces, hidden behind the heart of the falls, serves as a common hall, meeting place, and stage.

## Notable Locations

### The Four Towers
- **The Shifting Sky** - Highest chamber never faces the same way twice, reflects unfamiliar stars
- **The Long Step** - Time bends subtly; visitors uncertain if they truly arrived
- **The Falling Mist** - Ever-wet courtyards, upper galleries vanish into morning cloud
- **The Umbral Bound** - Shadows and reflections lag behind their owners

Each tower is stewarded by an awakened animal: Numat (kite), Voel (tortoise), Ruun (river otter), and Saith (moth).

### Goblinville
A noisy, ever-expanding network of goblin tunnels, kitchens, and workshops carved into the cliffs behind the falls.

### The Foot of the Falls
The water-bound Bullywug neighborhood of floating gardens and round homes that rise and fall with the wetlands.

### The Library of Rhymes and Antiquities
The city's primary archive of oral, written, and enchanted records, curated by Queen Librarian Melbara. Contains rare documents including the Testament of Embers, Grib's letter from the First War, and the Ode to Seven Lords.

### The Circle
Naeliste's permanent teleportation nexus, used for travel and diplomacy, maintained by Lomani Nae and tightly regulated.

### The Echo Root
A hidden crystalline chamber where memories can be exchanged for knowledge drawn from the Eladrin collective mind.

### The Goblinworks
A dense, chaotic warren of goblin-run workshops and forges carved into the cliffside behind the falls. Where Eigitch's submarine was built.

## Organizations

### The Makers' Alliance
A citywide guild of inventors and artisans that oversees shared workshops and magical infrastructure projects.

### The Spellscribes
A professional guild of enchanters and scribes who craft magical scrolls and items for those who can afford them.

### The Oni Guard
A rare sect of Oni bound by oath to protect Naeliste and enforce ancient, sacred boundaries throughout the city. Led by Vathru One-Eye.

## Defenses and Entry

Five Oni guard the approach to Naeliste. They demand travelers "Say the words" or turn back: "The joining pact remains and Naeliste is closed to those who sunder."

## Special Properties

Milicent cannot track visitors while they remain in Naeliste. It is one of the few places on the Material Plane safe from her gaze.

## Cultural Events

### The Festival of the Last Frost
Bullywug-led celebration marking the end of cold season. Features floating lanterns, swampball races, and public storytelling contests.

### Makers' Showcase
Seasonal exhibition of tools, toys, and magical devices in the Highmarket.

### Moon Chorus Rehearsals
Seasonal choir performance shaping fog and wind on the balconies of the Falling Mist tower.

### Goblin Storytime
Each evening after the community meal, Goblins assemble to tell stories and try to outdo each other. Eigitch the Old usually attends.

## Goblin Longevity

Though goblins are technically immortal, few live past 150 years—each decade makes them bolder, more eager to try the impossible. Their stories, stunts, and inventions are as much a part of the city's rhythm as the water that never stops falling.
