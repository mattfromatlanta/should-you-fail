# Letter to Melbara from Caera

*written weeks before Thorn's disappearance, at the end of the Rebellion*

---

To Melbara, in Naeliste—if this reaches you.

We've pushed them back again. The Estara are giving ground. No one knows why. They're not fighting like they used to. Thorn thinks something's changed. I don't care what it is—we'll take it.

We're camped near a burned-out village that was probably home to halflings. Nothing left here, really. Thorn found a wooden sword for a child and didn't say anything for a long time. He misses Aelira. We both do. I dream of her most nights. I wonder if she's picked a season yet.

Tell her we're safe. Tell her her mother and father are trying to make a world she can walk through without fear.

I hope she still listens when you tell stories.

We'll come home as soon as we can.

—Caera

---

*The letter speaks of her time in battle alongside Thorn, their weariness, and how the tide of war seemed to be shifting.*

*Caera expresses longing for Aelira and urges Melbara to care for her in her absence.*

*Melbara shares it now as an expression of trust and quiet remorse, acknowledging she's judged Aelira harshly and hopes to repair that distance.*
