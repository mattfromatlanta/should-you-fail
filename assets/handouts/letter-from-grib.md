# Letter from the goblin Grib to the goblin Yip

*Believed to be dated to the first years of the Age of Estara*

---

Dear Yip,

Still breathing. Still ugly. Still got all my fingers, though the pinky on the left's a little hesitant now. Might be nerves. Might be the charm I stole off a dryad's grave—hard to say.

We thought we'd hold the ridge at Varn's Curve. We had the Faelight archers with us, even one of those Elk Lords with the antlers like iron. All of them scattered like leaves in wind. The Estara didn't even sweat. They don't fight, Yip. They advance. Like fog that forgets to lift.

We lost Captain Murt. He laughed right up until he didn't. Said if he was going to die, he hoped it'd be in the middle of a boast. I think that's how he'd want to go. Still. Hurts.

They're saying now we'll fall back to the Faedwood and try to bottleneck the bastards. The roots run deep there, and the trees remember worse. But I've seen how the Estara burn memory itself. Don't know if the wood will hold. Don't know if we will.

Anyway, I traded boots with a satyr for half a map and three clean bandages. Still not sure who got the better end. He played a tune after and said it was for the ones we forgot. I didn't cry, but I chewed my lip until it bled. That's different.

Tell Ma I still wear her charm-stone. Tell Glit I'll teach her that knife trick if I come back.

If not—burn this letter. No one needs to know I was scared.

—Grib

P.S. If you ever see Yrk again, kick him once for calling me short. Twice if he's drunk. But we both know you won't see him.

---

*The Fey fought the Estara from the moment they arrived, but that period is somewhat lost to time. There aren't even many legends. Something about this letter stands out but she's not sure what. At the very least it's a window into a time she knows little about.*
