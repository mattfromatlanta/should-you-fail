# The Testament of Embers

*Recovered from an Estaran ruin after the Rebellion*
*indeterminate age, only known copy*

---

## Book VII: On the Final Wyrm

And lo, in the ninth silence of the ninth age,
when the Vaults of Heaven rang hollow, and the Line of Bright Kings had ended,
he came.

Not born, nor risen, nor summoned—
but beheld,
a terror entire, a flame made full,
crowned in all fire that had ever burned
and scaled with the memory of a thousand deaths.

His breath was the undoing of walls.
His wings were the shadows of endings.
No horn heralded him. No omen warned.
He was the Summation of Fire. He was Varkhasia.

The first realm to fall was Velimor,
where the hills ran green and mortals sang with iron on their tongues.
There stood Thale the Ever-Post,
Halfling of the Eastern Watch, who bore the Oath that Does Not Fade.
He stood upon the rampart until stone became ash and ash became sky.

His name was spoken seven times by the Estara before it was returned to silence.

The second was Karethorn,
stone-rooted citadel of the orc-lords in the Vale of Frost.
There fell Inuvien, First of the Flame-Wardens,
who could unmake fire with her breath and bind beasts with her songs.

The Wyrm struck down her tower with a single coil,
and the rivers boiled for thirty days.

Then were the Thirteen Banners lowered,
and the names of the slain carried skyward on the black wind.
The fields were salted with bone. The sun grew small.
In every city, songs turned to mourning.

And we—the Estara—noble in judgment,
pure in vision,
unyielding in the trust of the world's shaping—
we turned to that which must not be named.
Our Last Resort. Our final act.

The world was rent. Varkhasia fell.

Let no voice question what was done.
Let no hand reach backward.
Let peace endure beneath our governance,
until the stars are cold
and Time forgets its name.

---

*Appears to be written by the Estara themselves. Few if any other documents written by the Estara are known to exist. They would burn their estates before anything fell into rebel hands. It stands out to Aelira because it mentions dragons, but also because none of the places mentioned are known to anyone in this world.*

*Note: The Estara's Last Resort was the annihilation of the Material Plane, though this part of the story is not explicitly told. The names of the mortal heroes and places in the epic are unknown, as the text predates the current reality.*
