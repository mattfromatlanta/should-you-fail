# The Ode to Seven Lords

*by the Satyr Therinon*
*likely dated to the end of the Age of Rebellion*

---

In hush of bloom, before the fire grew loud,
They came with wind and wing and rooted tread,
No throne they sought, no chain, no crown, no shroud—
But kept the bounds where fear and wonder wed.
The mortal kin they watched, the stars they read.
Each bore a mark, a name the wild had sung,
Their stories old when mortal hearts were young.

Itrina watched from far beyond the frost,
With feathered brow and gaze that tore through lies.
He held the names the waking world had lost,
And spoke in dreams to hearts too tired for cries.
In every hush, his memory still flies.
He sees what was, what is, what should not be—
And waits for dusk beneath the listening tree.

Through stone and storm came Carris, crowned and still,
His antlers dark with moss and thunder's stain.
He held the lawless world within his will,
And kept the borders no one else could name.
No oath he swore that ever broke or waned.
When forest weeps, his hoofbeats still are heard—
The silent answer, heavier than word.

A riddle's end, a feather's flick, a grin—
So came bold Xilo, joy beneath the thorn.
He spoke in verse and taught the lost to win,
His riddles bright, his laughter sharply born.
To those who faltered, he brought light, not scorn—
And where he passed, the shadows thinned and died,
Though sorrow nests where robins used to fly.

Now speak, soft wind, and tell of Ozevriel—
The shield of flame, the calm before the wrath.
He stood alone upon the smoke-rung dell
And turned the Estarí from their chosen path.
His antlers broke; he wept, but did not ask.
The other six escaped the fire he caught—
And every age since then has held the cost.

Thessa walks slow, with petals red as sin,
Where broken hearts grow gardens in the dusk.
She binds the bloom to all that bleeds within,
And lifts the shattered mask from grief's cold husk.
To her, the dead are sweet, the proud are hushed.
She does not judge the ruin or the rain—
But crowns the pain that dared to rise again.

Two horns he wears, both curving far and wide,
One points to fate, the other back to shame.
Varuun, who guards the places worlds divide,
Who speaks but rarely, though he knows all names.
He holds the choice—the turn, the thread, the flame.
Where roads are lost, and songs are made of dust,
His silence walks beside you in the dusk.

Then Milicent, moon-eyed, rose last of all,
A voice of stars, of beauty bent to fire.
She led them forth when shadows learned to crawl—
When hope and wrath walked close upon the mire.
But power broke what sorrow first inspired.
She turned from light, and gave her name away,
And none now speak the price she chose to pay.

Now five remain, their names like wind in stone,
Still bound by oaths no newer bonds can break.
They meet in dream, or silent wood, alone—
For time has thinned the paths they used to take.
No war will make their hollow longing wake.
The ones they lost, they carry in their tread—
Too proud for tears, too old to count the dead.

---

*This is by far the most explicit mention of the fey lords that Erol calls the Rebel Fey, and the only document to mention Milicent in the entire collection. Therinon, the author, is known to Melbara (though he has passed years ago). He was a famous loremaster of the Feywild but never crossed over to her knowledge. His writing is hard to come by on the Material Plane.*
