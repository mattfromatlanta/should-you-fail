# The Veterans of Aerus: Conscription, Trauma, and the Rebellion

## The Estaran Philosophy of Civilization

The Estarans see themselves as stewards. They regard mortals as limited, lesser beings — children cursed to chaotic, short, and violent lives — and believe it is their duty to impose order on that chaos through civilization. They build cities, spread language, establish law. Every world they touch is, in their view, improved by their presence.

The tragedy of the Estaran Perspective, as they call it, is the love of law overrides any reflexive accounting of their own history. In truth, there is no greater source of oppression and violence in the Cosmos than the Estaran Empire. The Estarans are capable of tremendous cruelty not because they enjoy it but because their ordered, rule-bound nature narrows moral consideration until atrocities feel like administration. When they rebuild their Empire in a reborn Cosmos, they do it the same way they always have: by civilizing mortals across worlds, world by world, generation by generation. 

When they find again that their Empire is failing, they have a final law they always follow: Burn it to cinders. Start again.

The Fey are the structural enemy of this project. Fey existence is fundamentally incompatible with Estaran magic and ideology. Every world with an Estaran footprint sees conflict with the Fey — not as a contingent political accident but as an expression of what both are. The Estarans know this. They have fought this war fifty-four times.

## The Conscription of Veterans

The Estarans see danger in bonds of mortals and Fey. They corrupt order, and pull the great project of civilization into chaos. But when mortals align with Fey, they will not raise a sword against them.

So the Estarans do not ask them to. Instead, they conscript the strongest and fiercest mortal fighters and deploye them across the Cosmos — to fight the Fey they do not know, in places they cannot name, for reasons they are never given. To prevent the knowledge of other worlds from seeping back and threatening the safety of isolation, the conscripts' memories are crushed. Veterans return home knowing they were gone. They do not know where, or for how long, or what they did.

On occasion, the Estarans conscript powerful Fey with close allegiance to the mortal world — turning them against their own kind in their everlasting campaign. These Fey are led to servitude in many cases by the love they feel for a mortal soul. Erol the Faed was one such case, drawn in through her deep friendship with Aldrin Stoneward. Though she doesn't blame him, she cannot forget.

The psychic mechanisms used to suppress veterans' memories are an ancient Estaran tactic, refined across many iterations of the cosmos. They are a deliberate system of control, designed to mitigate the danger of rebellion by ensuring that veterans can never piece together their history. The traps are passive until probed, at which point they activate to prevent recovery. Veterans who attempt to explore their own past — through meditation, magic, or psychic introspection — encounter them without warning. Erol encountered this when she stood against the illusory Estarans in Aris, and Maelric sprung his own trap when he used his magic to look inward.

## Aerus: The Second Fey War and Its Conscripts

On Aerus, Estaran conscription of mortals and allied Fey began shortly before the Second Fey War, as conflicts on other worlds were becoming severe and the Estarans needed experienced fighters that would follow orders without complications.

The fighters conscripted on Aeus included later become the founders of the Veterans' Rebellion and the Allied Cities. Maelric Sturmuld, Aldrin Stoneward, and Erol the Faed were at the heart of this group.

The conflict on Ikellor is a parallel history, older than Aerus's Second Fey War. Ikellor is as old as Aerus, and the Estaran-Fey conflict there had been running for far longer — centuries, at minimum. The destruction of Ikellor's Feywild was not the beginning of that war but its terrible end: a deliberate strategic act by the Estarans to break Fey resistance permanently on that world. The result was an ice age that has been slowly killing the world ever since. Ikellor is very nearly dead when the party arrives. What the Aerus veterans were deployed to fight, and whether any of them served on Ikellor, is not recorded.

## What the Veterans Carry

The veterans of Aerus returned home damaged in ways they could not articulate and cannot explain. What they carry includes:

**A deep, persistent, sourceless fear.** They have seen things — committed things — they cannot recall. The fear remains even when the memory is gone. It surfaces without warning, disproportionate to present circumstances, resistant to reason.

**The scars of Fey psychic magic.** Many veterans were direct targets of Fey psychic attacks during their service. Fey psychic magic leaves particular kinds of wounds — not the blunt trauma of a suppressed memory but something more like a presence, a residue that lingers in the mind and does not heal cleanly.

**Unnatural longevity.** Estaran service altered the veterans physically. The Estarans frame this as a gift — the reward of a life well-spent in service to civilization. Mortals come to experience it as a curse. It is not a prize deliberately bestowed but the residue of what Estaran magic does to mortal bodies over extended exposure and service. Veterans do not naturally age out. On Aerus today, there are hundreds of them still alive in Aris — still present, still politically active, more than 150 years after the Rebellion ended — simply because they do not die. This is the engine of stagnation. The Young Bastards are not wrong that the Veterans have dominated political life for a century and a half. The Veterans are not wrong that they remember what was fought for. Neither can resolve this without something changing that isn't in anyone's power to change.

**An inability to grieve what they lost.** They know, in some sense, that time passed. They know they are different. They do not know what they lost, and they cannot mourn it cleanly. This is its own kind of wound.

## The Veteran Rebellion

The Veteran Rebellion on Aerus was fought by people who did not understood what had been done to them. It began intuitively. It was the breaking point for the Estaran conscripts, whose lives were spent in foreign wars they did not remember. Finally, the mortals of Aerus recognized evil at the core of Estaran benevolence.

The Veteran Rebellion coincided with Milicent's Shadow War — fought in secret by wraiths and death knights across Aerus over decades. This was the other half of the victory. The Shadow War was not a war of great battles. It was a persistent campaign to collapse Estaran power at key structural points. This way, the Estarans were made vulnerable enough that the Fey and the Veterans could finally succeed. 

The mortals of Aerus know the Veteran Rebellion succeeded. Only a few are aware of two Fey wars on their world. The role of Milicent's Dread Legion is a dark secret. Some great Fey -- like Xilo -- know the full history, including Milicent's gambit. The party, having spoken directly with Xilo and Milicent, now understands more than any living mortal.

## The Trauma That Persists

The Rebellion ended. The Estarans were driven from Aerus. The veterans did not heal.

The unresolved fear, the sourceless grief, their lingering life — all of it persisted and hardened. By the arithmetic of numbers and time, Veterans came to dominate politicis in Aris and other Allied Cities. They are a class of ageless, damaged souls, forever paranoid that their tormentors could return. They grip the available levers of safety, and the generations that follow grow up in their shadow.

Maelric Sturmwald came to understand the unbalance over decades. When he entered his trance, he did so deliberately, seeking a cure for the veterans' condition. Gallifax kept vigil over him, understanding why he had gone in. But he was caught in the psychic trap left for him by the Estarans, and could not return from his astral journey under his own power. It was only with the party's help that he could return.

After Erol faltered with many other Veterans at the Aris waterfront, she guessed that this was some control left behind by the Estarans, who would never allow her to raise arms against them. She was correct, and Maelric's long imprisonment reinforces it.

## Notes on Party Knowledge

The following summarizes what the party currently knows and doesn't know, as a guide for play:

**Known to the party:** The broad history of the Veterans' Rebellion. That veterans carry psychic damage of some kind. That the Estarans conscripted fighters from Aerus. That Maelric went into his trance seeking a cure. The Erol/Babas theory about deliberate Estaran tampering. The general shape of Milicent's Shadow War (that it occurred, that it was a secret campaign, that it helped the Rebellion win).

**Not known to the party:** The full details of Erol's personal timeline — her bond with Aldrin as the mechanism of her conscription, the strain this created, her path from Milicent to Xilo to the Teeth. The scope of Ikellor's history. The precise nature of the psychic traps or how they might be removed. Whether Maelric found anything useful before being trapped in his trance.

**In tension / unresolved:** Whether the veterans' longevity can be undone, and whether any of them would choose that if it could. What Maelric actually encountered. Whether the Estaran tampering can be specifically identified and targeted as a future story thread.
