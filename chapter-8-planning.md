# Chapter 8: Across The Void — Planning Document

## Title & Themes

**Title:** Across The Void

**Core themes:**
- The party steps off their world for the first time — truly alien territory
- Mirror characters: this world has its own Torma, Gullux, Bronik, and Cetyri — different fates, same blood
- A people abandoned by their gods asking if the party is their salvation
- Cetyri confronting another version of himself — the path not yet taken
- Heroism without recognition (Xilo's thesis, E01)

---

## Chapter Arc

1. **A Word with Xilo** — On the road to Metgal, a farewell and a philosophy.
2. **The Radiant Lance** — The heart of Metgal. Cetyri reads the paths. The party crosses over.
3. **Shock of Cold** — Arrival on a glacier. A blizzard. Shelter. Cave lions. A Frost Giant scout.
4. **The Settlement** — A village under siege. The people assume the Estarans sent champions. They marvel at the Fey.
5. **The Giant Tribe** — A series of battles to break Torma's grip. Gullux and Bronik as lieutenants.
6. **Torma's Realm** — Into the dead Feywild. Free the hatchling. The merge.

## Established Details

### The Radiant Lance

Perpendicular shafts of Estaran star metal mounted on a folding tripod. The lance attaches via an intricate set of adjustable gears controlling elevation, attitude, and cardinal direction. When idle or unaligned, a faint internal glow runs along its length — like dust caught in sunlight. As it comes into alignment with a path, a beam comes into focus pointing toward the target world.

When not in use, it folds magically into a nondescript metal cylinder approximately 2 feet long and 3 inches in diameter.

Felicity demonstrates its use and alignment. Cetyri is the one who actually aims it — he meditates in silence, turns his consciousness inward, and lets instinct guide the alignment. He will want space and quiet to do so.

### Transworld Communication

Magic cannot reach between worlds. Even spells with transdimensional capability reach only the local iterations of those dimensions. The party is completely cut off from home — no contact with Gallifax, Maelric, or anyone on their world.

The Estarans maintain a transworld communication network, but it is closely controlled and inaccessible to the party.

### World Naming

The party's home world is referred to as "Home" or "your world" in DM prose unless the party names it. The Estaran administrative code for it is 03b-11.

### Merging Rules

If the party's Cetyri is the stronger of the two dragons, the merge is safe. After a merger, Cetyri grows in power. Danger arises when the stronger dragon is deeply attached to its world — that is not the case here. The hatchling has known nothing but Torma's lair.

After the merge, Cetyri grows in power. As this continues across worlds, avoid scenarios where Cetyri solves everything — the party must remain the protagonists.

### Cetyri's Senses on the Ice World

Cetyri can give the party a general sense of direction toward the hatchling, but his senses are confused. The reason: the ice world's Cetyri is not on the mortal plane when the party arrives — he is already in the dead Feywild, imprisoned in Torma's lair. Cetyri knows something is nearby but cannot read it clearly. This resolves when they cross into the dead Feywild.

## E01: A Word with Xilo

**Type:** Roleplay / farewell set piece
**When:** On the road to Metgal, the evening before arrival.

### Setup

Xilo pauses the group for the night. He has something to say before they reach Metgal and the moment of departure. He does not expect to see any of them again.

### Xilo's Words

> *"None of us except the dragon have any idea where you'll go. And he doesn't know what waits for you. I expect we'll never meet again.*
>
> *But hear this: heroism requires no recognition. You can save the world whether you'll be known for it or not.*
>
> *We don't take wing for the first time expecting to soar. We leave the nest hoping for a soft landing."*

### Notes

- Give players time to respond. This is a genuine farewell from a character they fought for.
- Xilo is not being grim — he is being honest in his whimsical way. The soft landing line is his version of encouragement.
- This is also the last night with Yrk, Gallifax, Maelric, and Aldrin on the road. If players want other goodbye scenes, give them room.

## E02: The Radiant Lance

**Type:** DM set piece / transition
**When:** Heart of Metgal. The crossing.

### Setup

The troop has cleared a path to the heart of Metgal. Only Felicity and Cetyri will cross with the party — no Feywild Fey can safely enter Metgal; Xilo and Yrk stopped at the border. This is the final goodbye for Gallifax and Maelric as well.

**Note on Metgal:** Chapter 8 was originally conceived as a dungeon crawl through Metgal to reach the center. The strength of the Chapter 7 allies makes that unnecessary — they clear the path. The session begins here, at the activation. The ruins are described; they are not played through.

### DM Prose — The Heart of Metgal *(first draft)*

> The center of Metgal is a wound that has never closed. A crater a quarter mile across marks where the Estaran stronghold once stood — the tallest towers, the deepest vaults, the greatest concentration of their power on this world. What remains is scorched stone that melted and re-hardened into shapes no mason intended, and air that is wrong in a way you feel before you can name it. Too still. Too dry. Charged.
>
> The ruins extend in every direction beyond the crater's rim. Metgal was a great city once. It no longer resembles one. Buildings fused together into masses. Streets that end in sheer drops. Passages that lead somewhere else. The demons that the Estarans left behind — or that grew in the Estarans' absence — have drawn back from the path your allies cleared, but they have not left. You can hear them in the dark corridors beyond the light.
>
> Gallifax circles overhead, broad and slow. Maelric stands at the ridge with Aldrin, looking down into the crater. Neither speaks. They gave you this — the silence, the cleared path, the moment. Whatever happens next belongs to you.
>
> At the center of the crater, the Radiant Lance stands ready. Felicity stands beside it, waiting. Cetyri crouches nearby, eyes closed, head low. His breath mists in the dead air. He is reading the paths.

### Notes

- Felicity demonstrates the Lance's alignment first. Then Cetyri takes over — he will ask for quiet. Give the meditation moment room.
- Cetyri prefers the path that is shortest, strongest, and cleanest. The party can debate or defer. Either is valid, but he will have an opinion.
- None of the allies are coming through. This is the goodbye.

### DM Prose — The Crossing *(first draft)*

> The Radiant Lance hums at a frequency you feel in your jaw and behind your eyes. The light it produces is the same radiance you've seen before — Estaran light — but here it expands outward until there is nothing else. No crater. No ruins. No sky.
>
> You are nowhere, briefly. The sensation is not painful, but it is deeply strange — as if the self that you are is held in suspension while something vast and indifferent considers whether to accept it. Cetyri's voice reaches you, low and steady, though you cannot see him. You have the impression of a road, but the road is not made of anything.
>
> Then: cold.

## E03: Shock of Cold

**Type:** Survival / combat
**When:** Immediate arrival.

### Setup

The party arrives on a glacier in the middle of a blizzard. Visibility is near zero. The cold is immediately dangerous. They must find shelter before they can do anything else.

When the storm breaks, they are attacked by cave lions — large, hungry predators that have learned the glacier is a place where disoriented prey appears. In the middle of or shortly after this fight, a Frost Giant scout spots them from a ridge.

### DM Prose — Arrival *(first draft)*

> Cold arrives all at once, from every direction, so immediate and total that the first breath you draw feels like swallowing ice. Your feet find ground — dark stone beneath a skin of wind-packed snow. The light is gone.
>
> The blizzard hits you before you can orient yourselves. Wind screams across the glacier. You can see perhaps ten feet in any direction, and what you see is white.
>
> The sky, in the gaps between gusts, is wrong. You cannot immediately say how. The color is right — grey, overcast — but something that should be there isn't. The world feels thinner than it should. Quieter in a way that has nothing to do with sound.

### Environmental Hazards (cave lion fight)

The cave lions do not suffer these conditions. The party does.

- **Weak ice:** Sections of the glacier are unstable. Failed Perception or movement on marked sections risks breaking through — DC 13 Dexterity save or fall prone and take cold damage.
- **Wind:** Strong gusts impose disadvantage on ranged attacks. DC 12 Strength save when moving more than half speed or be knocked prone.
- **Visibility:** Even after the blizzard, wind and snow reduce visibility. Creatures beyond 30 feet have half cover.

### Notes

- The wrongness of the sky is felt, not named. Do not explain it in the read-aloud. It is an absence — absences are hard to see.
- **DM note — the dead Feywild:** The Estarans killed this world's Feywild deliberately. Players will not know this on arrival. Their first thread is in E04, when the villagers react to Fey party members.
- The Frost Giant scout does not engage. He watches and withdraws. His report reaches Torma before the party reaches the village.

## E04: The Settlement

**Type:** Roleplay / investigation
**When:** After the party gets their bearings and descends toward the valley.

### Setup

A settlement of perhaps 150–200 people in a valley adjacent to a frozen river. Once larger — the cold and the giants have reduced them. They survive by hunting large game (mammoths, wooly rhinoceroses, giant elk), but their hunting parties have been repeatedly ambushed by Frost Giants, who take the meat and leave the dead.

When the party descends into the valley, the village responds with cautious, exhausted hope. Their first question: *"Did the Estarans send you?"* They do not know whether to be hopeful or terrified by the answer.

### The Fey Reaction

If any Fey party members are visible, the village reaction shifts. To these people, Fey are things out of legend — creatures from a world that no longer exists. They will marvel, touch carefully if allowed, ask questions in hushed voices. This is the opening: *why* are Fey legend here? The party will have to ask the right questions to get to the answer.

The village elders can tell the history of the Feywild and the ice age, though much of it is myth by now. The core truth is buried in the legend: something happened to the Fey long ago, the sky changed, and the cold came and never left.

### Language

Locals speak the common tongue — strange accents, but understandable. The Estarans spread it throughout the cosmos. Fey languages are completely undecipherable here; the Estarans and the Fey never got along, so Fey languages never traveled.

### Abandoned Estaran Presence

There are abandoned Estaran fortifications and artifacts in and around the valley. The village has a complicated relationship with them — some are useful, some are dangerous, some have become sacred. Worth exploring.

### Notes

- The Frost Giant scout's report means Torma knows the party is here. The giants may probe the village before the party is ready.
- Give players time to learn the situation before the fighting starts. The village's desperation should land.

**To establish in Step 3:** Village name; 2–3 key local characters; what the elders know and how they tell it.

## E05: The Giant Tribe

**Type:** Combat series
**When:** The party moves against Torma's forces.

### Setup

To reach Torma, the party must break the giant tribe's hold on the valley. This is a small campaign within the chapter — not a single fight but a series of engagements that depletes and dismantles the raiding force.

Gullux and Bronik lead the raids as young white dragons, each enhanced to CR12. They are Torma's lieutenants and her most dangerous mobile assets. They are also mirrors of dragons the party has already encountered on their home world — but younger, and loyal to a mother who is very much alive.

### Notes

- Defeating the tribe — or driving it back decisively — is what opens the path to the dead Feywild. Torma will not receive the party in her realm while her forces are intact; the party must remove her protection.
- Cetyri can give the party a general sense of the hatchling's direction, but his read is confused — the hatchling is not on the mortal plane. He knows something is close. He cannot place it clearly until they cross over.
- Gullux and Bronik should feel like a genuine threat. They have been raiding this valley for months and know the terrain.

**To establish in Step 3:** The sequence of engagements; how Gullux and Bronik are introduced; what breaking the tribe looks like.

## E06: Torma's Realm

**Type:** Dungeon / boss encounter / climax
**When:** The party crosses into the dead Feywild.

### Setup

The dead Feywild can still be reached — it exists as a hollow ruin of a plane, collapsed but not entirely gone. Torma's lair is here. With the mortal tribe broken, the crossing becomes accessible.

The dungeon is Torma's domain in a dead world. What was once Feywild is now cold, grey, still — beautiful in a ruined way, like frost on a corpse. The hatchling is somewhere inside, imprisoned.

### The Hatchling

The merge is safe — the party's Cetyri is the stronger dragon, and the hatchling has no deep attachment to this world. Once free, the merge can proceed. What it looks and feels like from the outside is TBD — it should be significant and strange.

After the merge, Cetyri grows in power.

### Notes

- Torma is the boss of this encounter. She has chosen to live here, in the ruin of what the Estarans destroyed, which says something about her.
- The hatchling's imprisonment is Torma's leverage and prize. She may use it in the confrontation.
- The dead Feywild as a setting should feel unlike anywhere the party has been — even the Shadowfell had life in it. This is a place where something was killed and the wound has gone cold.

**To establish in Step 3:** Torma's stat block and tactics; the dungeon layout; how the hatchling is found; what the merge looks and feels like.

## Later Arc Notes *(not Chapter 8)*

- Eventually the Estarans will figure out what the party is doing and raise an interplanetary alarm.
- The party will encounter a Cetyri on some world who fights to keep them away.
- The Feywild's death at Estaran hands is a recurring theme — suppression, exploitation, or outright destruction on every world. The party will begin to recognize the pattern.
- **DM note — Estaran internal politics:** Some Estarans believe a reset is inevitable. Others believe the pattern of mortal failure is breakable with the right application of Estaran order. None recognize that their impulse to control is itself the engine of instability. The party will not learn this all at once — every world they visit is evidence.
